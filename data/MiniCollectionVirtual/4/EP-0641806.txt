<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a novel process for the production of mouldings, in particular contact lenses, in which a soluble prepolymer containing crosslinkable groups is crosslinked in solution, and to mouldings, in particular contact lenses, obtainable by this process. The present invention likewise relates to novel prepolymers which can be employed in the novel process, in particular derivatives of a polyvinyl alcohol having a molecular weight of at least about 2000 which contains from about 0.5 to about 80%, based on the number of hydroxyl groups in the polyvinyl alcohol, of units of the formula I,

<CLAIMS>
A process for the manufacture of a moulding, which comprises the following steps:

a) the preparation of a substantially aqueous solution of a water-soluble prepolymer that
comprises crosslinkable groups,
b) the introduction of the solution obtained into a mould,
c) the triggering of the crosslinking,
d) the opening of the mould such that the moulding can be removed from the mould.
A process according to claim 1, wherein the moulding is a contact lens.
A process according to claim 1, wherein the substantially aqueous solution of the
water-soluble prepolymer comprising crosslinkable groups is free or substantially free

from undesired constituents, such as especially monomeric, oligomeric or polymeric
starting compounds used for the preparation of the prepolymer, or secondary products that

have formed during the preparation of the prepolymer.
A process according to claim 1, wherein the substantially aqueous solution of the
water-soluble prepolymer comprising crosslinkable groups is used without the addition of

a comonomer, especially a vinylic comonomer.
A process according to claim 1, wherein an initiator for the crosslinking is added to the
solution of the prepolymer.
A process according to claim 1, wherein an extraction for the removal of undesired
constituents following the crosslinking is dispensed with.
A process according to claim 1, which comprises the following steps:

a) the preparation of a substantially aqueous solution of a water-soluble prepolymer that
comprises crosslinkable groups, which is free or substantially free from undesired

constituents, such as especially monomeric, oligomeric or polymeric starting compounds 
used for the preparation of the prepolymer, or secondary products that have formed during

the preparation of the prepolymer, and is used without the addition of a comonomer,
b) the introduction of the solution obtained into a mould,
c) the triggering of the crosslinking,
d) the opening of the mould such that the moulding can be removed from the mould.
A process according to claim 7, wherein the moulding is a contact lens.
A process for the manufacture of a contact lens according to claim 8, wherein the
substantially aqueous solution is a purely aqueous solution or a solution in an artificial

lacrimal fluid, which is preferably buffered.
A process for the manufacture of a contact lens according to claim 8, wherein a
crosslinking initiator is added to the solution and wherein the crosslinking is

photocrosslinking.
A process according to claim 3, wherein an extraction for the removal of undesired
constituents following the crosslinking is dispensed with.
A process according to claim 11, wherein the solution of the prepolymer is used
without the addition of a comonomer, especially a vinylic comonomer.
A process according to claim 7, wherein an extraction for the removal of undesired
constituents following the crosslinking is dispensed with.
