FLOOR CLEANING APPLIANCE

<ABSTRACT>
A floor cleaning appliance or the like has a handle attached by a cardan or half-cardan joint to the actual cleaning, scrubbing, wiping or other arrangement. A liftable hoop (10) articulated to the cleaning device (R) engages a projection (St), a screw or a pin on the handle or snaps into a horizontal groove of the handle.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Floor-cleaning appliance with a cardanic or semi-cardanic 
fastening of the handle to the actual cleaning, scrubbing, wiping 

or other device having a setting-up stirrup (10) articulated 
on the cleaning device (R) and intended for engaging behind 

a projection (St), a screw or a peg on the handle or for catching 
in a horizontal groove (N) of this handle and with a tread 

(18; 18') for putting up of the setting up stirrup. 
Floor-cleaning appliance according to one of the preceding 
claims characterized in that the setting-up stirrup (10) for 

receiving the handle comprises a stirrup-means or a half-lug 
(14). 
Floor-cleaning appliance according to one of the preceding 
claims characterized in that the tread (18) carries a plastic 

mass formed on the other side of the joint (20) than the stirrup. 
Floor-cleaning appliance according to one of claims 1 to 2 
characterized in that the tread (18) simply consists of two 

setting-up stirrup ends (22) similar to round hooks. 
Floor-cleaning appliance according to one of the preceding 
claims characterized in that the half-lug part (14) of the setting-up 

stirrup (10) is bent (14; 14') rearwards to form a support. 
Floor-cleaning appliance according to one of the preceding 
claims characterized in that in the longitudinal region (12; 

12') of the setting-up stirrup (10) stirrup wires are designed 
or bent in the case of a support (14') of the bent part on the 

base (R) in such a way that they come along this base (Fig. 3).  
 
Floor-cleaning appliance according to one of the preceding 
claims characterized in that the setting-up stirrup permanently 

is pressed against the baseplate of the cleaning appliance (R). 
Floor-cleaning appliance according to claim 7 characterized 
in that the setting-up stirrup (10) by means of a spring for instance 

consisting of steel will load the tread part (18; 18') 
permamently away from the plate. 
Floor-cleaning appliance according to claim 8 characterized 
in that the spring (30) is fastened to the tread (18; 18'), 

especially injection-molded on the plastic part of the tread. 
Floor-cleaning appliance according to one of claims 1 - 9 
characterized in that the setting-up stirrup (10) is arranged on 

the side opposite to the handle. 
</CLAIMS>
