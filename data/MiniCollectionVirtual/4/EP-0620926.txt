<INVENTION-TITLE>
PLUG FOR A PLUG-IN CONNECTOR FOR OPTICAL FIBRES.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G02B638	G02B638	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>G02B	G02B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G02B6	G02B6	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The invention concerns a plug for fibre-optic plug-in connectors. Prior art connectors suffer from the disadvantage that it is relatively easy for a fitter unintentionally to touch the ground end of the optical fibre (12) inside the pin (1) which carries the optical fibre. The invention calls for a pin-retracting spring (6) to be fitted which, when the plug is not plugged in, draws the pin (1) back into a protected position.
</ABSTRACT>
