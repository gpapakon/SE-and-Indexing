<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the "wash and wear" finishing of fibrous materials containing cellulose.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>D06M1521	D06M15263	D06M1537	D06M15423	D06M15564	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>D06M	D06M	D06M	D06M	D06M	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>D06M15	D06M15	D06M15	D06M15	D06M15	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Cellulosic fibre materials, especially woven and knitted fabrics containing bast fibres, for example linen, are pretreated with a solution or dispersion containing an acrylic polymer or a polyurethane. The materials are dried and then a cellulose crosslinker is applied, dried and cured. The articles obtained are suitable for manufacturing clothing. They have a better effect level than fibre materials where the pretreatment was not carried out.
</ABSTRACT>
