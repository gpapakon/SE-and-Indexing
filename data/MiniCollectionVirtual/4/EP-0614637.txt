Dispenser display rack for sweets
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
The present invention relates to a display rack for confectionery such as sweets. It consists of a casing (1, 3a, 3b, 4, 5) inside which a plurality of dispensing containers (2) are arranged in at least one column. It is characterised in that each container (2) has at its top a lid (16) that can slide towards the front of the display rack where it can be withdrawn, giving access to the inside of said container (2) and in that lock means (7, 6a, 6b) are provided in order in the locked position to prevent said lid (16) of each container (2) from sliding.

<CLAIMS>
Dispenser for confectionery products, such as sweets, consisting of a 
cabinet (1, 3a, 3b, 4, 5) inside which are several dispenser trays (2) arranged in at 

least one column, characterised in that each tray (2) has a cover (16) at the top, which 
can slide towards the front of the dispenser in such a way that it can be retracted, thus 

giving access to the inside of the tray (2), and that locking devices (7, 6a, 6b) are 
provided which lock to prevent the said cover (16) sliding off each tray (2). 
Dispenser as per claim 1, characterised in that the said interlocking devices 
consist of at least one vertical bar (6a, 6b, 7) which, when assembled and locked on 

the dispenser cabinet, positions in front of each column of trays (2) preventing the 
cover (16) from sliding off each tray (2). 
Dispenser as per claim 1 or 2, characterised in that the trays (2) are 
arranged in sets of two adjacent columns, the said interlocking devices consisting of 

a vertical bar (7) which, when assembled and locked on the dispenser cabinet, position 
in front of each column of trays (2) within the plane separating the columns of each 

set. 
Dispenser as per claim 1 or 2, characterised in that the said interlocking 
devices consist of two vertical bars (6 and 6b) which, when assembled on the 

dispenser cabinet, position either side in front of each column of trays (2). 
Dispenser as per claim 1 or 2, characterised in that the trays (2) are 
arranged in sets of two adjacent columns and that the said interlocking devices consist 

on the one hand of a vertical bar (7) which, when assembled and locked on the 
dispenser cabinet, positions in front of each column of trays in the plane separating 

the said columns in each set, and on the other, of two vertical bars (6a and 6b) which, 
when assembled on the dispenser cabinet, position either side in front of each set of 

two columns of trays.
