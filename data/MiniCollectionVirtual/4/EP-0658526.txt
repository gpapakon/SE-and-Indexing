<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for making asbestos-free binding material.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A62D300	A62D333	A62D340	A62D10141	C04B700	C04B724	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A62D	A62D	A62D	A62D	C04B	C04B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A62D3	A62D3	A62D3	A62D101	C04B7	C04B7	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Asbestos cement or the residues formed in its decomposition have hitherto been placed in a landfill. The invention proposes subjecting asbestos cement to a sintering process at from 800 to 1200 DEG C using a sintering aid selected from the group consisting of CaF2 and complex aluminium fluorides, for example in the context of cement production. Asbestos cement thus treated no longer has to be placed in a landfill, but is converted into asbestos-free binding (settable) material.
</ABSTRACT>
