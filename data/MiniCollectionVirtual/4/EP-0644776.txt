<CLAIMS>
The use of gas- or liquid-filled microcapsules for the manufacture of an ultrasound
contrast medium for contrasting by means of colour Doppler sonography regions of the

body in which there is substantially no observable movement of the microcapsules.
The use of gas- or liquid-filled microcapsules according to claim 1, wherein the gas- or
liquid-filled microcapsules are synthesised from polycyanoacrylates or biologically degradable

polyesters of α-, β- or γ-hydroxycarboxylic acids.
The use of gas- or liquid-filled microcapsules according to claim 1, wherein the gas- or
liquid-filled microcapsules are synthesised from biologically degradable polymers synthesised

from polymerisable aldehydes which, if desired, contain additives capable of copolymerisation
and/or crosslinkers, optionally surfactants or surfactant mixtures, coupling agents

and optionally bio- or macro-molecules bonded by way of those coupling agents.
The use of gas- or liquid-filled microcapsules according to claim 1, wherein the gas- or
liquid-filled microcapsules are synthesised from gelatin.
The use of gas- or liquid-filled microcapsules according to claim 1, wherein the gas- or
liquid-filled microcapsules are synthesised from partially denatured proteins.
The use according to claim 5 comprising, as partially denatured protein, denatured
albumin or denatured human serum albumin.
The use of gas- or liquid-filled microcapsules according to claims 1 to 6 for the manufacture
of a contrast medium for contrasting the reticuloendothelial system.
The use of gas- or liquid-filled microcapsules according to claims 1 to 6 for the manufacture
of a contrast medium for contrasting the myocardium. 
The use of gas- or liquid-filled microcapsules according to claims 1 to 6 for the manufacture
of a contrast medium for contrasting the gastrointestinal tract.
The use of gas- or liquid-filled microcapsules according to claims 1 to 7 for the manufacture
of a contrast medium for contrasting the lymphatic system, the liver or the spleen.
</CLAIMS>
