<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to the preparation of plasmids which carry the genes for biosynthesis of L-prolin, one of these genes being mutated in such a way that the allosteric inhibition of the biosynthesis of this amino acid, which usually exists, is no longer present. The invention furthermore relates to the use of these plasmids for developing bacterial strains of the species Escherichia coli which excrete L-prolin into the culture liquid and which are therefore particularly suitable for the preparation of this amino acid by fermentation. The plasmids here can either cause a capability of L-prolin overproduction in the first place, or considerably increase an already existing chromosomally determined capability for overproduction. The invention also relates to the preparation of suitable host cell mutants which, in connection with these plasmids, bring about a particularly high concentration of L-prolin in the culture liquid.
</ABSTRACT>
