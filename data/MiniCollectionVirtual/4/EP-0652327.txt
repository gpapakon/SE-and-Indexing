<INVENTION-TITLE>
Branching element for installing in a downpipe of a roof gutter
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>E03B300	E03B302	E04D1304	E04D1308	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>E03B	E03B	E04D	E04D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>E03B3	E03B3	E04D13	E04D13	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Branching element for installing in the downpipe of a roof gutter, comprising an intermediate member which encloses the downpipe ends and exhibits, on its outer surface, an opening for receiving a tongue for discharging the water into a rainwater collector, the intermediate member (2) having a connection stub (3) which projects into an opening (6b) provided in the wall (6a) of the collector (6), and the tongue (4) being arranged in the intermediate member (2) such that, beneath the tongue (4), there is a spacing (7) from the inner wall of the connection stub (3).

<CLAIMS>
Branch piece (1) for insertion in the downspout (1a) of a roof drain,
including an intermediate member locking the ends of the

downspout, whereas the sleeve of the intermediate member has an
opening with a connection piece (3) which is capable of projecting

into an opening (6b) provided in the wall of a cistern (6), whereas
the intermediate member has a spout (4) for draining the water into

the connection piece (3),
characterized in that
 the spout (4) projects through
the opening provided in the sleeve of the intermediate member (2)

into the connection piece (3) and is contiguous to the wall of the
intermediate member in the area of said intermediate member,

whereas a space (7) is provided underneath the spout (4) toward the
inner wall of the connection piece (3), the spout (4) being provided in

the area of the intermediate member with an opening (4a), whereas
the area around the opening (4a) has been given the shape of a deep

saucer.
Branch piece according to claim 1,
characterized in that
 the connection piece (3) is
screwable onto the
 wall (6a) of a cistern (6).
Branch piece according to claim 1,
characterized in that
 the opening (4a) of the spout
(4) is arranged in a concentric position relative to the downspout

(1a).
