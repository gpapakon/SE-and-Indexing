<INVENTION-TITLE>
Method of making oxide ceramic dental prosthetic parts.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61C508	A61C510	A61C1308	A61C1308	A61K602	A61K606	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A61C	A61C	A61C	A61C	A61K	A61K	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A61C5	A61C5	A61C13	A61C13	A61K6	A61K6	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
To make accurately fitting oxide ceramic dental prosthetic parts (dental prostheses) in a single step, shrinking of the mixture comprising ceramic particles together with binders and/or make-up liquids during sintering has to be prevented. This is achieved by addition of particles of easily oxidisable metals, metal suboxides and/or metal hydrides which, during sintering, undergo a volume increase with uptake of oxygen and compensate for sintering shrinkage at an appropriate mixing ratio.
</ABSTRACT>
