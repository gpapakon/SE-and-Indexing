<ABSTRACT>
For highly loaded friction pairings, at least one sintered material layer (93), consisting in any desired composition of carbides, nitrides, borides, oxides of the metals of groups IV to VI of the periodic table, is applied by means of a high-temperature CVD process to a hardenable steel (27) which has, directly on the surface or in the surface a diffusion zone (2) specially enriched with carbon and/or nitrogen and/or boron, the sintered material layer (93) of which there is at least one growing on at varying rates due to a differentiated nucleation density in the diffusion zone (2). As a result, about 100,000 to 1 million microscopically small flat depressions (4) per square centimetre are formed, whose depth amounts to about 25% to 40% of the total layer thickness (16) and whose areal extent comprises about 300  mu m
