<INVENTION-TITLE>
ARROW FEATHERING STRIP
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>F42B606	F42B600	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>F42B	F42B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>F42B6	F42B6	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
An arrow feathering strip (10) consists of a part cut out of a plastic web with two fastening flaps (12, 14) elastically bent in opposite directions which cause the contour of the strip to become S-shaped when they are glued on the shaft (18) of the arrow.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Arrow feathering strip of resiliently deformable 
material which is cut out from a length of material and 

has a strip body (10) and two securing flaps (12, 14) 
bent in opposite directions along a fold line (16) 

relative to the strip body (10), characterised in that 
the securing flaps are located one behind the other in 

the longitudinal direction. 
Strip according to claim 1, characterised in that 
the flaps (12, 14) can be stuck to an arrow shaft (18). 
Strip according to claim 1 or 2, characterised in 
that the region of the fold line (16) between the body 

(10) and flaps (12, 14) is resiliently pre-stressed. 
Strip according to one of claims 1 to 3, 
characterised in that it is stamped out of weather-resistant 

plastics. 
Strip according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterised in that the flaps (12, 14) are separated 

from one another by an indentation (15). 
Method of feathering an arrow shaft using the strip 
according to one of claims 1 to 5, characterised in that 

the strip is applied with the fold line (16) which 
connects the flaps (12, 14) and the body (10) aligned 

parallel to the axis (18) of the shaft. 
Arrow shaft feathered with strips according to one 
of claims 1 to 5 and preferably produced according to 

the method of claim 6, characterised in that the front 
flaps (12) of all the strips are bent in one direction 

and the rear flaps (14) of all the strips are bent in 
the other direction. 
</CLAIMS>
