<INVENTION-TITLE>
Finger articulation prosthesis with one dish shaped element.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61B1768	A61B1786	A61F200	A61F200	A61F230	A61F230	A61F242	A61F242	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A61B	A61B	A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A61B17	A61B17	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The prosthesis consists of a proximal component (5a,b) attachable to a first bone (10a), and a distal component (6a,b) attachable to a second bone (20a) which is articulated to the proximal component via a sliding joint (7,8). Each component includes a convex head (9a,b;11a,b) corresponding in size to a cartilage surface which it replaces. The convex head of each component covers the entire end face and the end of the lateral surfaces of the bone portion to which it is attached. Alternatively, the head may cover the end surfaces only, while leaving the lateral end portions of the bone free.
</ABSTRACT>
