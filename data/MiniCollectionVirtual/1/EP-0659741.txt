Process for the preparation of lactams

<ABSTRACT>
The present invention consists of a process for the preparation of lactam by vapour-phase reaction of an aliphatic aminonitrile with water, in the presence of a solid catalyst, characterised in that the catalyst is a metal phosphate of general formula (II):  (PO4)nHhM(Imp)p      (II
>
 in which:   - M represents a divalent, trivalent, tetravalent or pentavalent element chosen from groups IIa, IIIb, IVb, Vb, VIb, VIIb, VIII, IIb, IIIa, IVa and Va of the periodic classification of the elements or a mixture of a number of these elements, or M=O, - Imp represents a basic impregnation compound consisting of an alkali metal or alkaline-earth metal, or of mixtures of a number of these metals, in combination with a counteranion to provide for electrical neutrality,   - n represents 1, 2 or 3,   - h represents 0, 1 or 2, - p represents a number between 0 and 1/3 and corresponds to a molar ratio of the impregnant (impregnating agent) Imp to the impregnated (PO4)nHhM.   The aliphatic lactams, such as epsilon-caprolactam in particular, are base compounds in the preparation of polyamides (polyamide 6 from caprolactam).
</ABSTRACT>

Process according to either of claims 1 and 2,
characterized in that, in formula (II) of the metal 

phosphates, the metals from groups 2a, 3b, 4b, 5b, 6b,

Elements represented by M are chosen from beryllium,

 calcium, strontium, barium, aluminium,

Process according to one of claims 1 to 3,
characterized in that metal phosphates of formula (II)

are used which are mixtures of phosphates of several
of the metals M or mixed phosphates of several of the

metals M or alternatively mixed phosphates containing
one or more of the metals M and one or more other

metals such as alkali metals or alkaline earth metals.
Process according to one of claims 1 to 4,
characterized in that the counteranions forming part of

the formula of the impregnating compound Imp are basic

fluoride, nitrate, benzoate and oxalate ions.
Process according to one of claims 1 to 5,
characterized in that the molar ratio p is preferably

between 0.02 and 0.2.
Process according to one of claims 1 to 6,
characterized in that the catalyst of formula (II) is

subjected to a calcination at a temperature between
300°C and 1000°C and preferably between 400°C and 900°C.
Process according to one of claims 1 to 7,
characterized in that the catalyst is chosen more

particularly from lanthanum phosphate, calcinated
lanthanum phosphate, lanthanum phosphate combined with

cerium phosphate, cerium phosphate combined with a

compound.
Process according to one of claims 1 to 8,
characterized in that the molar ratio between the water

and the aminonitrile used is between 0.5 and 50 and
preferably between 1 and 20.
Process according to one of claims 1 to 9,
characterized in that the temperature at which the

process of the invention is carried out is between 200°C
and 450°C and preferably between 250°C and 400°C.
