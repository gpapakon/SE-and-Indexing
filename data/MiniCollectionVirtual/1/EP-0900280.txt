USE OF A POLYPEPTIDE AS CELL RECEPTOR FOR ADENOVIRUSES

<ABSTRACT>
The invention concerns the use of a polypeptide comprising at least 6 continuous amino acids of the sequence as shown in the sequence identifiers 1 to 5 as cell receptor and/or co-receptor of adenoviruses. It also concerns the use of a cell capable of expressing such a polypeptide and that of a ligand capable of influencing the binding of an adenovirus to a host cell and/or its insertion in said cell. Finally, the invention further concerns a method for selecting or identifying a cell receptor of a virus or part of a viral protein determining the binding of the virus to its cell receptor and the use of a bi-functional ligand for targeting an adenovirus towards a host cell bearing on its surface a surface protein other than the natural cell receptor of said adenovirus.
</ABSTRACT>
