<INVENTION-TITLE>
BISPHENOL-DICARBOXYLIC ACID DIESTERS, PROCESS FOR THEIR PREPARATION, COMPOSITIONS CONTAINING THEM AND SHAPED ARTICLES THEREFROM

<ABSTRACT>
Linear aromatic polyesters containing residues of a 
bisphenol and dicarboxylic acid are known to be useful in 

the preparation of shaped articles but lack hydrolytic stability 
at elevated temperatures. 
The invention provides diesters of a bisphenol having 
two dicarboxylic acid residues wherein the dicarboxylic 

acid residues terminate in carboxylic acid ester groups derived 
from a long chain aliphatic monofunctional hydrocarbon 

alcohol. These diesters can be prepared by esterifying 
a diacyl halide with the alcohol and then reacting the 

resulting mixture with the bisphenol. 
The diesters are useful as processing aids for the linear 
aromatic polyesters and compositions containing a 

diester and a polyester provide shaped articles of enhanced 
hydrolytic stability. 
</ABSTRACT>
