<INVENTION-TITLE>
Bias voltage control device of an optical source.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G02F101	G02F101	G02F121	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>G02F	G02F	G02F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G02F1	G02F1	G02F1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
One embodiment comprises:     - a semiconductor laser (11);   - a modulator (13) of the Mach-Zehnder type; - an analog adder (12) for adding a modulation signal (Vm) representing binary data and a DC bias voltage (Vb); - a coupler (18) taking off a fraction of the modulated optical signal;   - a photodiode (19);   - an amplifier (20); - a low-pass filter (21) tuned to a frequency equal to the transmission rate of the binary data;   - an amplifier (22); - an amplitude detector (23) having a time constant very much longer than the period of the filtered signal;   - an amplifier (24); - a microprocessor (16) associated with an analog-to-digital converter (17) and with a digital-to-analog converter (15), modifying the bias voltage (Vb) so as to make the amplitude of the ray extracted by the filter and the amplitude detector less than a set threshold value.   Application to digital optical transmission.
