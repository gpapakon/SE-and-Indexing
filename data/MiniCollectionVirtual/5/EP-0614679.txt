<INVENTION-TITLE>
Infrared lamp having a cuvette in the radiation path.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61N506	A61N506	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A61N	A61N	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A61N5	A61N5	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
In an infrared lamp a cuvette is provided in the radiation path, which cuvette is filled with a liquid containing water and a fungicidally active substance. However, the cuvette comprises two transparent panes lying opposite each other and a housing receiving these panes. The liquid in the cuvette is a buffer solution having a pH value of 7, preferably based on phosphates.
