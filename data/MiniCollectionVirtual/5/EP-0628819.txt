<INVENTION-TITLE>
Coated carrier, method for manufacturing it and its use in immobilising biomolecules on the surfaces of solids.

<ABSTRACT>
Coated carriers consist of an inert solid body and of a monomolecular layer of a polymer which contains active groups which are located at the end of a hydrophilic spacer group. These active groups are suitable for binding molecules of biological origin. Between the inert solid body and the monomolecular polymer layer it is possible to insert 0-200 monomolecular layers without active groups. All the layers are applied by the LB or SA technique. Molecules of biological origin can be bound (immobilised) on these coated carriers.
</ABSTRACT>
