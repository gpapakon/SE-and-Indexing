<INVENTION-TITLE>
UNIT USING HALL ELEMENTS FOR DETERMINING THE POSITION OF THE THROTTLE VALVE IN AN INTERNAL-COMBUSTION ENGINE.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>F02D3500	F02D3500	G01B700	G01B700	G01B730	G01B730	G01D512	G01D514	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>F02D	F02D	G01B	G01B	G01B	G01B	G01D	G01D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>F02D35	F02D35	G01B7	G01B7	G01B7	G01B7	G01D5	G01D5	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The unit proposed for determining the position of the throttle valve in an internal-combustion engine includes a device with means for producing a magnetic field and an array made up of a multiplicity of Hall elements. The Hall elements are spaced a given distance apart along a given line. A magnetic-induction effect is produced which is dependent on the throttle-valve position. Also included is an interpolation circuit which interpolates the analogue output signals from at least two Hall elements to produce a common analogue output signal.
</ABSTRACT>
