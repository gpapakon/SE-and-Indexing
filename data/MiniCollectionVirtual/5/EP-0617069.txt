<ABSTRACT>
Curable, pulverulent mixtures containing     A) a product of the reaction of   A1) compounds containing at least two 1,2-epoxide groups per molecule and having epoxide equivalent weights of from 160 to 600, and A2) aromatic dicarboxylic acids, or a mixture thereof with compounds from the group consisting of (cyclo) aliphatic dicarboxylic acids, monocarboxylic acids and/or monohydric phenols and   A3) optionally cyclic anhydrides,  and   B) a curing agent, where the curing agent cannot be a carboxyl-containing polyester.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A curable pulverulent mixture comprising

A) an epoxy resin having a number average molar
mass M
n
 of from 500 to 10,000 g/mol and a
glass transition temperature of at least 20°C,

obtainable by reaction of
A1) an aromatic compound having at least two 1,2-epoxide
groups per molecule and an epoxide

equivalent weight of from 160 to 600, and
A2) an aromatic dicarboxylic acid or mixture
thereof with a compound from the group

consisting of (cyclo)aliphatic dicarboxylic
acids, monocarboxylic acids and/or monohydric

phenols, and, if desired,
A3) a cyclic anhydride, and
B) a curing agent, polyesters containing carboxyl
groups being excluded as curing agents.
A curable pulverulent mixture as claimed in claim
1, which comprises as curing agent B) a compound of

formula I or II

 
in which each R, independently of the others, is

hydrogen or an alkyl, aryl, aralkyl, cycloalkyl or
heterocyclic radical, R' is the same as R or is an

alkylene or arylene radical which may possibly be
substituted by one or more alkyl, aryl, aralkyl,

cycloalkyl or heterocyclic radicals, and where two
or more radicals may be connected, possibly also by

heteroatoms.
A curable mixture as claimed in claim 1, wherein
2-phenylimidazoline is employed as component B).
A curable mixture as claimed in claim 1, wherein
the quantity of curing agent B) is from 0.01 to 60%

by weight, based on the sum of A) and B).
A curable mixture as claimed in at least one of
claims 1 to 4, wherein component A) has an epoxide

equivalent weight of from 300 to 1,200.
A curable mixture as claimed in at least one of
claims 1 to 5, wherein component A) possesses an

acid number of from 0.01 to 20 mg of KOH/g.
A curable mixture as claimed in at least one of
claims 1 to 6, wherein the glass transition

temperature of component A) is at least 35°C.
A curable mixture as claimed in at least one of
claims 1 to 7, wherein reaction component A1) has

an epoxide equivalent weight of from 160 to 200.
A curable mixture as claimed in at least one of
claims 1 to 8, wherein reaction component A2)

comprises terephthalic acid as aromatic
dicarboxylic acid. 
A curable mixture as claimed in at least one of
claims 1 to 9, wherein the (cyclo)aliphatic carboxylic

acid employed in reaction component A2) is
one having from 2 to 12 and preferably from 6 to 8

carbon atoms.
A curable mixture as claimed in at least one of
claims 1 to 10, wherein from 0.01 to 1 mol of

cyclic anhydride A3) is employed per mol of
reaction product of A1) and A2).
A curable mixture as claimed in at least one of
claims 1 to 11, wherein the cyclic anhydride A3)

employed is one from the group consisting of
succinic anhydride, phthalic anhydride, Δ
4
-tetrahydrophthalic
anhydride and hexahydrophthalic

anhydride.
A curable mixture as claimed in at least one of
claims 1 to 12, which comprises conventional additives.
A substrate coated with a coating which is obtained
by fully curing a mixture as claimed in at least

one of claims 1 to 12.
</CLAIMS>
