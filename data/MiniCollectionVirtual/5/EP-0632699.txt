<CLAIMS>
Snap fastener (1) for securing shoe laces (8), 

characterized by
 the following features:
 
an inner sleeve (2) is guided in an outer sleeve (3) so that 

the sleeves are substantially movable vertically to each 
other;
 
the inner sleeve (2) accommodates an inner body (25) which is 

rotatably mounted;
 
means (40, 41; 44-48) are provided which effect rotation of 

the body 25 upon relative movement between the inner sleeve 
(2) and the outer sleeve (3);
 
at least one opening (4; 5) permits the shoe lace (8) to be 

passed therethrough;
 
each opening (4; 5) is formed by a borehole (31, 35, 51; 32, 

36, 52) extending through the inner sleeve (2), the body (25) 
and the outer sleeve (3);
 
the boreholes (31, 35, 51; 32, 36, 52) of the inner sleeve 

(2), the body (25) and the outer sleeve (3) associated with 
each of said openings are in alignment with each other when 

the snap fastener (1) assumes the unlocking position so that 
the shoe lace (8) can be readily passed therethrough;
 
a relative movement between the inner sleeve (2) and the 

outer sleeve (3) from the unlocking position effects the 
displacement of the boreholes (31, 35, 51; 32, 36, 52), 

whereby the shoe lace (8) is immovably clamped. 
Snap fastener according to claim 1, characterized in 
that two openings (4; 5) offset relative to each other are  

 
provided for one shoe lace end portion (6, 7) each to be 

passed therethrough, the shoe lace end portions (6, 7) being 
bent at right angles at least once when the inner sleeve (2) 

and the outer sleeve (3) are pressed together, said bending 
being reversed when the inner sleeve (2) and the outer sleeve 

(3) are pulled apart again. 
Snap fastener according to claim 1 or 2, characterized 
in that, when pressure is exerted on the outer sleeve (3), 

the body (25) is made to rotate as the vertical movement is 
converted into a horizontal circular movement so that the 

shoe lace (8) is immovably clamped by the snap fastener (1), 
while said clamping can be released upon a slight upward 

drawing action. 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that the inner body (25) comprises at least 

one surface (44; 45) bevelled relative to the axis of 
rotation of the body, said bevel substantially extending in 

the circumferential direction of the body (25), and that a 
projection (40; 41) is provided opposite the bevelled surface 

(44; 45), said projection sliding on the bevelled surface 
(44; 45) upon relative movement of the inner sleeve (2) and 

the outer sleeve (3) towards each other, whereby the body 
(25) is rotated. 
Snap fastener according to claim 4, characterized in 
that the bevelled surface (44; 45) starts from a side of the 

body extending substantially vertically to the axis of 
rotation of the body (25). 
Snap fastener according to claim 4 or 5, characterized 
in that the body (25) comprises two bevelled surfaces (44; 

45), and that a projection (40; 41) is disposed opposite each 
of the bevelled surfaces (44; 45).  

 
Snap fastener according to one of claims 4 to 6, 
characterized in that the projections (40; 41) are disposed 

on the inner surface of the sleeve (3) opposite the bevelled 
surface. 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that means (27, 29) are provided which limit 

the maximum extent to which the two sleeves (2, 3) can be 
moved apart. 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that means (26, 28) are provided which 

prevent a turning movement between the inner sleeve (2) and 
the outer sleeve (3). 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that the rotatably mounted body (25) is 

operatively connected with at least one spring (24) 
counteracting a turning movement of the body (25) caused by 

the relative movement between the inner sleeve (2) and the 
outer sleeve (3). 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that the inner sleeve (2) and the outer 

sleeve (3) have a circular cylindrical shape, and that the 
rotatably mounted body (25) comprises a circular cylindrical 

circumferential surface (49) guided by the inner wall of the 
inner sleeve (2). 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that the rotatably mounted body (25) is 

designed as a ring. 
Snap fastener according to claim 12, characterized in 
that at least one of the sleeves (2) is provided with an  

 
axially extending hub (17) around the outside of which the 

ring (25) is rotatably mounted. 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that one of the sleeves (3) comprises an 

axially extending projection (28) which is displaceably 
guided in a cylindrical recess (26) provided in an axially 

extending projection (17) of the other sleeve (2). 
Snap fastener according to claim 14, characterized in 
that the axially extending projection (17) provided with the 

cylindrical recess (26) is designed as a central hub. 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that the boreholes (51, 52) extend through 

the rotatably mounted body (25) parallel to the axis of 
rotation of said body. 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that the boreholes (51, 52) through which 

the shoe lace end portions (6; 7) are passed extend through 
the rotatably mounted body (25) on diametrically opposed 

sides. 
Snap fastener according to one of claims 6 to 17, 
characterized in that the bevelled surfaces (44; 45) are 

diametrically opposite and mirror symmetrical to each other. 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that the boreholes (51, 52) extending 

through the rotatably mounted body (25) and the bevelled 
surfaces (44; 45) arranged on said body are offset, in 

relation to the axis of rotation of said body, by about 90Â° 
relative to each other.  

 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized by a small cover plate (20) which is disposed 

between the rotatably mounted body (25) and the outer sleeve 
(3) and which is held at the inner sleeve (2) and comprises 

boreholes (58, 59) in alignment with the boreholes (35, 36) 
of the outer sleeve (3). 
Snap fastener according to claim 20, characterized in 
that the small cover plate (20) comprises through-holes (56, 

57) which are arranged above the bevelled surfaces (44; 45) 
of the rotatably mounted body (25) when said body assumes the 

unlocking position. 
Snap fastener according to claim 20 or 21, characterized 
in that the threaded shoe laces (8) are drawn into joints 

between the inner surface of the inner sleeve (2) and the 
rotatably mounted body (25) and between said body and the 

inner surface of the small cover plate (20) and clamped by 
the circular movement of the rotatably mounted inner body 

(25). 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that flat depressions (53, 54; 53a, 54a) are 

provided in the end portion of the boreholes (51, 52) of the 
rotatably mounted body (25) against the direction of rotation 

(D) taken in the securing operation, in which depressions the 
shoe lace end portions (6, 7) can be clamped. 
Snap fastener according to claim 23, characterized in 
that portions of the depressions (53, 54; 53a, 54a) are 

provided with a friction-increasing surface (55). 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that the outer sleeve (3) comprises a 

protruding gripping portion (60).  
 
Snap fastener according to one of claims 6 to 25, 
characterized in that the rotatably mounted body is designed 

as an inner ring (25) comprising two bevelled through-holes 
(44, 45; 46, 47), and that two pins (40; 41) are provided on 

the outer sleeve (3) which act on the bevelled through-holes 
when pressure is exerted on the outer sleeve and convert the 

vertical movement into a horizontal circular movement of the 
inner ring (25). 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that it has a cylindrical shape. 
Snap fastener according to one of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that the free shoe lace ends are detachably 

held in shaped parts (9; 10) which are larger than the 
openings (4; 5) so as to prevent the shoe laces (8) from 

being inadvertently withdrawn from the snap fastener (1). 
Snap fastener according to claim 28, characterized in 
that the shaped parts (9; 10) comprise a through-bore (11) 

and another borehole (12) adjacent the one end of the 
through-bore (11), into which borehole the free end of the 

shoe lace end portion (6, 7) threaded through the through-bore 
(11) can be inserted.
