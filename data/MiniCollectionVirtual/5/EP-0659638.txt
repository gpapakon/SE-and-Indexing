<INVENTION-TITLE>
Inflatable lifejacket.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B63C900	B63C9125	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B63C	B63C	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B63C9	B63C9	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
In a lifejacket in which a collar-shaped neck piece 1 connects two breast lobes 2 to form an inflatable, one-piece floating body, the double walls of which, in the non-inflated state, can be folded from the outside to the inside in the area of the side parts 6, 7 of the neck piece 1 and of the outer parts 22 of the breast lobes 2 and from the inside to the outside in the area of the inner parts 3 of the breast lobes 2 and are releasably held in this position, the belt bands of a body and/or rescue belt 3 are fastened to the free ends of the breast lobes 2. A neck belt 12 provided on the neck piece 1 engages on the body and/or rescue belt 13. In this arrangement, the outer parts 22 of the breast lobes 2, and in particular the side parts 6, 7 of the neck piece 1, can be folded about a common fold line b in such a way that the outer margins of the side parts 6, 7 of the neck piece 1 rest on one another in its centre so that the neck piece 1 forms a triangle, the point or tip of which is releasably connected to the neck belt 12.
