<INVENTION-TITLE>
Arrangement for pressing and holding wooden floor- or wallcovering elements during their fastening.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>E04F2100	E04F2122	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>E04F	E04F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>E04F21	E04F21	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Device (arrangement) (1) for pressing and holding down planks of wood during the operation of fastening them into one another, these planks of wood being of the floor slat or wainscotting type which are intended, for example, to cover a wall of a room such as a ceiling, a floor, a wall, a vault, etc., fastening to a framework or panel preferably made of wood, but which could also be made up of panels lining the said wall of the room, characterised in that it is made up of the following elements:     - an operating knob (2),   - a metal rod (3),   - a lever arm (4) exhibiting an extension (5) at one of its ends,   - a first metallic element (6) connected with the said arm (4),   - a second metallic element (7) for raising the rod (3),   - at least three springs (8, 9, 10), - a connection means (11) intended to couple onto the said plank of wood, and - at least one fastening means (12) intended to fasten the said device (1) onto the wooden framework of the wall.
