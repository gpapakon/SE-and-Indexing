<INVENTION-TITLE>
Improved high alkaline proteases.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C11D338	C11D3386	C12N952	C12N954	C12N1557	C12N1557	C12R107	C12R119	</CLASSIFICATIONS>

<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C11D3	C11D3	C12N9	C12N9	C12N15	C12N15	C12R1	C12R1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Optimized high-alkaline proteases suitable for use in detergent formulations are described. Also described is a process for their production, in which microorganisms transformed by mutated DNA sequences are used. These DNA sequences are obtained by modifying, by directed mutagenesis in particular positions, DNA sequences which code for high-alkaline protease produced by bacillus species. In the resulting high-alkaline proteases the original amino acids are replaced by certain other amino acids in two, three, four or five of the stated positions.
</ABSTRACT>
