<INVENTION-TITLE>
Antifoam compounds for ABS-latex
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C08F600	C08L5502	C08F600	C08L5500	C08J303	B01D1904	C08J302	B01D1902	C08L5500	</CLASSIFICATIONS>

<ABSTRACT>
A mixture suitable as a defoamer - in particular for ABS latexes - comprising (A) C10-C20-alcohol (B) mineral oil (C) esters of a thiodicarboxylic acid having 4 to 8 C atoms and a C10-C20-alcohol and (D) nonionic emulsifier, and a process for removing residual monomers from ABS latexes using these defoamer (antifoam) mixtures.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A mixture suitable for use as a defoamer comprising 

A) a C
10
-C
20
 alcohol, 
B) a mineral oil, 
C) an ester from a thiodicarboxylic acid with 4 to 8 
carbon atoms and an alcohol with 10 to 20 carbon 

atoms and 
D) a non-ionic emulsifier. 
A mixture according to Claim 1 comprising 

30 to 85 parts by wt. of A, 
5 to 30 parts by wt. of B, 
5 to 40 parts by wt. of C and 
2 to 15 parts by wt. of D. 
A mixture according to Claim 1, comprising 

A) octadecanol and/or octadecenol, 
B) a paraffin oil, 
C) didodecyl thiodipropionate and 
D) an alkoxylated fatty alcohol and/or an 
alkoxylated alkylphenol. 
A process for removing residual monomers from ABS 
latices, characterised in that a defoaming mixture in 

accordance with Claim 1 is added to an ABS latex and 
the latex is then degassed in a manner known per se. 
ABS latices obtained by a process in accordance with 
Claim 4.
