<INVENTION-TITLE>
Three-phase-three-conductors current meter with three measuring systems.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G01R2100	G01R2100	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>G01R	G01R	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G01R21	G01R21	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The current in the third phase Is is formed using simple means on the basis of current measurements (Ir, It) in two phases (R, T). For this purpose, the outputs of two matching converters (3, 4) which serve for conversion of the two phase currents, are connected to the inputs (E1, E2) of an adding and inverting amplifier (5, 19). The latter forms the third current (Is) according to the relationship Is = -(Ir + It). The three-phase meter can simultaneously be used for active and reactive power measurement in a three-phase/three-conductor network (R, S, T) without a neutral conductor.
