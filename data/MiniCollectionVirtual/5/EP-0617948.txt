<ABSTRACT>
The invention proposes a pacifier (A, B) for children which comprises a mouth rubber (1), a mouth plate (2) and a connection element (4) engaging in an opening (3) of the mouth plate (2) and firmly clamping the open end of the mouth rubber (1). The connection element (4) mounts a gripping member (6, 6a) which can be pivoted relative to the mouth plate (2) and which essentially does not project beyond the edge of the mouth plate (2) at least in a position pivoted against the mouth plate (2).

<CLAIMS>
Pacifier (A, B) for children, having a rubber 
mouthpiece (1) a mouth plate (2) and a connection element 

(4) for pivotably mounting a handle part (6, 6a), which 
connection element engages in an opening in the mouth 

plate (2) and firmly clamps the open end of the rubber 
mouthpiece (1) on the mouth plate (2), characterized 


in that the mouth plate (2) and the handle part (6, 6a) 
have a cross-sectional contour such 
that the mouth plate (2) has a face plate region which, 
in the in-use position of the pacifier (A, B), faces the 

nose and the chin of the child, and 
that the handle part (6; 6a) does not project beyond 
the face plate region of the mouth plate (2), at least in 

a transverse position (Q) in which it is pivoted onto the 
mouth plate. 
Pacifier for children according to Claim 1, 
characterized in that the the handle part (6, 6a) has a 

pivoting radius which is substantially smaller than the 
radius of the mouth plate (2). 
Pacifier for children according to Claim 1 or 2, 
characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) has a shape 

which is flattened by comparison with a circular ring, 
the flattened side lying approximately parallel to the 

pivot pin (26). 
Pacifier for children according to Claim 1 or 2, 
characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) has the 

shape of a circular ring. 
Pacifier for children according to Claim 1, 
characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) has a  

 
contour which, when it is resting on the mouth plate (2), 

has a recess in the region of the nose of the child. 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims 
1 to 5, characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) has 

eyelets (20, 21), in particular annular eyelets, which 
are attached on the outside, or inner braces (30). 
Pacifier for children according to Claim 6, 
characterized in that the handle part (23, 24) is 

designed in the manner of a key-ring, a butterfly or a 
pair of spectacles. 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims 
1 to 7, characterized in that the mouth plate (2) has 

further openings (23, 24), the handle part (6, 6a), in 
its position (Q) pivoted towards the mouth plate (2), 

surrounding in a protective manner, in particular with 
the eyelets (20, 21) or with the sections formed by the 

braces (30), at least part of the openings (23, 24). 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims 
1 to 8, characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) has 

a pivot pin (26) which lies substantially parallel to the 

mouth plate (2). 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims 
1 to 9, characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) has 

latching means (17) for fixing in a predetermined position 
(Q). 
Pacifier for children according to Claim 10, 
characterized in that the latching means are designed as 

a latching projection (17) on a bearing web (16) of the 
handle part (6, 6a) and engage in a recess (18) in the 

connection element (4). 
Pacifier for children according to Claim 11, 
characterized in that the connection element (4) has an 

annular eyelet (15) which receives the bearing web (16) 
for pivotably mounting the handle part (6, 6a). 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims 
1 to 12, characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) 

and/or the connection element (4) are injection-moulded 
in one piece. 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims  
 

1 to 13, characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) is 
injection-moulded in essentially one piece, the pivot pin 

(26) being subsequently attached, in particular bonded or 
welded on. 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims 
1 to 14, characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) is 

secured centrally on the mouth plate (2), in particular 
in the central opening (3). 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims 
1 to 14, characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a) is 

secured off-centre on the mouth plate (2). 
Pacifier for children according to Claim 16, 
characterized in that the annular eyelet (15), for the 

purpose of the off-centre securing, is arranged laterally 
on the centrally secured connection element (4). 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims 
1 to 17, characterized in that its pivot pin (26) lies 

parallel to and/or outside the plane of the handle part 
(6, 6a). 
Pacifier for children according to one of Claims 
1 to 18, characterized in that the handle part (6, 6a), 

the mouth plate (2) and/or the connection element (4) is 
manufactured from polypropylene or from another suitable 

plastic. 
Pacifier for children according to one or more of 
the preceding claims, characterized in that the mouth 

plate (2) is designed as a flat mouth disc. 
Pacifier for children according to one or more of 
the preceding claims, characterized in that the mouth 

plate is concavely curved in the direction of the rubber 
mouthpiece (1).
