Magnetic ink concentrate.

<ABSTRACT>
Magnetic ink concentrates are described which consist of a dispersion of superparamagnetic or magnetically hard solid particles which are present in water or in alcohol having a dielectric constant of 
>
/= 20 in the presence of dispersing substances, the dispersing substances (dispersants) being polyelectrolytes which have a molecular weight of between 500 and 100,000 and contain at least two identical or different hetero atoms in the main chain and at least three free carboxyl groups or three free amino groups in the side chain. Such polyelectrolytes are readily biodegradable. These ink concentrates have advantageous properties, especially with regard to stability to sedimentation.
</ABSTRACT>
