<INVENTION-TITLE>
Acid modified polyesters and their use in stove enamels.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B65D2500	B65D2534	C08G6300	C08G6312	C08G6316	C08G6320	C08G6360	C09D16700	C09D16700	C09D16702	C09D16702	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B65D	B65D	C08G	C08G	C08G	C08G	C08G	C09D	C09D	C09D	C09D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B65D25	B65D25	C08G63	C08G63	C08G63	C08G63	C08G63	C09D167	C09D167	C09D167	C09D167	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Acid-modified polyesters obtained by reacting   (a) from 20 to 60 mol%, preferably 30-50 mol%, of at least one dicarboxylic acid, (b) from 3 to 30 mol%, preferably 5-20 mol%, of a dihydroxyalkanecarboxylic acid,   (c) from 10 to 60 mol%, preferably 20-50 mol%, of a diol, (d) from 0 to 30 mol% of a polyalcohol containing more than two OH groups or of a polycarboxylic acid, (e) from 0 to 20 mol%, preferably up to 10 mol%, of a monocarboxylic acid, where the ratio between the sum of the hydroxyl equivalents and the sum of the carboxyl equivalents in the reactants is between 0.5 and 2.0, the amounts of free hydroxyl groups in the end product are between 30 and 350 mmol of hydroxyl groups in 100 g of solid resin, preferably between 100 and 250 mmol of hydroxyl groups in 100 g of solid resin, and the content of free neutralised and/or neutralisable acid groups is between 5 and 350 mmol of acid groups in 100 g of solid resin, preferably between 9 and 120 mmol of acid groups in 100 g of solid resin.
</ABSTRACT>
