<CLAIMS>
Cam mechanism with selectively switchable 
constant and variable output shaft (45) stroke, in 

particular for looms for the production of towel 
fabric and/or smooth fabric, wherein the elements 

which transmit the series of movements of the 
output shaft (45), such as cams (8,9), a pivotably 

mounted roller lever (15) and connecting rods (24, 
38, 43, 25, 40, 44) are engaged between a motor-driven 

drive shaft (3) and the output shaft (45), 
wherein the cams (8,9) are disposed on the drive 

shaft (3), sensor elements (11,12) of the roller 
levers (15) being in operating connection with the 

peripheral contour of the cams, with linkages 
formed of the connecting rods (24, 38,43, 25, 40, 

44), and engaged between the roller lever (15) and 
the output shaft (45), and with a switching device 

(54) connecting the linkages to the roller lever 
(15), characterised in that 


the linkages are formed as a four-bar mechanism 
and the cam mechanism comprises at least a first 

and a second four-bar mechanism; 
in each case, a four-bar lever (24,25) is  
 

disposed in the casing (4) coaxially to an 
imaginary pivot axis of the roller lever (15) on 

both sides and spaced from the roller lever (15); 
the four-bar mechanism can be brought, 
alternatively or alternatingly, into operative 

connection with the roller lever (15) by means of 
an engaging mechanism (54); 
the imaginary pivot axis of the roller lever 
(15) is surrounded by a roller lever hollow shaft 

(15a); the roller lever hollow shaft (15a) having 
entraining elements (20,21) at its free end; 
a spline shaft (19) passes, to engage, through 
the roller lever hollow shaft (15a), and is 

alternatively or alternatingly in operative 
connection with the entraining elements (20,21) of 

the hollow shaft (15a); 
the spline shaft (19) is coupled to the 
engaging mechanism (54) which performs a switching 

movement; 
the engaging mechanism (54) is in operative 
connection with a control device (33) disposed on 

the drive shaft (3); 
the four-bar lever (24) comprises providing 
means (24c, 57, 59) for the adjustment of the 

rotational angle of the batten shaft (46) which 
serve to determine the height of the nap in the 

fabric; and 
the four-bar lever (24) which determines the  
 

height of the nap comprises a device (60) for 
stopping the providing means (24c, 57, 59) which 

perform the adjustment of the rotational angle. 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 1,
 
characterised by being formed as a gear change 

which can be switched between a nap fabric 
mechanism and a smooth fabric mechanism by means 

of the spline shaft (19) which is axially 
displaceable by the control device (33) and the 

engaging mechanism (54). 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 1,
 
characterised in that each four-bar mechanism 

comprises the first four-bar lever (24) and the 
second four-bar lever (25) disposed on the left 

side and on the right side of the roller lever 
(15). 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 1,
 
characterised in that each of the entraining 

elements (20,21) disposed on either side of the 
roller lever hollow shaft (15a) has inner gear 

teeth (20a,21a). 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 4,
 
characterised in that the entraining ele
ments 
(20,21) are a component of the roller lever hollow  

 
shaft (15a). 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 4,
 
characterised in that the entraining elements 

(20,21) are gear wheels having inner teeth. 
Cam mechanism according to Claims 1 to 3, 
characterised in that the first and second four-bar 

levers (24,25) which are arranged or disposed 
at a distance from each entraining element (20,21) 

are pivotably mounted. 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 7,
 
characterised in that the first and second four-bar 

levers (24,25) and the roller lever (15) are 
mounted in the mechanism casing (4) so as to be 

pivotable about the imaginary axis and laterally 
spaced from the roller lever hollow shaft (15a). 
Cam mechanism according to one of Claims 1 
to 8, characterised in that the shaft of the four-bar 

lever (24) is partially formed as a hollow 
shaft and the shaft of the four-bar lever (25) is 

completely formed as a hollow shaft and both 
shafts have an inner thread (24b or 25b). 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 9,
 
characterised in that the inner gear teeth (24b or  

 
25b) of the four-bar lever (24 or 25) can be 

brought alternatively or alternatingly into 
contact with corresponding outer gear teeth (19c) 

of the spline shaft (19). 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 1,
 
characterised in that the one free end of the 

spline shaft (19) is connected to a connecting rod 
(26) of the engaging mechanism (54) in a shape-locking 

manner. 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 1,
 
characterised in that the engaging mechanism (54) 

comprises a connection rod (26); a lever (36) 
engaging on the free end of the connection rod 

(26), which lever is preferably clamped to a shaft 
(28) rotatably mounted in a part mechanism casing. 

(29); and a pair of externally controlled pivot 
levers (30,31) connected rigidly to the shaft 

(28). 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 12,
 
characterised in that each pivot lever (30,31) 

bears, at its free end, a roller (32) which can 
be brought into engagement with one of the control 

cams (33a) of the control device (33) which is 
non-rotatably mounted on the drive shaft (3).  

 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 12,
 
characterised in that each pivot lever (30,31) is 

connected to a setting member (35). 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 1, in the 
case of which the providing means consist in that. 

of the two first four-bar levers (24,25), the 
pivot axis of which is identical to the imaginary 

pivot axis of the roller lever (15), has a section 
(59) provided with gear teeth (59a), the teeth of 

which engage a pinion (57c) which is driven by a 
setting motor (57) and can be brought out of 

engagement, and the four-bar lever (24) has an 
arcuate guide (24c) for a pivot lever (37), which 

connects a coupling (38) of the cam mechanism to 
the four-bar lever (24) in a manner which is 

adjustable as regards stroke. 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 1,
 
characterised in that the first four-bar levers 

(24,25) have a fork-shaped arm (24a,25a) for 
accommodating a pivot lever (37,41). 
Cam mechanism according to Claim 1,
 
characterised in that the stopping device (60) 

comprises a pressure-agent operated piston and 
cylinder unit (61), the cylinder (60a) of which is 

secured to one of the arms (24a) whereas the  
 

piston (61b) thereof has a piston rod (61c) 
engaging the arms (24a) and the pivot bearing (37) 

through the floor of the cylinder (60a), the 
piston rod (61c) accommodating at its free end a 

connection element (62) formed as an adjustable 
abutment supported on one of the arms (24a). 
Cam mechanism according to claim 17,
 
characterised in that the stopping device (60) is 

provided in the vicinity of the fork-shaped arm 
(24a).
