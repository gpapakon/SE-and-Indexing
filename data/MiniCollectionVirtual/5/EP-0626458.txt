<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for recovering the metallic phase from dispersed mixtures of light metals and non-metallic components.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C01F700	C22B704	C22B704	C22B2100	C22B2100	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C01F	C22B	C22B	C22B	C22B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C01F7	C22B7	C22B7	C22B21	C22B21	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The invention deals with the object of providing an advantageous process for recovering the metallic phase from a disperse mixture consisting of at least one light metal and of at least one non-metallic phase, and, if appropriate, for at least partial separation of the non-metallic phases. For this purpose, the disperse mixture is brought to a temperature in a range above the melting point of the metal or of the alloy, whereupon the mixture is introduced into a device and subjected to an acceleration and is disintegrated. The metallic phase is coalesced, collected and allowed to solidify.
</ABSTRACT>
