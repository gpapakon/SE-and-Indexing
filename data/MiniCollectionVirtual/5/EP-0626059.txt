<ABSTRACT>
Described is a material handling apparatus for processing heated formed ferrous metal products and/or glass products comprising a face centered cubic crystal intermetallic alloy of a metal aluminide having the property of withstanding repeated thermal cycling from ambient temperature to about 1600  DEG F wherein the metal is selected from the group consisting of a group VIII metal of the Periodic Table. Also described is an aluminide roll that also is characterized as retaining its ultimate tensile strength at 1600  DEG F of at least 75 % of the tensile strength the roll had at ambient room temperature.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A material handling apparatus for processing heated
formed ferrous metal products and/or hot glass products,

comprising:

   a cast metal apparatus having an as-cast structure
comprising:


a face centered cubic intermetallic single phase of
nickel aluminide and having the property of retaining at

least 75% of its ultimate tensile strength as measured at
room temperature when heated to about 871°C (1600°F)

wherein the nickel aluminide consists
essentially of nickel, aluminum and chromium, wherein the

nickel ranges from 78,9 to 79,9% by weight; chromium is
present in an amount from 7,5 to 8,0% by weight and

aluminium is present in an amount of 8,0 to 8,5% by weight
and of alloying elements chosen from the group of

boron, silicon, manganese, hafnium, colombium, tantalum, titanium
tungsten, zirconium, molybdenum and mixtures thereof in an

amount of 0,001 to 5% by weight.
Apparatus according to claim 1 wherein the nickel
aluminide comprises as alloying elements in weight percent:


molybdenum 2,9 to 3,2
zirconium 0,6 to 0,9
boron 200 to 500 p.p.m.
A roll useful for transferring heated formed
ferrous metal products and/or glass products comprising a

cast metal roll having an as-cast structure comprising a
face centered cubic intermetallic single phase of nickel

aluminide and having the property of retaining at least 75%
of its ultimate tensile strength as measured at room

temperature when heated to about 871°C (1600°F) wherein
the nickel aluminide consists essentially of

nickel, aluminum and chromium, wherein the nickel ranges
from 78,9 to 79,9% by weight, chromium is present in an
