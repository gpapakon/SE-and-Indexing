<INVENTION-TITLE>
Method for determining the optimal length of a data block in a time division multiple access (TDMA) communications system.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>H04J316	H04J316	H04L100	H04L100	H04L2503	H04L2503	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>H04J	H04J	H04L	H04L	H04L	H04L	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>H04J3	H04J3	H04L1	H04L1	H04L25	H04L25	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
An estimate of the pulse response of the communication channel is produced on the basis of received symbols of the reference sequence (SR), each data sequence (D', D) has associated with it a mean quadratic estimate error, a bracketing of which is determined by two respectively lower and upper limit functions depending particularly on the length (ns) of this data sequence (D) and on the length (P) of the reference sequence (SR), and, on the basis of the said lower and upper limit functions, a bracketing of the optimal length (nopt) of the said data sequence (D) is determined. The method also comprises an optimisation of the length (P) of the reference sequence (SR). Use in telecommunications.
