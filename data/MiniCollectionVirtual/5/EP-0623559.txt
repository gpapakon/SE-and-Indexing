<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for separating dissolved sulfates from aqueous solutions, especially from waste waters.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C02F152	C02F152	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C02F	C02F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C02F1	C02F1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
In a process for separating off dissolved sulphates from aqueous solutions, in particular waste waters (effluents), these sulphates are precipitated out as sparingly soluble calcium aluminate sulphates. In order to carry out the process with short reaction times, calcium hydroxide suspension and aqueous sodium aluminate solution are used for the precipitation.
</ABSTRACT>
