Radiator.

<ABSTRACT>
The radiator (space heater) has heating elements (5, 6) which run in a rectangular housing and are held and guided in the housing interior by means of transverse walls (7). Arranged above the top housing wall (3) is a baffle (9) which extends over the length of the heater, preferably forms part of a box section (10) and runs obliquely forwards and upwards from the housing rear wall (2). By virtue of this arrangement, a heated airflow is directed towards the middle of the room.
