<INVENTION-TITLE>
Cylinder lock with key, groups of such cylinder locks, a key for such a cylinder lock, and a group of keys and key blanks
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>E05B1900	E05B2700	E05B2700	E05B1906	E05B1900	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>E05B	E05B	E05B	E05B	E05B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>E05B19	E05B27	E05B27	E05B19	E05B19	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
What is proposed is a lock cylinder (16), the key-receiving channel (12) of which and the key (10) of which each have a bottle profile. The bottle profile can be modified for one or for both side faces (10l, 10r).

<CLAIMS>
A key body (10, 56) of a cylinder lock key, comprising a
key handle (56) and a key shank (10) with a shank axis

(A) and with an enveloping shank profile (10b), the
enveloping shank profile (10b) comprising a central

plane (E-E) containing the shank axis (A), a spine edge
face (10d), a breast edge face (10p) and two side faces

(10l, 10r) on each side of the central plane (E-E),
which define a shank height (h) measured orthogonally

with respect to the shank axis (A) and along the central
plane (E-E) and a shank width (2S) measured orthogonally

with respect to the central plane (E-E) and variable
over the shank height (h),

wherein, in addition, at least one side face (10l, 10r)
comprises, at least over part of the shank height (h),

at least one convex-cylindrical curved face (10lg, 10rd)
parallel with the shank axis (A), and wherein grooves

(n1-n4, m1-m4) and/or ribs parallel with the shank axis
(A) are formed or provided at least on one of the side

faces (10l, 10r), which grooves (n1-n4, m1-m4) and/or
ribs, in conjunction with the enveloping shank profile

(10b), provide a shank profile (10a),

characterised in that the at least one convex-cylindrical
curved face (10lg, 10rd) has a maximum

radius of curvature (rg, rd), at least over a portion
extending parallel to the shank axis (A), which is

greater than the distance (S) between this portion and
the central plane (E-E).
A key body according to claim 1, characterised in that
the shank height (h) is at least twice, preferably at

least 2.5 times, as great as the maximum shank width 
(2s) and/or that the maximum radius of curvature (rg,

rd) corresponds to from approximately 1.5 to
approximately 6 
times the maximum shank width,
preferably at least 2.5 times to at least 5 times.
A key body according to either one of claims 1 and 2,
characterised in that the two side faces (10l, 10r) each

have at least one curved face (10lg, 10rd).
A key body according to any one of claims 1 to 3,
characterised in that the curved face (10lg, 10rd)

exhibits the maximum radius of curvature (rg, rd) over
the full extent of its height.
A key body according to any one of claims 1 to 4,
characterised in that the curved face (10lg) exhibits

different radii of curvature (10lgm, 10lgo, 10lgu) over
the full extent of its height.
A key body according to claim 5, characterised in that
the curved face (10lg, 10rd) exhibits the maximum radius

of curvature in the central part (10lgm) of its height
and a smaller radius of curvature in at least one end

area (10lgo, 10lgu) of its height.
A key body according to any one of claims 1 to 6,
characterised in that the at least one side face (10l,

10r) takes the form of a curved face (10lg, 10rd) in an
envelope profile portion in the vicinity of the spine

edge face (10d) and of a flat face (10lp, 10rp) in an
envelope profile portion in the vicinity of the breast

edge face (10p).
A key body according to claim 7, characterised in that
the flat face (10lp, 10rp) of the envelope profile

portion in the vicinity of the breast lies obliquely or
approximately parallel to the central plane (E-E). 
A key body according to either one of claims 7 and 8,
characterised in that the curved face (10lga, 10rda) of

the envelope profile portion in the vicinity of the
spine merges with the spine edge face (10da) at a

transition angle (α) in the vicinity of the spine of
between approximately 70° and approximately 100°.
A key body according to any one of claims 7 to 9,
characterised in that the curved face (10lga, 10rda) of

the envelope profile portion in the vicinity of the
spine merges with the flat face (10rpa) of the envelope

profile portion in the vicinity of the breast at a
transition angle (β) in the vicinity of the breast of

between approximately 140° and approximately 170°.
A key body according to any one of claims 1 to 10,
characterised in that the enveloping shank profile (10b)

is shaped approximately like the outline of a bottle.
A key body according to any one of claims 1 to 11,
characterised in that the spine edge face (10d) is

rounded.
A key body according to any one of claims 1 to 12,
characterised in that the breast edge face (10p) has

different levels to control spring-loaded pin tumbler
pairs (30a, 30b).
A key body according to any one of claims 1 to 13,
characterised in that at least one of the side faces

(10l, 10r) is designed to interact with a retaining
element or security element (40; 48; 160; 260) of a

cylinder plug (20; 120; 220), which retaining element or
security element (40; 48; 160; 260) influences the

rotatability of the cylinder plug (20; 120; 220). 
A key body according to claim 14, characterised in that
at least one of the side faces (10l, 10r) is designed to

interact with at least one springless auxiliary locking
pin (48) of the cylinder plug (20).
A key body according to claim 14 or claim 15,
characterised in that at least one of the side faces

(10l, 10r) is designed to interact with at least one
security pin (40) of the cylinder plug (20).
A key body according to any one of claims 14 to 16,
characterised in that at least one of the side faces

(110l, 110r, 210l, 210r) is designed to interact with a
locking lever (160, 260) mounted on the cylinder plug

(120; 220).
A key body according to any one of claims 14 to 17,
characterised in that at least one of the side faces

(10l, 10r) is provided with a bevelled insertion portion
(54; 254) in the area of one key shank tip.
A set of key bodies according to any one of claims 1 to
18, especially for hierarchically designed lock

installations, characterised in that the key bodies of
the set (Figs. 1a-1e) each have like enveloping shank

profiles (10b) and different groove and/or rib
arrangements on at least one side face (10l, 10r).
A set of key bodies according to claim 19 in context of
a multiple set of such sets, characterised in that the

key bodies of the set differ from those of other sets in
the multiple set by different enveloping shank profiles

(10ba, 10bb ...).
A set of key bodies according to any one of claims 1 to
18, especially for hierarchically designed lock

installations, characterised in that the key bodies 
differ from each other by different shapes of enveloping

shank profile (10ba, 10bb, ...).
A set of key bodies according to any one of claims 1 to
18, especially for hierarchically designed lock

installations, characterised in that the key bodies,
while having grooves (n1-n4, m1-m4) and/or ribs of

identical shape, at least in the cross-sectional areas
thereof in the vicinity of the central plane, differ

from each other by different shapes of enveloping shank
profile.
A combination of a key body according to any one of
claims 1 to 18 with a cylinder lock conformed to the

lock code, the latter comprising a cylinder lock housing
(24) with a plug bore (18) having a plug bore axis (A)

and a cylinder plug (20) mounted in the plug bore (18)
so as to be rotatable about the plug bore axis (A),

which cylinder plug (20) has a key receiving channel
(12) which is substantially parallel to the plug bore

axis (A) and has a channel profile to which the profile
of the key shank is conformed for insertion into the key

receiving channel.
A group of key body/cylinder lock combinations according
to claim 23 with different lock codes, especially for

hierarchically designed lock installations,
characterised in that the enveloping shank profiles

(10b) of the individual combinations are of identical
construction.
A group of key body/cylinder lock combinations according
to claim 24, characterised in that the key shanks of

like enveloping profile of the individual cylinder locks
(Figs. 1a-1e) of the group differ from each other by

different grooves (n1-n4, m1-m4) and/or ribs on at least
one of the side faces (10l, 10r) and/or by different 

variations in the profile of the breast edge face (10p)
and/or by different variations in the profile of at

least one side face (10l, 10r).
A group of key body/cylinder lock combinations according
to either one of claims 24 to 25, characterised in that,

within a subgroup (Figs. 1a-1e) of this group, the
distribution of the grooves (n1-n4, m1-m4) and/or ribs

over the side faces (10l, 10r) is fundamentally the same
for the various cylinder locks (Figs. 1a-1e), whereas

the cross-sectional dimensions of grooves (n1-n4, m1-m4)
and/or ribs of corresponding position differ at least

partially from cylinder lock (Fig. 1a) to cylinder lock
(Fig. 1e).
A group of key body/cylinder lock combinations according
to claim 26, characterised in that the grooves (n1-n4,

m1-m4) and/or ribs of corresponding position of the
individual cylinder locks (Figs. 1a-1e) are

geometrically alike in cross-sectional shape, but

different in cross-sectional size.
A group of key body/cylinder lock combinations according
to claim 27, characterised in that the grooves (n1-n4,

ml-m4) and/or ribs of corresponding position differ in
their width, measured in the direction of the shank

height (h), but are alike in their penetration depth
(n2x) perpendicular to the central plane (E-E).
A group of key body/cylinder lock combinations according
to any one of claims 24 to 28 in the context of a

superordinate group comprising several such groups,
characterised in that the group differs from other

groups in the superordinate group by different
enveloping shank profiles (10b1, 10bb, 10bc ...) and the

channel profiles corresponding thereto. 
A group of key body/cylinder lock combinations according
to claim 29, characterised in that the enveloping shank

profiles (10ba, 10bb...) of the groups (Figs. 2a-2e)
differ from those of other groups by difference radii of

curvature (rda, rdb ...) of the curved faces (10lga,
10lgb ...) and/or different centre positions (Mrda,

Mrdb, ...) of the centres of curvature of the curved
faces (10lga, 10lgb, ...) or portions thereof.
A group of key body/cylinder lock combinations according
to claim 23 with different lock codes, especially for

hierarchically designed lock installations,
characterised in that the cylinder locks within the

group have different enveloping shank profiles (10ba,
10bc), while the grooves and/or ribs are of identical

construction from cylinder lock (Fig. 2a) to cylinder
lock (Fig. 2c) of the group (Figs. 2a-2e) at least in

their cross-sectional areas in the vicinity of the
central plane.
A group of key body/cylinder lock combinations according
to claim 31 in the context of a superordinate group

comprising several such groups, characterised in that,
when the grooves and/or ribs in the individual groups

are in basically the same position, the group differs
from other groups of the superordinate group by

different cross-sectional shapes of the grooves and/or
ribs.
