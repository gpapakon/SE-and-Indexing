<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the connecting of wooden elements and epoxy-based composition used in the process.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C08K300	C08K334	C09J16300	C09J16300	E04B126	E04B126	F16B1200	F16B1204	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C08K	C08K	C09J	C09J	E04B	E04B	F16B	F16B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C08K3	C08K3	C09J163	C09J163	E04B1	E04B1	F16B12	F16B12	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Wooden elements (1) are assembled by means of metal reinforcements (3), such as rebars (concrete-reinforcing bars), which remain completely inside the construction, this enabling all the drawbacks related to the bolting of wooden assemblies to be eliminated. In order to fix the reinforcing elements (3) to the wooden elements (1), the reinforcement (3) is coated with a mass of thermosetting resin containing a filler which includes lamellar mineral components. The presence of these lamellar components in the filler makes it possible to confer on the mixture of resin and filler, before it matures, sufficient fluidity for it to be able to be poured into small-sized interstices, while preventing sedimentation of the filler.
