<INVENTION-TITLE>
Monitoring circuit for the operational state of a signal line of a differential signal transmission link.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G08B2900	G08B2906	H04L124	H04L124	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>G08B	G08B	H04L	H04L	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G08B29	G08B29	H04L1	H04L1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A bridge rectifier (B) is connected to the signal lines (A,Â) of the transmission link (US). A first circuit (R1,D1,R2) conducts the voltage (Un) to arrive from the output of the bridge rectifier to a comparator (K). The output signal (WS) of the latter indicates the operational state of the signal lines (A,Â). When a defined voltage is missing at the output of the bridge rectifier (B) in the faulty state of the signal lines (A,Â), a second circuit (R4,D2,R3) becomes active. This circuit forces a voltage (uf) across the comparator (K) with a sign which is reversed in comparison with the derived voltage (Un), using the power supply of the transmission link (+Uv,MP). The output signal (WS) of the comparator (K) now indicates the faulty operational state of the signal lines (A,Â).
