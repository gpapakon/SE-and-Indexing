<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for making coating crumbs and coated product obtained
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
A breadcrumb coating for food products which are to be cooked in the oven comprises cereal crumbs, a source of reducing sugars and more than 7% by weight of fats, preferably from 8 to 12% by weight. It is obtained by cooking and extruding the mixture of starting ingredients, grinding and finally drying the ground product.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Process for preparing breadcrumbs for food products
intended to be oven-cooked, wherein


there is introduced into an extruder a mixture comprising
ground cereals, a reducing sugar and a source of fats in

such a quantity that the final product has a fat content of
8-12 parts by weight,
this mixture is extrusion-cooked at a temperature of
150-230Â°C and at a pressure above 45 bar,
the extruded product is ground, and
the ground product is then dried.
Process according to claim 1, characterized in that
extrusion is carried out in a twin-screw extruder.
Process according to claim 1, characterized in that the
passage time through the extruder is longer than 30

seconds.
Process according to claim 1, characterized in that the
ground cereals are a semolina or flour of wheat, rice or

oats, alone or mixed.
Process according to claim 1, characterized in that the
reducing sugar is lactose, glucose and/or fructose, alone

or mixed.
Process according to claim 1, characterized in that the
fats consist of hydrogenated palm oil.
Breadcrumbs which can be produced by the process
according to one of claims 1 to 6. 
Food product intended to be oven-cooked coated with the
breadcrumbs according to claim 7.
</CLAIMS>
