<INVENTION-TITLE>
Composite material for magnetooptical recording, its production and its use.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G11B1100	G11B11105	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>G11B	G11B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G11B11	G11B11	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Composite material for magnetooptical recording, comprising a thin layer deposited on a non-magnetic solid substrate. The said thin layer is a polycrystalline layer consisting of microcrystals having a gappy spinel ferrite structure based on iron, cobalt and transition metals. By virtue of a particular process for producing the thin layers, making it possible in particular to increase the dimensions of the crystallites, it is possible to optimise the magnetic and magnetooptical properties, and to obtain thin layers exhibiting for example a remanent Faraday rotation and a coercive field which are greater, respectively, than 1 degree per micrometre for the Faraday rotation and 1000 Oe for the coercive field.
</ABSTRACT>
