<INVENTION-TITLE>
Device to adjust the force of a spring.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>F16B502	F16B502	F16F104	F16F112	G05D1604	G05D1606	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>F16B	F16B	F16F	F16F	G05D	G05D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>F16B5	F16B5	F16F1	F16F1	G05D16	G05D16	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a device for adjusting the force of a spring, in particular a set-point spring in a gas pressure regulator, by means of an adjusting screw (7) which can be screwed into a tapped hole (6). The adjusting screw (7) is restrained in the tapped hole (6) by at least one adjusting spring (3) with a force which is greater than the maximum adjustable force of the spring. The construction permits a sensitive, accurate and readily reproducible adjustment of the force of the spring.
