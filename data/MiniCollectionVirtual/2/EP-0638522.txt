<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for degrading oxidizable and reducible substances in processwater and sewage.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C02F1461	C02F1467	C02F172	C02F172	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C02F	C02F	C02F	C02F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C02F1	C02F1	C02F1	C02F1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Process for degrading oxidisable and reducible compounds in process waters and waste waters (effluents) using a two-part electrolysis cell in which peroxosulphates or peroxosulphuric acids are generated in the presence of sulphates or sulphuric acid and nitrogen-carbon-group-containing and/or carbon-sulphur-group-containing and/or carbon-nitrogen-sulphur-group-containing compounds and these peroxosulphates or peroxosulphuric acids are directly reacted with the oxidisable substances in the electrolysis cell or in a downstream reactor. At the same time, the reducible compounds are degraded at the cathode which is composed of a metal which forms poorly soluble metal chlorides. The waste water can be circulated from the cathode chamber or in the anode chamber through the cathode chamber until the oxidisable and reducible compounds are conditioned or degraded.
</ABSTRACT>
