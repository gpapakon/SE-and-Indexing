<INVENTION-TITLE>
Occlusion detection method for an infusion line.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61M5168	A61M5168	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A61M	A61M	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A61M5	A61M5	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Method for detecting occlusions in an infusion line. The method is employed in a motorised syringe pump (8) having a strain-gauge detector and a microprocessor controller. The strain gauges detect the force on the plunger (18) of the syringe over time. The microprocessor converts the force into a pressure and determines the gradient of the pressure/time relationship with respect to a particular time interval. When the gradient exceeds a predetermined value during a time period, the microprocessor triggers an alarm.
