<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for hot end coating of hollow glass articles by vapour deposition of tin compounds.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C03C1723	C03C17245	C03C1725	C03C1725	C07F700	C07F722	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C03C	C03C	C03C	C03C	C07F	C07F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C03C17	C03C17	C03C17	C03C17	C07F7	C07F7	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A process for the hot end coating of hollow glass articles by applying tin compounds, optionally dissolved in organic solvents, to the outer surface of hollow glass articles at a high temperature of from 500 to 600 DEG C, where the tin compounds pyrolise to form thin tin oxide coatings, and monobutyl tin tricarboxylates, which can be dissolved in organic solvents having a boiling point of 
<
 180 DEG C, are vapour-deposited onto the glass surfaces.
</ABSTRACT>
