<INVENTION-TITLE>
Method for evaluation of measurement values from a detector in the exhaust pipe of a combustion engine.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>F02D4114	F02D4114	F02D4122	F02D4122	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>F02D	F02D	F02D	F02D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>F02D41	F02D41	F02D41	F02D41	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
In an internal combustion engine, a probe which senses the exhaust gas mixture is arranged in the exhaust gas system, the said probe supplying a measured value which can be assigned to a lambda value which is stored in an evaluation and control unit ASE. The object of the invention is to detect misfirings and injection failures. For this purpose, the voltage applied to the probe is evaluated. If the voltage tapped off from the probe lies outside the bandwidth of a measured value prescribed for the internal combustion engine in the upper range, an injection failure is detected in the evaluation and control unit. If, on the other hand, the measured value lies outside the bandwidth at a lower voltage value, misfiring is detected. Application in internal combustion engines.
