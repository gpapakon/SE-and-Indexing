<INVENTION-TITLE>
Method for terminating a lightguide
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G02B6245	G02B6245	G02B625	G02B625	G02B638	G02B638	G02B642	G02B642	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>G02B	G02B	G02B	G02B	G02B	G02B	G02B	G02B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G02B6	G02B6	G02B6	G02B6	G02B6	G02B6	G02B6	G02B6	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Fibre-optic light guide (1) is made rotationally symmetrical to fibre's axis (5). Condition and position of the end face (6) of the fibres is crucially important for the optical quality of a plug-in connection between two light guides. The ends are spaced at a minimum distance and the fibres' axes aligned exactly to each other. Projecting fibre end is cut off with a laser producing a front face with the required mechanical and optical quality.
</ABSTRACT>
