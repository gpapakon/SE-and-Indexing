<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the fabrication of fibre reinforced composite materials

<ABSTRACT>
For the fabrication of fibre reinforced composite materials, an endless fibre strand is drawn through a moving aqueous thermoplastic-powdered dispersion by means of deflecting elements. After removing the water phase, the thermoplastic powder is heated and melted onto the fibres. Finally, the fibre strand is impregnated by melt pultrusion with a polymer melt.

<CLAIMS>
A process for producing fiber-reinforced composites
with continuous fiber strands, which comprises

pulling the fiber strand through an agitated aqueous
thermoplastic powder dispersion via deflectors,

removing the dispersing medium, melting the thermoplastic
powder onto the fibers, and subsequently

impregnating the fiber strand in a melt pultrusion.
The process of claim 1, characterized in that more
than 90% of the impregnation of the fiber strand is

effected by the melt pultrusion.
The process of claim 1, characterized in that the
fiber strand is pulled through the aqueous

thermoplastic powder dispersion at a speed of at
least 3 m/min.
