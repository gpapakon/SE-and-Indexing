<INVENTION-TITLE>
Adhesive bandage
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61F1306	A61F1306	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A61F	A61F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A61F13	A61F13	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A bandage is provided with a self-adhesive coating on one side for support of the ankle joint with two elongate strips which is characterised in that the first strip (1) is designed such that it can be stuck under the plantar arch in such a way that each of its ends extends over the ankle region and covers the latter, and the second strip (2) is arranged next to the first strip (1) and is connected to the latter centrally via a connecting member (7), and this second strip (2) has a length such that one of its free part-pieces (5) can be guided, coming from the medial direction, over the dorsal back of the foot to the lateral malleolus as far as the heel and, if appropriate, further approximately upto the big toe, and its other free part-piece (6), coming from the lateral direction, can be guided over the dorsal back of the foot to the medial malleolus as far as the heel and, if appropriate, further below the lateral malleolus to the little toe.

<CLAIMS>
Prefabricated bandage which is self-adhesively
coated on one side for supporting the ankle

joint with two elongated strips, characterized in that
the second strip (2) is arranged adjacent to the first

strip (1) and is connected centrally thereto via a
connection part (7), the first strip (1) being

approximately 50 - 60 cm long and the second strip (2)
being approximately 80 - 100 cm long, the first strip

being approximately 5 - 6 cm wide and the second strip
being approximately 3.5 - 4.5 cm wide, and the two

strips extending at an angle (α) from 0 - 80° to one
another, to the right and left of the connection part

(7).
Prefabricated bandage according to Claim 1,
characterized in that the two strips extend parallel to

one another.
Prefabricated bandage according to Claim 1,
characterized in that the bandage overall is designed

in one piece.
Prefabricated bandage according to Claim 1,
characterised in that the connection between the two

strips has been produced by adhesive bonding, welding
or sewing.
Prefabricated bandage according to Claim 1,
characterized in that the two strips are covered on

their adhesive-coated side with release paper.
Prefabricated bandage according to Claim 5,
characterized in that the release paper is designed in

three parts, one part covering the first strip (1) and
one further part in each case covering each half of the

second strip (2).
Prefabricated bandage according to Claim 5,
characterized in that the release paper is designed in

five parts, one part covering the connection part (7)
and four further strips each covering a part (3), (4),

(5) and (6) of the strips (1) and (2).
</CLAIMS>
