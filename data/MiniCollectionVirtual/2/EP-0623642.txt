<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a process for improving the shelf life (storage stability) of one-component silicone pastes which cure on contact with atmospheric moisture with elimination of acetic acid.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Process for improving the storability of silicone 
materials which harden by eliminating acetic acid, 

these being prepared by using anionically polymerised 
and alkali metal phosphate-containing polysiloxanes, 

characterised in that acid additives containing the 
functional group -SO
3
H or hydrogen chloride or 
compounds which release hydrogen chloride in the 

presence of acetic acid, are added in concentrations 
sufficient to convert the alkali metal phosphates into 

alkali metal salts of the acid additives. 
Process according to Claim 1, characterised in that 
hydrogen chloride, chlorosilanes, sulphuric acid, 

metal hydrogen sulphates, alkyl or aryl sulphonic 
acids, silyl sulphates or silyl sulphonates are used. 
Process according to Claim 1, characterised in that 1 
to 20 equivalents of the acid additive are added per 

equivalent of the alkali metal ion contained in the 
polydimethylsiloxane. 
Process according to Claim 1, characterised in that 
sulphuric acid or hydrogen sulphates of the alkali 

metals are used as additives. 
</CLAIMS>
