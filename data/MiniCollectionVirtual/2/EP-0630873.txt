<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for enhancing the pinning strength of a superconducting Bi-Pb-Sr-Ca-Cu-O ceramic.
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
To increase the pinning force of superconductive Bi-Pb-Sr-Ca-Cu-O material, the Pb2223 ceramic is heated to a temperature of preferably from 865 to 870 DEG C in an oxygen-containing atmosphere for a period of from 1 to 60 minutes. This produces precipitates of (Sr,Ca)14Cu24O41-x and a melt having a composition Bi1.2Pb0.7Sr0.7Ca0.5CuOx.
