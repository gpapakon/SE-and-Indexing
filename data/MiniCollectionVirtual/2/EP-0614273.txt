<INVENTION-TITLE>
Filtering circuit with a resonator in series resonance
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>H03H1104	H03H1104	H03H900	H03H1112	H03H954	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>H03H	H03H	H03H	H03H	H03H	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>H03H11	H03H11	H03H9	H03H11	H03H9	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A filter circuit having a resonator operated in series resonance is described and consists of:   a) a two-pole resonator circuit (2) having a resonator, the conductivity (Lp) of which can be divided into a useful component (LN) and a parasitic component (Lp), b) a first differential stage (1), driven by a signal source (S) and having two active elements (Q1, Q2), the basic terminal of which is in each case connected to a current source and a terminal of the two-pole resonator circuit, c) a compensation circuit which exhibits two terminals and which simulates the parasitic component of the resonator conductivity, d) a second differential stage (3) driven by the signal source and similar to the first differential stage (1) and having two active elements, the basic terminal of which is in each case connected to a current source and a terminal of the compensation circuit, e) an evaluating circuit (5) connected to the two mutually interchanged output terminals of the two differential stages.
