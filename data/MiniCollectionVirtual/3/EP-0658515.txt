<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for removing the oil pollution from liquids, particularly from water.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C02F134	C02F136	C02F334	C02F334	E02B1504	E02B1504	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C02F	C02F	C02F	C02F	E02B	E02B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C02F1	C02F1	C02F3	C02F3	E02B15	E02B15	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A process is specified for removing oil pollution from liquids which do not dissolve oils, in which oil-processing or oil-consuming bacteria are added to the contaminated liquid, which bacteria convert oil particles into environmentally tolerable excretion products. To improve the mixing of oil and bacteria and thus to improve the efficiency of the oil degradation by the bacteria, to generate a superfine colloidal distribution of the oil, the contaminated liquid is radiated with high-energy ultrasound.
