<INVENTION-TITLE>
Assembly for tipping over control cabitnets, capable of being lowered into the ground.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>H02B100	H02B150	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>H02B	H02B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>H02B1	H02B1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
This assembly is of the type in which at least one cabinet (2) is fixed against the inner face of a flap (7) which is articulated horizontally on the edge of a pit. According to the invention, it comprises:   - on the one hand, and for each of the pits, a removable and interchangeable subassembly (8) which, separate from the subassembly (4, 5, 6) delimiting the pit, on which it is set and fixed, consists of the flap (7) first carrying the cabinet and/or other technical elements, of motor means (17) able to make the flap (7) pivot between either of its extreme positions, respectively normal and retracted or temporary and erect, and circuits (45, 46) providing the power supply to the motor means, from means of connection (23), forming part of the subassembly (8) and accessible from outside the pit - and, on the other hand, a means (32) movable between the various pits, common to all these pits and carrying an energy source (32), a power supply circuit equipped with means of connection (43), complementary to those (23) of the subassemblies (8) and means (34) for control of the energy supply to the supply circuit.
