<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for preparing a modified polytetrafluoroethylene and use thereof
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
If the use of polymerisation assistants in ammonium form is avoided in the preparation of a polytetrafluoroethylene having a content of from 0.02 to 1% by weight of perfluoroalkyl vinyl ether units by the suspension process, a polymer is obtained which does not tend to discolour on heating.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A process for the preparation of a polymer of 
tetrafluoroethylene having a content of 0.01 to 1% 

by weight of perfluoro-(alkyl vinyl) ether units 
with 1 to 4 carbon atoms in the perfluoroalkyl 

chain, which comprises reacting the monomers by 
the suspension process in an aqueous medium, 

initiators and polymerization auxiliaries 
essentially not being employed in the form of 

ammonium salts and the initiators being employed 
as alkaline earth metal or alkali metal 

persulfates. 
The process as claimed in claim 1, wherein alkali 
metal persulfates are employed as initiators. 
The process as claimed in claim 1 or 2, wherein 
the polymerization is carried out at 60 to 80Â°C. 
The process as claimed in one or more of the 
preceding claims, wherein the tetrafluoroethylene 

is metered in under a pressure of about 5 to about 
15 bar. 
The use of the polytetrafluoroethylene obtained as 
claimed in claim 1 to 4 for the preparation of a 

molding powder for the preform sintering 
technique. 
</CLAIMS>
