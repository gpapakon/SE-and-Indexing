<INVENTION-TITLE>
Head for a modular shoulder prosthesis.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61F200	A61F200	A61F202	A61F202	A61F230	A61F230	A61F240	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A head (233) for a modular shoulder prosthesis has a substantially spherical outer surface (237) and a stud (241) which interacts with a hole along a longitudinal axis (243) which is inclined by a predetermined angle with respect to a longitudinal axis of a stem of the said device. The substantially spherical outer surface (237) has a centre of curvature (2C) which is offset with respect to the longitudinal axis (243) of the stud (241). The spherical surface extends through a dihedral angle (180@- beta ) lying between 150@ and 170@. One of the faces (239) of the dihedron is substantially perpendicular to the longitudinal axis (243).
