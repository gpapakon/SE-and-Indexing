<CLAIMS>
Process for the electrolytic generation of
arsine from an electrochemical cell (12) in which are

positioned a cathode (7) fed with H
+
 and AsO
2
-
 ions,
where two concurrent reactions take place respectively

producing gaseous arsine and hydrogen, and an anode
(3), where a reaction takes place which is a source of

H
+
 ions, characterized in that the ratio of the H
+
/As
concentrations at the cathode (7) is controlled and

kept constant, so as to produce an H
2+
AsH
3
 mixture in
proportions of between 50% and 95% of AsH
3
, depending on
the H
+
/As ratio applied.
Process according to Claim 1, characterized in
that the H
+
/As ratio is controlled by the following
stages:


a) the electrochemical cell is separated into
two compartments, an anode compartment and a cathode

compartment, using a cationic membrane (11), thus
making it possible to control the material flows inside

the cell;
b) a circulation of the fluid feeding the
cathode compartment is provided which is sufficiently

high to produce a degree of conversion to arsenic at
the cathode of less than 10%;
c) the cathode compartment is fed with H
+
 and
AsO
2
-
 ions via an As
2
O
3
 makeup (8), which is flushed with
an acid solution (19).
Process for the electrolytic generation of
arsine according to either of Claims 1 and 2, 

characterized in that the cathode (7) is made of a
material which promotes the reaction for the formation

of arsine at the expense of the reaction for the
formation of hydrogen.
Process according to one of Claims 1 to 3,
characterized in that the cathode (7) is composed of

lead or copper on which has been deposited bismuth,
lead, thallium or alternatively cadmium.
Process according to one of Claims 1 to 4,
characterized in that the H
+
/As ratio is maintained
between 0.7 and 1.5, preferably between 0.75 and 1.25.
Process according to one of Claims 1 to 5,
characterized in that the mixture of hydrogen and

arsine produced at the cathode (7) is subjected to a
subsequent separation stage in a membrane module (32),

so as to obtain, at the outlet of the module, a higher
concentration of arsine than in the said mixture.
Process according to Claim 6, characterized in
that the permeate side of the membrane is placed under

vacuum.
Process according to Claim 6, characterized in
that flushing is carried out on the permeate side of

the membrane using a gas exhibiting a weak permeation
of the permeate towards the inside of the membrane,

such as nitrogen or SF
6
.
Process according to one of Claims 6, 7 and 8,
characterized in that, before its arrival at the

membrane module (32), the mixture produced at the
cathode (7) is subjected to at least one drying

operation, on a device such as a Peltier-effect cooler
(27) or a molecular sieve (30) or a combination of

these two means, and, if appropriate, to at least one
filtering operation on a particle filter (31).
Device for the electrolytic generation of
arsine which is suitable in particular for the

implementation of the process according to one of
Claims 1 to 9, comprising:


an electrochemical cell (12) in which are
positioned a cathode (7) in contact with an electrolyte 

comprising H
+
 and AsO
2
-
 ions, where two concurrent
reactions take place respectively producing gaseous

arsine and hydrogen, and an anode (3), where a reaction
takes place which is a source of H
+
 ions, the ratio of
the H
+
/As concentrations at the cathode being kept
constant in order to obtain concentrations of arsine in

the hydrogen, at the outlet of the cathode compartment,
varying from 50 to 95%, depending on the H
+
/As ratio
applied; and
a cationic membrane (11) separating the
electrochemical cell into two compartments, an anode

compartment and a cathode compartment; and
means for feeding the cathode compartment
with H
+
 and AsO
2
-
 ions comprising a saturator (8)
composed of an As
2
O
3
 makeup which is flushed with an
acid solution (19).
Device according to Claim 10, characterized in
that the saturator (8) is situated between the

electrochemical cell (12) and the storage tank (10) for
the acid solution (19) feeding the cathode compartment.
Device according to Claim 10, characterized in
that the saturator (8) is situated inside the storage

tank (10) for the acid solution feeding the cathode
compartment, within the said acid solution (19).
Device according to one of Claims 10 to 12,
characterized in that the cathode (7) is made of a

material promoting the reaction for the formation of
arsine at the expense of the reaction for the formation

of hydrogen.
Device according to Claim 13, characterized in
that the cathode (7) is composed of lead or copper on

which has been deposited bismuth, lead, thallium or
alternatively cadmium.
Device according to one of Claims 10 to 14,
characterized in that it comprises, downstream of the

electrochemical cell, a membrane module (32) in which
the arsine/hydrogen mixture produced at the cathode (7)

is subjected to a separation stage, so as to obtain, at 
the outlet of the module, a higher arsine concentration

than in the said mixture.
Device according to Claim 15, characterized in
that the membrane module is connected to means which

make it possible to place the permeate side of the
membrane under vacuum.
Device according to Claim 15, characterized in
that the membrane module is connected to a gas source,

making it possible to flush the permeate side of the
membrane using this gas, which exhibits a weak

permeation of the permeate towards the inside of the
membrane, such as nitrogen or SF
6
.
Device according to one of Claims 15 to 17,
characterized in that it comprises, upstream of the

membrane module, at least one device for drying the
mixture produced at the cathode and, if appropriate, at

least one particle filter (31).
Device according to one of Claims 10 to 18,

characterized in that the anode compartment is of the
flat electrode fed with acid solution type, where

electrolysis of water takes place.
Device according to one of Claims 10 to 18,
characterized in that the anode compartment is of the

gas diffusion electrode fed with hydrogen type, where
oxidation of hydrogen takes place.
</CLAIMS>
