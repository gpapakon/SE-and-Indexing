<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the production of wax esters.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C07C6700	C07C6708	C07C6900	C07C6926	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C07C	C07C	C07C	C07C	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C07C67	C07C67	C07C69	C07C69	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The wax ester is produced from a liquid mixture containing at least one fatty acid and at least one fatty alcohol, the fatty acid and the fatty alcohol containing 6 to 30 C atoms per molecule. The mixture is agitated at temperatures in the range from approximately 105 to 300 DEG C and sprayed into a spray zone. In the spray zone, a water vapour-containing atmosphere is formed which is at least partially removed. Ahead of the spray zone, the mixture of fatty acid and fatty alcohol can be agitated in at least one stirring vessel. The process is preferably carried out without addition of catalyst.
