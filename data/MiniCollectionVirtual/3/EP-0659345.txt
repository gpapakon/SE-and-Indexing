<INVENTION-TITLE>
Method for jointing of poultry such as a duck.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A22C2100	A22C2100	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A22C	A22C	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A22C21	A22C21	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
According to this method, the fowl is arranged on its back:     - the winglets are removed, - the flesh is cut away at the breastbone and the thighs are spread open,   - the liver and the viscera are extracted,  this fowl is then arranged on its front, and   - the thighs and the parson's nose are cut off at the level of the lumbar vertebrae, - notches are made in the neck and along the vertebral column, and the ribs are cut, so as to remove the assembly consisting of the neck and vertebral column,   - the upper wings are deboned as far as the join of the bones,   - the lungs are removed by scraping the small ribs, and - the remaining whole is matured in the cold above freezing after being placed in a net.   Application in the agri-foodstuffs industry.
</ABSTRACT>
