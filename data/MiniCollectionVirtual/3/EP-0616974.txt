<INVENTION-TITLE>
Violet zircon vanadium pigments.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C01B3300	C01B3320	C03C100	C03C104	C09C100	C09C100	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C01B	C01B	C03C	C03C	C09C	C09C	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C01B33	C01B33	C03C1	C03C1	C09C1	C09C1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to violet zircon vanadium pigments which are doped with vanadium and phosphorus in the zircon host lattice and have an increased red shift and a higher colour intensity than previously known pigments of this generic type. The pigments of the invention have, in the CIE-Lab colour system, L values equal to or less than 58, a values equal to or greater than +9 and b values of less than -10. They are prepared by igniting, at 700-900@C, a powder mixture which is free of thermally dissociated zirconium silicate and consists essentially of ZrO2, SiO2, a vanadium and a phosphorus compound and also a fluoride/chloride mineraliser combination in an atom ratio Zr:Si:V:P = 1 : (0.95-1.1) : (0.05-0.20) : (0.008-0.028).
</ABSTRACT>
