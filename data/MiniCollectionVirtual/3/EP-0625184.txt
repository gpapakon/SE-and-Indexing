<CLAIMS>
A process for the production of low-viscosity aqueous 
esterquat concentrates, 
characterized in that
 at least one 
viscosity regulator selected from the group consisting of 


(a) quaternized unsaturated difatty acid trialkanolamine 
ester salts, 
(b) quaternized or pseudoquaternized fatty acid amidoamine 
salts, 
(c) diquaternized fatty acid trialkanolamine ester salts 
 
is added to aqueous solutions of quaternized difatty acid 

trialkanolamine ester salts corresponding to formula 
(I)
: 

 
in which 


R¹CO
is an aliphatic, linear or branched saturated acyl 
radical containing 6 to 22 carbon atoms, 
[Z]
is an ethylene, propylene or isopropylene group and 
X
represents chloride, bromide, sulfate, methosulfate 
or phosphate. 
A process as claimed in claim 1, 
characterized in that
 
quaternized difatty acid trialkanolamine ester salts 

corresponding to formula 
(I)
, in which R¹CO is an acyl 
radical containing 12 to 18 carbon atoms, Z is an ethylene 

group and/or X is chloride or methosulfate, are used as the  
 

esterquats. 
A process as claimed in claims 1 and 2, 
characterized 
in that
 quaternized unsaturated difatty acid trialkanolamine 
ester salts corresponding to formula 
(II)
: 

 
in which 


R²CO
is an aliphatic, linear or branched acyl radical 
containing 16 to 22 carbon atoms and 1, 2 or 3 double 

bonds, 
[Z]
is an ethylene, propylene or isopropylene group and 
X
is chloride, bromide, sulfate, methosulfate or phosphate, 
 
are used as the viscosity regulators. 
A process as claimed in claim 3, 
characterized in that
 
quaternized unsaturated difatty acid trialkanolamine ester 

salts corresponding to formula 
(II)
, of which the fatty 
acid components are derived from oleic acid and/or elaidic 

acid, are used as the viscosity regulators. 
A process as claimed in claims 1 and 2, 
characterized 
in that
 quaternized or pseudoquaternized fatty acid amido-amines 
corresponding to formula 
(IIIa)
, (
IIIb)
 and/or 

(IIIc)
: 

 
in which 


R³CO
is an aliphatic, linear or branched acyl radical 
containing 6 to 22 carbon atoms and 0, 1, 2 or 3 

double bonds, 
[Z]
is an ethylene, propylene or isopropylene group and 
X
is chloride, bromide, sulfate, methosulfate or phosphate, 
 
are used as the viscosity regulators. 
A process as claimed in claim 5, 
characterized in that
 
quaternized fatty acid amidoamines corresponding to formulae 


(IIIa)
, 
(IIIb)
 and/or 
(IIIc)
, of which the fatty acid 
components contain 12 to 18 carbon atoms, are used as the 

Viscosity regulators. 
A process as claimed in claim 5, 
characterized in that
 
quaternized fatty acid amidoamines corresponding to formulae 


(IIIa)
, 
(IIIb)
 and/or 
(IIIc)
, of which the fatty acid 
components contain 12 to 14 carbon atoms, are used as the 

viscosity regulators. 
A process as claimed in claim 5, 
characterized in that
 
quaternized fatty acid amidoamines corresponding to formulae 


(IIIa)
, 
(IIIb)
 and/or 
(IIIc)
, of which the fatty acid 
components contain 16 to 18 carbon atoms, are used as the 

viscosity regulators.  
 
A process as claimed in claim 5, 
characterized in that
 
quaternized fatty acid amidoamines corresponding to formulae 


(IIIa)
, 
(IIIb)
 and/or 
(IIIc)
, of which the fatty acid 
components are derived from oleic acid and/or elaidic acid,

characterized 
in that
 diquaternized fatty acid triethanolamine ester 
salts corresponding to formula 
(IV)
: 

 
in which 


R⁴CO

[Z]

 viscosity regulators corresponding to formula 
(IV)
,

characterized 
in that
 the viscosity regulators are added to the aqueous
