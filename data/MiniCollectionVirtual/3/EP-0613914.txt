<INVENTION-TITLE>
Unsaturated polyurethanes and their use as a dispersing agent for alkyd resins.
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
Unsaturated polyurethanes containing from 20 to 80 % by weight of polyethylene glycol units, obtainable by reacting     a) at least one organic polyisocyanate, b) an unsaturated fatty alcohol or a polyalkylene glycol ether thereof, an unsaturated fatty amine or a product of the reaction of an unsaturated fatty acid and a monoepoxide, c) a compound containing at least one ionic group in addition to two groups which are reactive with isocyanate groups, d) a polyalkylene glycol having a molecular weight of from 750 to 10000, and optionally   e) a compound containing from 3 to 6 hydroxyl or amino groups, where the ratio between the number of isocyanate groups in a) and the number of hydroxyl and amino groups in the other components is from 0.7 : 1 to 1.2 : 1. These unsaturated polyurethanes are used as dispersants for alkyd resins.
</ABSTRACT>
