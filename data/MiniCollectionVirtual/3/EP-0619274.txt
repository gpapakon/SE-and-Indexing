<INVENTION-TITLE>
Bronze or grey coloured soda lime silicate glass
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C03C400	C03C3087	C03C3076	C03C402	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C03C	C03C	C03C	C03C	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C03C4	C03C3	C03C3	C03C4	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A bronze or grey coloured soda-lime-silicate glass, in particular for the production of flat (window, plate, sheet) glass by the casting method or by the float method, having a base composition for the soda-lime-silicate glass of     SiO2: 66 - 74 % by weight   Al2O3: 0 - 3 % by weight   TiO2: 0 - 0.15 % by weight   CaO: 7 - 12 % by weight   MgO: 2 - 6 % by weight   Na2O: 10 - 15 % by weight   K2O: 0 - 3 % by weight   BaO: 0 - 0.04 % by weight   SO3: 0.1 - 0.4 % by weight is characterised in that it contains colouring additives in the following amounts:     Fe2O3: 0.0 - 0.45 % by weight   V2O5: 0.0 - 0.5 % by weight   MnO2: 0.5 - 2 % by weight   NiO: 0.0 - 0.05 % by weight   CuO: 0.0 - 0.1 % by weight   CoO: 0.0 - 0.008 % by weight where the percentages by weight of the constituents forming the soda-lime-silicate glass total 100.
