<INVENTION-TITLE>
Welding studs made with sheets.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B23K920	B23K920	B23K1100	B23K1100	B23K3502	B23K3502	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B23K	B23K	B23K	B23K	B23K	B23K	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B23K9	B23K9	B23K11	B23K11	B23K35	B23K35	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Welding studs are punched from sheet metal, the stud shank 11 being formed from a material strip which is integral with the head 10 and is partly cut free 12 relative to the latter and is bent out of the plane of the head 10. To achieve sufficient buckling stiffness, the material strip forming the shank 11 is deformed about the stud axis, preferably to such an extent that the two side edges 15, 16 of this material strip touch one another.
