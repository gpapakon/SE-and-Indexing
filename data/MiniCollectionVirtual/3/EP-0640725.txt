<CLAIMS>
A loader (18) with posts (22) for attachment to a vehicle (10), by means of one or more brackets
(20) and throats (50) receiving the brackets (20) therein, characterized in that the bracket (20)

has a non-circular cross-section with a maximum distance between two points remote from one
another in the plane of reception of the loader (18), in that the throat (50) has two contact

surfaces (52, 56) for abutment with the points and in that the width of the opening of the throat
(50) is smaller than the maximum distance.
A loader according to claim 1, characterized in that a line extending though the end points of the
maximum distance is inclined in relation to the standing surface of a vehicle (10) receiving the

loader (18).
A loader according to claim 1 or 2, characterized in that the bracket (20) has a pin (72) and the
throat (50) has a recess (70) adapted to receive the pin (72) or vice versa.
A loader according to one or more of the preceding claims, characterized in that the bracket (20)
has a square cross-section and the maximum distance is the path from a rear, lower corner (58)

up to a front, upper corner (54) of the cross-section of the bracket (20).
A loader according to one or more of the preceding claims, characterized in that a front surface
(39) of the bracket (20) is inclined down and to the rear and subtends an angle of about 18

degrees with the vertical.
A loader according to one or more of the preceding claims, characterized in that a further contact
surface (60) is provided between the two contact surfaces (52, 56) and is adapted to make contact

in particular at a point directly above a rear, upper corner (62) of the bracket (20).
A loader according to one or more of the preceding claims, characterized in that the pin (72) on
the bracket (20) extends to the rear, i.e. against the direction of reception of the loader (18) on the

bracket (20).
A loader according to one or more of the preceding claims, characterized in that the throat (50) is
C-shaped and the bracket (20) has an oval cross-section.
</CLAIMS>
