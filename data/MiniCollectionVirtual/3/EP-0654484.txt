<ABSTRACT>
The unpleasant taste of thioctic acid or of dihydrolipoic acid can be concealed by embedding in cyclodextrins. Pharmaceutical forms are described. The inclusion compounds of enantiomerically pure dihydrolipoic acid, thioctic acid and cyclodextrins are novel. They are suitable for the preparation of medicaments.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Granulate, chewable, drinkable or effervescent tablets wherein thioctic acid or its
salts are present in a granulate that is provided with physiologically acceptable

coatings which are insoluble in water and saliva but soluble in the gastric or intestinal
juice.
