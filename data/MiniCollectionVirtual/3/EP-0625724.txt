<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for poling and orienting organic materials containing a non-linear optical chromophore, and their use.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C09K902	C09K902	G02F101	G02F1065	G02F135	G02F135	G02F1355	G02F1361	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C09K	C09K	G02F	G02F	G02F	G02F	G02F	G02F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C09K9	C09K9	G02F1	G02F1	G02F1	G02F1	G02F1	G02F1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a process for the polarisation and orientation of layers of E/Z-isomerisable organic materials, which contain nonlinear-optical chromophores, an electrical field being applied to the layer containing nonlinear-optical chromophores which have a donor/acceptor structure, with simultaneous irradiation, for the purposes of polarisation and orientation. This process is suitable for the polarisation and orientation of layers of E/Z-isomerisable materials, which contain nonlinear-optical chromophores, in a process for the production of devices for nonlinear optics.
</ABSTRACT>
