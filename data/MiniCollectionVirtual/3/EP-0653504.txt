<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for making polycristalline or monocristalline objects by growth from a molten bath.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C30B1500	C30B1500	C30B1534	C30B1534	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C30B	C30B	C30B	C30B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C30B15	C30B15	C30B15	C30B15	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
According to this process the starting point is a bath of molten material 2 and the molten material is drawn in the direction D1 through the die 3 in order to solidify it on the seed 9. To obtain more or less complex shapes, the seed and/or the die is/are additionally subjected to at least one movement of translation, for example along D2 or D3, in a plane perpendicular to the drawing direction D1. The seed may additionally simultaneously be subjected to a movement of rotation about itself. The use of these movements and an appropriate adjustment of the rates of travel make it possible to attain any desired shape whatever.
