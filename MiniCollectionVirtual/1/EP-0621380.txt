Metal reinforcement for the reinforcement of concrete structures, and process and device for making the same

<ABSTRACT>
The subject of the invention is a reinforcement member comprising a steel strip (10) which can be reeled up and intended to be incorporated in the concrete. The faces of the steel strip each comprise substantially straight ribs (12) arranged in at least one longitudinal row (R), these ribs being substantially parallel to each other and inclined with respect to the longitudinal direction of the strip. The ribs (12) of one of the faces of the strip are symmetrical with the ribs (12) of the other of the faces of the strip with respect to the mid-plane of the strip parallel to the faces of this strip. The subject of the invention is also a process and a device for manufacturing this reinforcement.

<CLAIMS>
Reinforcement for reinforcing concrete structures, 
especially concrete slabs or shells, comprising a 

steel tape (10; 10A; 10B; 10C) that can be coiled up, 
intended to be incorporated in the concrete, characterized 

in that the faces of the steel tape each comprise ribs (12) 
arranged in at least one longitudinal row (R) while being 

substantially parallel to one another and inclined at an 
angle α with respect to the longitudinal direction of the 

tape, each row (R) of ribs being laterally bordered by 
smooth longitudinal areas (14) co-planar with the faces of 

the tape. 
Reinforcement according to Claim 1, characterized 
in that the ribs (12) of one of the faces of the tape are 

disposed symmetrically to the ribs (12) of the other face 
of the tape with respect to the median plane (P) of the 

tape parallel to the faces of the tape. 
Reinforcement according to Claim 1 or 2, 
characterized in that the ribs (12) are substantially 

rectilinear. 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
3, characterized in that the angle of inclination α has an 

absolute value of between 45 and 90°. 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
4, characterized in that each face of the steel tape (10; 

10A; 10C) comprises at least two rows (R) of ribs (12) 
arranged side by side over the width of the tape, the ribs 

(12) of these two rows having opposed angles of inclination 
α. 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
5, characterized in that each face of the steel tape (10B; 

10C) comprises at least two rows (R) of ribs (12) arranged 
side by side over the width of the tape, the ribs (12) of  

 
these two rows having identical angles of inclination α. 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
6, characterized in that each face of the steel tape 

comprises an even number of rows (R) of ribs (12), these 
rows (R) being distributed symmetrically with respect to 

the longitudinal median axis (X) of the tape (12). 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
7, characterized in that each face of the steel tape (10; 

10A) comprises at least two rows (R) of ribs (12) arranged 
side by side over the width of the tape, the close ends of 

the ribs (12) of these two rows being offset longitudinally 
with respect to one another. 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
8, characterized in that the ribs (12) within the same row 

(R) are spaced longitudinally from one another at a 
constant pitch of between 3 and 20 mm, preferably between 

5 and 10 mm. 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
9, characterized in that the width of the base of each rib 

(12) is between 1 and 4 mm, preferably approximately 2.5 
mm. 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
10, characterized in that the height of each rib (12) is 

between 0.1 and 0.5 mm. 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
11, characterized in that the cross section of each rib 

(12) is bounded substantially by an isosceles trapezium, 
the non-parallel sides of which form an angle β of approximately 

45° with respect to the base of the trapezium. 
Reinforcement according to any one of Claims 1 to 
12, characterized in that the steel tape comprises an 

aperture (16) and a hook (18) which are arranged at 
opposite ends of the tape, forming means for attachment of 

the tape, especially to another identical tape or to a reel 
for coiling up the tape having complementary attachment 

means.  
 
Reinforcement according to Claim 13, characterized 
in that the aperture (16) is of generally rectangular 

shape and the hook (18) is formed by a slash of generally 
rectangular shape made in the tape. 
Process for the manufacture of a steel tape of a 
reinforcement for reinforcing concrete structures, 

according to any one of Claims 1 to 14, according to which 
process: 


on the faces of a steel sheet or strip are formed 
longitudinal rows (R) of ribs (12) which are inclined with 

respect to a longitudinal axis of the strip, the rows (R) 
being spaced from one another over the width of the strip 

by smooth longitudinal areas (14); and 
the strip is split along selected smooth longitudinal 
areas so as to form tapes (10; 10A; 10B; 10C) of a 

desired width comprising at least one row of ribs (12) on 
each of the faces. 
Process according to Claim 15, characterized in 
that the ribs (12) are formed by passing the steel strip 

continuously between two knurled wheel assemblies, each of 
the assemblies being intended to form ribs (12) on a 

corresponding face of the strip so that the ribs of one 
face are symmetrical to the ribs of the other face with 

respect to the median plane (P) of the strip parallel to 
the faces of the strip. 
Process according to Claim 15 or 16, characterized 
in that the ribs (12) are formed by cold working of a 

steel strip obtained by cold or hot rolling, in such a way 
that after final working the elastic limit of the strip is 

raised, and is preferably above 650 MPa. 
Process according to Claim 15 or 16, characterized 
in that the ribs (12) are hot formed on a steel strip 

with raised elastic limit, preferably above 650 MPa. 
Manufacturing device for the implementation of 
the process according to any one of Claims 15 to 18, of the 

type comprising a frame (20), two parallel notching rolls  
 

(40, 41), each mounted on a drive shaft (26, 27) carried by 
bearings (28, 29), means (37, 42, 43) for adjusting the gap 

between the notching rolls (40, 41), and means for 
synchronising the rotation of the said notching rolls (40, 

41), in which device each notching roll (40, 41) includes, 
on the one hand, an assembly of independent, juxtaposed 

disks (44), mounted on the corresponding drive shaft (26, 

27) and the working faces (44A) of which are provided with 
projecting portions (44B) of matched shape in order to 

produce the said ribs (12) on the faces of the strip and, 
on the other hand, means (45) for the relative positioning 

of the projecting portions (44B) of each disk (44) with 
respect to the adjacent disks according to the distribution 

of the ribs (12) on the faces of the strip, the said disks 
(44) of each assembly being connected to one another and to 

the corresponding drive shaft (26, 27) by connecting means 
(50, 51, 52, 55, 56). 
Device according to Claim 19, characterized in 
that the projecting parts (44B) of each disk (44) are 

inclined at an angle α' with respect to the axis of the 
disk (44). 
Device according to Claims 19 and 20, characterized 
in that the means for relative positioning of the 

projecting portions (44B) of each disk (44) with respect to 
the adjacent disks are formed by transverse openings (45) 

distributed at an angle and radially on the lateral faces 
of each of the disks (44), the axes of the openings (45) 

being parallel to the drive shaft (26, 27) and the openings 
(45) being intended to co-operate with the means for 

connecting the disks (44) to the corresponding drive shaft 
(26, 27). 
Device according to any one of Claims 19 to 21, 
characterized in that the means for connecting the disks 

(44) of each notching roll (40, 41) to the corresponding 
drive shaft (26, 27) are formed, on the one hand, by two 

parallel plates (50, 51), clamping the said disks (44) and,  
 

on the other hand, by tie-rods (52) connecting the two 
plates (50, 51) and passing through the disks (44) by way 

of the openings (45), one of the plates (50, 51) carrying 
the corresponding drive shaft (26, 27) and the other of the 

said plates (50, 51) being traversed by the said drive 
shaft (26, 27). 
Device according to any one of Claims 19 to 21, 
characterized in that the means for connecting the disks 

(44) of each notching roll (40, 41) to the corresponding 
drive shaft (26, 27) are formed, on the one hand, by two 

parallel flanges (55, 56), clamping the said disks (44) and 
rigidly connected in rotation with the said corresponding 

drive shaft (26, 27) and, on the other hand, by tie-rods 
(52) connecting the two flanges (55, 56) and passing 

through the disks (44) by way of the said openings (45). 
Device according to Claim 19, characterized in 
that it includes means (60, 61) for guiding the strip 

upstream and downstream of the notching rolls (40, 41) with 
respect to the direction in which the strip passes through, 

in order to limit the cutting and skid effects of the said 
strip. 
Device according to Claim 19, characterized in 
that it includes means for splitting the strip so as to 

form tapes of a desired width and means for grinding the 
longitudinal edges of the tapes. 
The use of a reinforcement according to any one 
of Claims 1 to 14, for reinforcing roads or runways.
