<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the preparation of modified resins of esterified resin acids.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C08G800	C08G828	C08G1600	C08G1600	C09D1110	C09D1110	C09F100	C09F102	C09F104	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C08G	C08G	C08G	C08G	C09D	C09D	C09F	C09F	C09F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C08G8	C08G8	C08G16	C08G16	C09D11	C09D11	C09F1	C09F1	C09F1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A process for the preparation of low-odour, pale, modified natural resin (rosin) acid esters which are suitable as binder resins in printing inks for offset printing and illustration intaglio printing, by reacting     A) natural resin acids with   B) phenols,   C) aldehydes,   D) polyols in the presence of   E) condensation catalysts, and optionally with F)  alpha , beta -ethylenically unsaturated carboxylic acids or derivatives thereof,   G) fatty acids or fatty acid compounds,   H) ethylenically unsaturated monomers or   I) ethylenically unsaturated hydrocarbon resins, characterised in that the water formed during the preparation of these modified natural resin acid esters from a reaction temperature of 200 DEG C is removed continuously by distillation at a temperature of up to 300 DEG C in vacuo at a pressure of from 0.1 to 1000 mbar.
</ABSTRACT>
