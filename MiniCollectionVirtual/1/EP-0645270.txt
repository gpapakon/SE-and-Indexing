<CLAIMS>
A wind deflector for a passenger vehicle provided with a folding hood, wherein the wind 
deflector is displaceable from a position of non-use to an operative position and vice 

versa, 
characterised in that
 the wind deflector (22) is linked to the hood (3) by way of 
control means (31) in such a manner that when the hood (3) is folded back into the rear 

storage position (B), the wind deflector (22) is automatically moved into the operative 
position (E) thereof. 
A wind deflector according to Claim 1, 
characterised in that
 the control means (31) is 
mechanical, hydraulic, electric, electronic or of similar form . 
A wind deflector according to Claims 1 and 2, 
characterised in that
 the control 
means (31) is formed by means of two guide rods (32, 33) rotatably connected to each 

other, wherein the first guide rod (32) is rigidly attached to the wind deflector (22) and 
the second guide rod (33) is rotatably connected to the hood linkage (5) and flexibly 

connected to the first guide rod (32). 
A wind deflector according to any one of Claims 1 to 3, 
characterised in that
 the 
control means (31) is only provided on one longitudinal side of the hood (3) between the 

wind deflector (22) and the hood (3). 
A wind deflector according to any one of Claims 1 to 4, 
characterised in that
 the first 
guide rod (32), in plan view, extends substantially at right angles from a horizontal, 

transversely extending region (34) of the bearing journal (26) of the wind deflector (22). 
A wind deflector according to any one of Claims 1 to 5, 
characterised in that
 the 
second guide rod (33) co-operates by way of a plug connection (39) with a lever arm (10) 

of an angled lever (11) of the hood linkage (5). 
A wind deflector according to Claim 6, 
characterised in that
 the plug connection (39) is 
formed by means of an outwardly-extending pin (40) positioned on the second guide 

rod (33) and inserted into a corresponding opening (41) of the angled lever (11).  
 
A wind deflector according to any one of the preceding claims, 
characterised in that
 the 
wind deflector (22) co-operates with the bearing brackets (29) on the hood by way of 

easily detachable plug connections. 
</CLAIMS>
