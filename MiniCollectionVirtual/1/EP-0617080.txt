<INVENTION-TITLE>
Use of y-hydroxy-alkylated silicone oils as additive for the improvement of the optical properties in compositions based on chlorinated polymers.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C08G7700	C08G7714	C08G7722	C08K500	C08K55419	C08L2700	C08L2704	C08L8700	C08L8700	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C08G	C08G	C08G	C08K	C08K	C08L	C08L	C08L	C08L	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C08G77	C08G77	C08G77	C08K5	C08K5	C08L27	C08L27	C08L87	C08L87	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Use, as additive for improving optical properties (transparency or gloss), of a  gamma -hydroxyalkylated polydiorganosiloxane oil containing at least one  gamma -hydroxyalkyl functional group per molecule, with a dynamic viscosity at 25@C of the order of 20 to 2,000 mPa s, in compositions containing     - 100 parts by weight of a chlorine-containing polymer,   - from 0.005 to 2 parts by weight of the said silicone oil,   - an effective quantity of at least one heat stabiliser,   - from 1 to 20 parts by weight of an impact-improving elastomer.   The said compositions may be converted by blow-extrusion, calendering, extrusion, injection moulding, rotational moulding etc., into articles with improved transparency or gloss.
</ABSTRACT>
