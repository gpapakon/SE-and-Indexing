<CLAIMS>
Arrangement for securing a built-in hob, in
particular a glass ceramic hob, in a hob mounting clearance

provided for this purpose, having a hob housing,
which bears the heating devices and can be fastened in

the hob mounting from above via retaining means, and
having a hob top part, comprising a surround frame with

cooking vessels, which can be positioned on the hob
housing from above and can be secured in a latchable

manner via clamping-type connecting means, characterized
in that the retaining means (3) for fastening the hob

housing (2) in the hob mounting (1) has a section which
is designed as clamping-type connecting means and, when

the hob top part (4,5,6) is positioned on the hob housing
(2), can be brought into engagement with the corresponding

clamping-type connecting means (8) of the hob top
part (4,5,6).
Arrangement according to Claim 1, characterized
in that that section of the retaining means (3) which is

designed as a clamping-type connecting means projects
into the hob housing (2).
Arrangement according to Claim 1 or 2, characterized
in that the corresponding clamping-type connecting

means (3,8) each comprise a two-legged spring (8) and a
round-pin-like part which can be brought into engagement

therewith.
Arrangement according to Claim 3, characterized
in that the two-legged springs (8) are seated, in a

manner known per se, all the way around the outer border
of the hob top part (4,5), and the corresponding round-pin-like 

parts are formed by cylindrical extended sections
which belong to the retaining means (3) and project

horizontally into the interior of the hob housing (2).
Arrangement according to Claim 4, characterized
in that the retaining means (3) are screws (3a), the

cylindrical extended section being formed by the extended
screw heads which run along the longitudinal axis.
Arrangement according to Claim 4, characterized
in that the retaining means (3) are screws (3a), the

cylindrical extended section being formed by a sleeve
(3b) which is drawn onto the screw shank.
Arrangement for securing a built-in hob, in
particular a glass ceramic hob, in a hob mounting clearance

provided for this purpose, having a hob housing,
which bears the heating devices and can be fastened in

the hob mounting from above via retaining means, and
having a hob top part, comprising a surround frame with

cooking surface, which can be positioned on the hob
housing from above and can be secured in a latchable

manner via clamping-type connecting means, characterized
in that the hob housing (2) has openings, and the retaining

means (3) for fastening the hob housing (2) in the
hob mounting (1) engage through these openings, and in

that guide sleeves (3c) for the retaining means (3) are
fitted at these openings and, when the hob top part

(4,5,6) is positioned on the hob housing (2), can be
brought into engagement with corresponding two-legged

springs (8) which are fitted all the way around the hob
top part.
Arrangement according to Claim 7, characterized
in that the guide sleeves (3c) are arranged so as to be

oriented outwards from the hob housing (2), the length of
the sleeves (3c) being such that they serve, at the same

time, as spacers between the hob housing (2) and the hob
mounting (1).
Arrangement according to at least one of Claims
1 to 8, characterized in that the corresponding clamping-type

connecting means (3,8) or (3c,8) are arranged so as
to be displaceable relative to one another along the side 

edges of the hob mounting (1).
Arrangement according to Claim 9, characterized
in that the two-legged springs (8) are arranged on the

hob top part (4,5) so as to be displaceable along the
side edges of the hob mounting (1) via guides (8a).
Arrangement according to at least one of Claims
1 to 10, characterized in that the surround frame of the

hob top parts comprises, in a manner known per se, a
visible frame (4) and a retaining frame (5).
Arrangement according to Claim 11, characterized
in that the visible frame (4) can be positioned on the

hob housing (2) with retaining frame (5) from above and
can be secured via the clamping-type connecting means

(3,8) or (3c,8).
Arrangement according to Claim 11, characterized

in that the visible frame (4), which grips over the
cooking surface (6), and the retaining frame (5) with the

clamping-type connecting means (8), are connected
releasably to one another via suitable fastening means

(4b) and clamp the cooking surface (6) together with the
sealing profile (7).
