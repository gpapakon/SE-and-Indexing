<INVENTION-TITLE>
Insole in sandwich form
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
An insole design for shoes of the California type is characterised in that the insole design comprises at least two layers of material which are joined together, the lower layer having a projection which is at least 1 mm wide and serves for sewing on the shaft. The insole design is particularly suitable for producing shoes of the California type with a slip-lasted insole and for imitation moccasin shoes with a sewn insole design or slip-lasted insole design.

<CLAIMS>
An insole structure for California type shoes, 
characterised in that the insole structure comprises 

at least two material layers which are connected to 
one another, the lower layer having an overlap of at 

least 1 mm in width used for stitching to the shaft. 
An insole structure according to claim 1, 
characterised in that the overlap has a width of at 

least 2 mm. 
An insole structure according to one of claims 1 or 2, 
characterised in that the lower layer is a textile 

material. 
An insole structure according to one or more of claims 
1 to 3, characterised in that the upper layer is made 

of leather or imitation leather. 
An insole structure according to one or more of claims 
1 to 4, characterised in that the lower layer is made 

of waterproof material. 
An insole structure according to one of claims 1 to 5, 
characterised in that the upper layer has a multilayer 

structure. 
The use of the insole structure according to one of 
claims 1 to 6, for manufacturing an imitation moccasin 

shoe with a sewn insole structure. 
The use of the insole structure according to one of 
claims 1 to 6, for manufacturing an imitation moccasin 

shoe with a stitched-on insole structure.  
 
The use of the insole structure according to one of 
claims 1 to 6, for manufacturing California type shoes 

with a stitched-on insole. 
A California type shoe with an insole structure 
according to one or more of claims 1 to 6, an upper 

material and a lining located inside the upper 
material, characterised in that the lining comprises a 

waterproof film. 
A shoe according to claim 10, characterised in that 

the film of the lining is water vapour permeable. 
</CLAIMS>
