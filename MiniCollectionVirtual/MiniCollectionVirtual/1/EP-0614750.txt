<INVENTION-TITLE>
Method for manufacturing thick moulded articles by pressing fibrous material, and moulded article obtained by this method.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B27N304	B27N304	B29C7008	B29C7008	C08J512	C08J512	E04C210	E04C216	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B27N	B27N	B29C	B29C	C08J	C08J	E04C	E04C	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B27N3	B27N3	B29C70	B29C70	C08J5	C08J5	E04C2	E04C2	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
For manufacturing a relatively thick moulded article, first of all a plurality of flat mouldings are produced by mixing a fibrous material with an adhesive and keeping it in a mould under superatmospheric pressure and elevated temperature until the adhesive sets. Subsequently, the flat mouldings are pressed, with a cold-acting adhesive being inserted, to form the finished, multilayered moulded article.
</ABSTRACT>
