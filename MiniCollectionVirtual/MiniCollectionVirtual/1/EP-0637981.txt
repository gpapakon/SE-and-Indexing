<CLAIMS>
An equipment for gliding sport over snow of the 
kind constituted by a single board having a turned up tip 

(2) and by bindings (6, 7, 135, 136) for interlocking 
shoes with said board, the bindings (6, 7, 135, 136) do 

not allow any degree of freedom to the shoes in relation 
to the board, characterized in that it is constituted by 

a board (1) whose width, at its center, is between 105 
millimeters and 150 millimeters, said board (1) 

comprising a first binding (6, 120, 121) for positioning 
the shoe (135) on the board (1) in a position in which 

its longitudinal axis (8) makes with the median 
longitudinal axis (5) of the board (1), an angle between 

0 degree and 20 degrees and a second binding (7, 122, 
123), longitudinally shifted in relation to the first 

binding, for interlocking the second shoe (136) on the 
board (1) in a position in which its longitudinal axis 

(9) makes with the median longitudinal axis (5) of the 
board (1), an angle between 0 degree and 20 degrees. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 1, characterized in that the distance, 

measured on the median longitudinal axis (5) of the 
board, between the line perpendicular to the median 

longitudinal axis of the board going through the front 
end of the most backward shoe and the line perpandicular 

to the median longitudinal axis of the board going 
through the rear end of the second shoe is at the most 

equal to 100 millimeters. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 2, characterized in that said bindings 

(6, 7, 120, 121, 122, 123) are disposed such that the 
transverse line perpendicular to the median longitudinal  

 
axis (5) of the board (1) going through the rear end on 

the binding (6, 122) for the most advanced shoe (136) is 
behind the transverse line perpendicular to the median 

longitudinal axis, going through the front end of the 
second binding (7, 121) for the most backward shoe (135). 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 3, characterized in that the bindings 

(6, 7, 120, 121, 122, 123) are disposed according to a 
longitudinal shifting in order that the line 

perpendicular to the median longitudinal axis (5) of the 
board going through the front end of the most backward 

binding is before the line perpendicular to the median 
longitudinal axis of the board going through the rear end 

of the second binding, the distance between the two 
perpendicular lines being between 0 and 100 millimeters. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 1 or 2, characterized in that the 

middle of each of the bindings is placed on the median 
longitudinal axis of the board, the bindings (6, 7) being 

parallel and placed one behind the other in order that 
the point of the front binding (6) reachs the level of 

one of the lateral side of the board, and the heel of the 
rear binding reachs the level of the opposite lateral 

side of the board, the median longitudinal axis of the 
two bindings (6, 7) being parallel and making with the 

median longitudinal axis (5) of the board an angle of 
about 7Â°. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 1 or 2, characterized in that the 

point of the front binding (6) and the heel of the rear 
binding (7) are disposed on the median longitudinal axis 

(5) of the board, the point of the rear binding (7) and  
 

the heel of the front binding (6) being laterally shifted 
on each side of the median longitudinal axis (5). 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to any one of claims 1, 2, 3 or 5, 

characterized in that the bindings (6, 7, 120, 121, 122, 
123) are disposed symetrically in relation to an oblique 

axis (150) making an angle between 4 degrees ans 16 
degrees with the longitudinal axis (5) of the board. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to any of the above claims, characterized in 

that the width of the board (1) at its center is of the 
order of 140 millimiters. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to any one of the above claims, characterized 

in that the part of the board (1) backward the tip (2) 

and the front part of the heel (3) is laterally delimited 
on both side by concave sides. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 9, characterized in that the edge 

between the concave side and the beginning of the tip (2) 
is straight. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to any one of claims 7 to 9, characterized in 

that the central hollow of the board is between 1 and 3 
centimeters. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to any one of the above claims, characterized 

in that the equipment according to the invention 
comprises a linking plate (100) comprising two pairs of  

 
bindings (120 to 123) for shoes (135,136), the board (1) 

comprising two safety bindings (101, 102) longitudinally 
oriented and disposed on the median axis (5) for 

interlocking the linking plate (100) with the board (1). 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 11, characterized in that the linking 

plate (100) is constituted by a plate having front (103) 
and rear (104) ends proper to cooperate with safety 

bindings (101, 102) of known kind, said linking plate 
comprising : 


a rear wedge (130) of a thickness between 5 
and 30 millimeters, said rear wedge (130) being fastened 

on the linking plate (100) in an angularly adjustable 
way, 
a front wedge (133) of a thickness between 5 
and 30 millimeters, said front wedge (133) being fastened 

on the linking plate (100) in an angularly adjustable 
way, 
two median wedges (131, 132) of a thickness 
between 0 and 20 millimeters, said central wedges (131, 

132) being fastened on the linking plate (100) in an 
laterally adjustable way. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to any one of the claims 1 to 11, characterized 

in that it comprises a rear stop (101) and a front stop 
(102), an well as a median set (260, 270) comprising two 

holding elements (261, 271, 262, 272) mechanically 
coupled and movable between a rest position in which they 

come into contact with the point of the most backward 
shoe and the heel of the most advanced shoe respectively, 

and make with the median longitudinal axis (5) of the 
board, an angle corresponding to the angle made between 

the longitudinal axis of the stops (101, 102) and the  
 

median axis (5) of the board, and a safety position in 
which said holding elements (261, 262) allow the take-off 

of the shoes. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 13, characterized in that it comprises 

a rear stop (101) and a front stop (102), as well as a 
median set (260) constituted by a longitudinal guide rail 

(263) and two holding elements (261,262) longitudinaly 
movable, said holding elements (261,262) being kept apart 

by a calibrated spring (264). 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 13, characterized in that it comprises 

a rear stop (101) and a front stop (102), as well as a 
median set (270) constituted by a fixed plate and by two 

holding elements (271,272) revolving on two axis 
(276,277) firmly attached of the fixed plate (273) and 

approximately perpendicular to the surface of the board 
(1). 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to claim 15, characterized in that the both 

intermediate holding elements (271,272) are mechanically 
coupled by geared toothed sectors (277,278) and in that a 

return spring (280) positions the intermediate holding 
elements (271,272) according to opposite angles 

corresponding to the angles made between the longitudinal 
axis of the stops (101, 102) and the median axis (5) of 

the board. 
An equipment for gliding sport over snow 
according to any one of the claims 13 to 16, 

characterized in that the front (101) and rear (102) 
stops make with the median longitudinal axis, an angle of  

 
approximately 7 degrees, and in that the guide rail (263) 

is disposed on the median longitudinal axis (5).
