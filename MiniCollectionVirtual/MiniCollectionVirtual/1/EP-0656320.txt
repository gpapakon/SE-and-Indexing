<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for removal of organic solvents from waste water
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C02F126	B01D1104	C02F126	B01D1104	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C02F	B01D	C02F	B01D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C02F1	B01D11	C02F1	B01D11	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Waste waters which occur in the production or use of paints containing organic solvents are cleaned up by extraction with higher monoalcohols, that is alcohols having 8 or more carbon atoms in the molecule.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A process for the purification of wastewaters from
the production and use of solvent-containing

paints, which comprises extracting the wastewaters
with monoalcohols which contain 8 to 16 carbon

atoms in the molecule.
The process as claimed in claim 1, wherein the
wastewaters are extracted with 2-ethylhexanol.
