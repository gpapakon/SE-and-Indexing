<ABSTRACT>
Novel food products such as bread, coffee, noodles and pasta, etc. containing benzimidazole and/or at least one of its derivatives, and methods of preparing or processing said products, are disclosed.

<CLAIMS>
Use of benzimidazole and/or at least one of its derivatives as an
agent promoting assimilation by the organism of a food product having an

acid character which, when ingested is liable to cause stimulation and
hypersecretion of the gastric and/or duodenum mucosae.
Use according to claim 1, characterized in that the aforesaid
benzimidazole derivative has the following chemical formula:



in which

X and Y independently represent an alkyl group, notably methyl, a phenyl
group or an halogen atom; and
n = 1 to 4.
Use according to claim 1 or 2, characterized in that the aforesaid
food product having an acid character is contained in food products for

children or newborn babies.
Use according to claim 3, characterized in that the aforesaid food
product having an acid character consists in milk, in powder form if

appropriate, for infants.
Use according to claim 1 or 2, characterized in that the aforesaid
food product having an acid character is selected from pasta, bread,

sauces, jam, marmelade, jellies, sweets, icecreams, chocolate, desserts,
biscuits, coffee, tea, sausages, oils, a drink such as an alcoholic drink, a

fruit juice, a gaseous or non gaseous mineral water, beer.
Use according to one of claims 1, 2, 3, 5, characterized in that
the aforesaid food product is bread and in that it contains, per 100 grams,

from 15 to 25 mg of benzimidazole and/or at least one of its aforesaid
derivatives.
Use according to one of claims 1 to 3, 5, characterized in that the
aforesaid food product is coffee and in that it contains, per 1 kilogram, about

500 mg of benzimidazole and/or at least one of its aforesaid derivatives.
Use according to one of claims 1 to 3, 5, characterized in that the
aforesaid food product is a pasta and in that it contains, per 1 kilogram, 

about 150 mg of benzimidazole and/or at least one of its aforesaid
derivatives.
Use according to one of claims 1 to 3, 5, characterized in that the
aforesaid food product is a sauce and in that it contains, per 100 g, from 50

to 60 mg of benzimidazole and/or at least one of its aforesaid derivatives.
Use according to one of claims 1 to 3, 5, characterized in that the
aforesaid food product is a jam and in that it contains, per 1 kilogram, about

100 mg of benzimidazole and/or at least one of its aforesaid derivatives.
Method for treating a food product having an acid character
which, when ingested is liable to cause stimulation and hypersecretion of

the gastric and/or duodenum mucosae, for promoting its assimilation by the
organism, said method consisting in including during the preparation of said

product and/or in adding to said prepared product, an efficient amount of
benzimidazole and/or at least one of its derivatives, as defined in any one of

claims 2 to 10.
</CLAIMS>
