<INVENTION-TITLE>
Holding device for supported upright standing base soap dispensing sachet or the like
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A47K512	A47K500	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A47K	A47K	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A47K5	A47K5	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A holding device which makes it possible to use supported upright standing base sachets (bags) in conventional dispensers for storage bottles made of plastic. A preferably rectangular plate (1) is adapted in its size to the base of the sachet (5) to be held and at its corners has holding hooks (2a-2d) which are received by punched holes (6a-6d) in the region of the peripheral base weld seam (8) of the sachet. A handle (4) on the holding plate (1) enables secure handling with the filled sachet.

<CLAIMS>
Holding device for a supported upright standing base soap dispending
sachet or the like equipped with a dispenser casing for a

storage bottle, with a pump immersing into the storage bottle
and with an operational unit 
characterized
 by a holding plate
(1) of preferably rectangular shape showing at its edge holding

means (2a-2d) and adapted in its size to the bottom of the
supported upright standing base sachet (5) to be hold in such a

way that the sachet (5) can be fixed in the region of the
surrounding bottom welding seam (8) at the provided

holding means (2a-2d) whereby in the middle of the holding
plate (1) an opening (3) equipped with clamping means

(3a) is provided the size of which corresponds to the diameter of
the pump cylinder (7) which is enclosed by the clamping

means (3a).
Holding device according to claim 1 
characterized in that
 the
supported upright standing base sachet (5) shows punching holes

(6a-6d) in the surrounding bottom welding seam (8).
Holding device according to one of the preceding claims 
characterized
in that
 a handle (4) is attached on it.
Holding device according to one of the preceding claims 
characterized
in that
 the opening (3) is enlarged at two opposite points
by recesses forming a straight slot (9) which goes right through

the middle of the opening (3).
