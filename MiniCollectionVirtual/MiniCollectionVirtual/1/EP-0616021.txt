<INVENTION-TITLE>
Use of fire-proof gels for fire-resistant glazings.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B32B1706	B32B1710	C09K2100	C09K2104	C09K2112	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B32B	B32B	C09K	C09K	C09K	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B32B17	B32B17	C09K21	C09K21	C09K21	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Fire protection gels (fireproofing gels) based on amine salts of acidic phosphates, characterised in that they comprise an aluminium component, a phosphorus component, an amine component and, if desired, water in ratios such that at from 5 to 30@C they form for more than 24 hours a flowable mixture which on being heated at temperatures above 45@C is transformed in less than 24 hours into a transparent gel, are used for fire protection glazing (fireproof glass).
</ABSTRACT>
