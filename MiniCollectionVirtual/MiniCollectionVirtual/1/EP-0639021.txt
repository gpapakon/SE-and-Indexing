<INVENTION-TITLE>
Circuit arrangement for a transformerless subscriber line interface circuit.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>H04M1900	H04M1900	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>H04M	H04M	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>H04M19	H04M19	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A circuit arrangement for a transformerless physical interface for feeding power to telecommunication terminals is used for simultaneously transmitting signals in both directions on a two-wire copper line. The signals are coupled into and out of an interface chip via capacitors. If the circuit arrangement is unbalanced, an electronic common-emitter transistor choke (D) is provided and if the circuit arrangement is balanced, two electronic transistor chokes (D1, D2) are configured in a complementary circuit. Capacitors (C1, C2) of the electronic transistor chokes (D1, D2) are used for forming a high impedance with respect to the coupling-in and -out of the signals.
