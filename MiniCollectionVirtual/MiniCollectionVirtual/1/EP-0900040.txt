<INVENTION-TITLE>
SNIFTER FOR ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES SUCH AS BRANDY, SPIRITS AND LIQUEURS
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
A snifter for alcoholic beverages such as brandy, spirits and liqueurs is disclosed. The glass includes a softly flaring vessel (1), with a parison (2) extending therefrom and including two parts, a first lower part (20) flaring more sharply, extended by a second, higher part (21) gradually tapering up to the drinking edge (22), the opening of the latter defining a surface equal to or larger than that defined by the upper edge (10) of said vessel (1), which is the recommended filling limit.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Glass for tasting alcohols such as brandies, spirits and
liquors, including a slowly widening container (1) on the upper

edge (10) of which begins a parison (2) that includes two

portions, a first portion (20) of a small height, which widens
to a larger extent, extended by a second portion (21) of a large

height, which progressively narrows towards the lip (22) the
opening of which delimits a surface at least equal to the one

delimited by the upper edge (10) of said container (1),
characterized in that said upper edge (10) of this container (1)

forms the recommended filling limit, whereas the volume of the
parison (2) is larger than that of this container (1).
Glass according to claim 1, characterized in that the
surface area ratio is comprised between 1 and 3.5.
Glass according to claim 1 or claim 2, characterized in
that the upper portion of the parison (2) includes, on its whole

periphery, at the level of the lip (22), a re-entrant rim (23)
of a concave shape.
Glass according to any of the preceding claims,
characterized in that it includes, at the lower side, under the

container (1), a solid portion (3) of a certain height aimed at
seizing it.
Glass according to any of claims 1, 2 or 3,
characterized in that it includes, at the lower side, under the

container (1), a stem (40) and a foot (4).
Glass according to any of the preceding claims,
characterized in that it is made of mouth-blown crystal or

crystalline glass, of glass or any other crystalline material.
</CLAIMS>
