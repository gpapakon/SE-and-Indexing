<ABSTRACT>
DNA sequences comprising all or part of the (K. lactis) pyruvate-decarboxylase promoter gene or a derivative thereof, and having transcriptional promoter activity. The invention also relates to the use of said sequences for the expression of recombinant genes.

<CLAIMS>
DNA fragment comprising the sequence
SEQ ID No. 1, its complementary strand, or a derivative

of these, and having a transcriptional promoter activity.
Recombinant DNA comprising a DNA sequence
according to claim 1.
Recombinant DNA according to claim 2,
characterized in that it additionally contains one or

more structural genes.
Recombinant DNA according to claim 3,
characterized in that it also contains signals enabling

the secretion of the expression product or of the said
structural gene(s).
Recombinant DNA according to claims 3 and 4,
char
acterized in that the structural gene(s) encode
proteins of pharmaceutical or agrifood interest.
Recombinant DNA according to claim 5,
characterized in that the structural gene encodes serum

albumin.
Recombinant DNA according to any one of claims 2
to 6, characterized in that it is part of an expression

plasmid which can be of autonomous or integrative
replication.
Recombinant cell containing a DNA sequence or a
recombinant DNA according to any one of the preceding

claims.
Recombinant cell according to claim 8,
characterized in that it is a yeast.
Recombinant cell according to claim 9,
characterized in that it is a yeast of the genus


Kluyveromyces.
Use of a DNA sequence according to any one of
claims 1 to 7 for the expression of recombinant genes.
Use according to claim 11 for the expression of
genes encoding proteins of pharmaceutical or agrifood

interest.
Process for the production of recombinant
proteins, characterized in that a recombinant cell

according to any one of claims 8 to 10 is cultured and
the proteins produced are recovered.
Process according to claim 13 for the production
of proteins of pharmaceutical or agrifood interest.
Process according to claim 14, characterized in
that the protein is human serum albumim or one of its

molecular variants.
