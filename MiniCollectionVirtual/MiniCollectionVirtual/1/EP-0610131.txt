<INVENTION-TITLE>
Feed through connection for superconducting coil
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>H01F606	H01F606	</CLASSIFICATIONS>

<ABSTRACT>
The subject of the present invention is a connection for the electrical feed to a transposed superconducting coil, the said coil being placed in a cryostat and comprising a plurality of superconducting conductors identical to each other, the accessible ends of which are uniformly distributed in cylindrical symmetry, the said connection consisting of two current intakes consisting of a plurality of conductors which are identical to each other comprising a first end and a second end, and being characterised in that the number of the said conductors is identical for the two inlets and equal to the number of the said superconducting conductors of the said coil, in that the said conductors of the said inlets are arranged regularly and parallel along the said cylindrical symmetry with respect to the axis of the said coil, in that the said inlets are arranged coaxially, and in that the said first end of the said conductors of the said inlets is linked to the said superconducting conductors.

<CLAIMS>
A connection for feeding electricity to a transposed superconductive 
coil (1), said coil (1) being placed in a cryostat (4) and 

comprising a plurality of identical superconductive conductors 
(101..., 104), the free ends of which are distributed uniformly with 

cylindrical symmetry, said connection being constituted by two 
current leads each composed of a plurality of identical lead 

conductors (2, 3) each comprising a first end and a second end, and 
being charac
terized in that the number of said conductors (2, 3) is 
identical in each lead and equal to the number of said superconductive 

conductors (101..., 104) in said coil, in that said 
conductors (2, 3) of said leads are disposed regularly and in 

parallel with said cylindrical symmetry about the axis (105) of said 
coil (1), in that said leads are disposed coaxially and in that said 

first end of each said lead conductor is connected to a respective 
said superconductive conductor (101..., 104). 
A connection according to claim 1, characterized in that said 
conductors (2, 3) of each current lead are connected to said superconductive 

conductors (101..., 104) of said coil (1) by soldering or 
by magneto-forming. 
A connection according to either of the preceding claims, 
characterized in that said second end of each said conductor (2, 3) 

of each said current lead is secured to a cylindrical conductive part 
(17, 18). 
A connection according to claim 3, characterized in that said 
cylindrical conductive part (17, 18) is of copper. 
A connection according to any preceding claim, characterized in 
that each said conductor (2, 3) of said current leads is individually 

placed within an electrically insulating tube (13, 14). 
A connection according to claim 5, characterized in that 
circulation of cryogenic fluid vapor (6) ensures that each said  

 
conductor (2, 3) of said current leads is cooled inside said 

insulating tube (13, 14). 
A connection according to any of claims 1 to 4, characterized in 
that said conductors (2, 3) of each said lead are placed within the 

double wall of an electrically insulating tube (406, 407), the two 
tubes being disposed coaxially. 
A connection according to claim 7, characterized in that 
circulation of the cryogenic fluid vapor (6) ensures cooling of said 

conductors (2, 3) of said current leads inside the double wall of 
each said insulating tube (406, 407). 
A connection according to any preceding claim, characterized in 
that said conductors (2, 3) of said current leads are of the 

assembled type comprising a plurality of strands constituted by 
copper filaments in a cupro-nickel matrix. 
A connection according to either of claims 5 or 6, characterized 
in that said conductors (2, 3) are of the hybrid type comprising, in 

their low temperature portion, a superconductive element of high 
critical temperature. 
Application of the connection according to any preceding claim 
to an apparatus comprising a superconductive coil (1) placed in a 

cryostat (4), characterized in that said cryostat (4) comprises an 
external wall (7) and a metal internal wall (8) maintained at the 

high voltage, said walls (7, 8) being separated by a vacuum (9). 
Application according to claim 11, characterized in that said 
external wall (7) of said cryostat (4) is constituted by an 

electrically insulating material. 
Application according to claim 12, characterized in that said 
external wall (7) of the cryostat bears ribs (11) of an insulating 

material.  
 
Application according to claim 11, characterized in that said 
external wall (30) of the cryostat (302) is made of metal. 
Application according to claim 14, characterized in that said 
walls of said cryostat are also insulated from each other by an 

insulating part (303) comprising anti-coronas. 
Application according to claim 15, characterized in that said 
part of insulating material (303) also comprises ribs of an isolating 

material. 
Application according to claim 11, characterized in that a solid 
electrical insulator is included between the conductors in the hot 

zone of said cryostat. 
</CLAIMS>
