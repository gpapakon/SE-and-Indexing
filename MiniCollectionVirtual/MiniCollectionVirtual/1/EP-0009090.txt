<INVENTION-TITLE>
PROCESS FOR THE PURIFICATION OF VITAMIN A
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C07C40300	C07C6700	C07C6700	C07C40100	C07C40300	C07C40312	C07C40100	C07C40308	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C07C	C07C	C07C	C07C	C07C	C07C	C07C	C07C	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C07C403	C07C67	C07C67	C07C401	C07C403	C07C403	C07C401	C07C403	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
1. A process for removing free fatty acids of 8 to 20 carbon atoms from esters of vitamin A alcohol with these fatty acids, characterized in that the free fatty acis are extracted with a mixture of an aqueous alkali and one or more of the alcohols methanol, ethanol, isopropanol and 1,2-propylene glycol at a temperature of from 0 degrees C to the boiling point of the alcohol used.
</ABSTRACT>
