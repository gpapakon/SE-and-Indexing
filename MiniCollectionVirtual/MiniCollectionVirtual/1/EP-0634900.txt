<INVENTION-TITLE>
SPORTS SHOE
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A43B504	A43B504	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A43B	A43B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A43B5	A43B5	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A sports shoe with fastening units (8, 9) for attaching the shoe to a sports apparatus movable on the ground such as skis, roller skates or the like. A characteristic line (7) formed by the line connecting the middle part of the heel (3) and the middle part (5) of the sole arch (6) accommodating the big toe is parallel to the direction of movement (BR) of the ski.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Sports shoe with anchoring devices which, for example, make 
engagement with a ski binding possible, for binding the shoe to an 

article of sports equipment moveable on the ground, upon which the 
owner stands and which he steers with the feet, such as skis, roller 

skates or the like, wherein a characteristic line which forms the line 
connecting from the centre of the heel to the centre of the arch of 

the foot, which includes the big toe, runs at least approximately 
parallel, preferably exactly parallel to the direction of movement 

(BR) of the sports equipment, for example of the ski (1), 
characterised in that the sole is deeper at the outside edge (13') 

than at the inside edge (13''). 
Sports shoe according to claim 1, characterised in that the heel 
supporting surface of the sole is 3 to 5 mm deeper at the outside 

edge (13') than at the inside edge (13''). 
Sports shoe according to claim 1 or 2, characterised in that the heel 
supporting surface (13) of the insole is between 20 to 40 mm, 

preferably between 25 to 30 mm, higher than the ball supporting 
surface (14) of the sole in the case of a size 42 shoe. 
</CLAIMS>
