<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for treating the surface of metallic parts.
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
According to this surface treatment process, a thin film of chromium or of chromium alloy, of thickness not exceeding approximately 1  mu m, is vacuum-deposited on at least one face of the zinc-coated metal article. The vacuum-deposition can be performed chemically or physically; advantageously, simultaneously with the deposition of the thin chromium or chromium alloy film, an ionic bombardment of the face of the zinc-coated metal article is carried out in a gas or a gas mixture made of at least one inert gas and/or at least one reactive gas. This process improves the corrosion resistance of the zinc with a view to a subsequent deposition, such as placing the article in paint, while eliminating any environmental pollution by virtue of the removal of the chromate ions from the surrounding atmosphere.
</ABSTRACT>
