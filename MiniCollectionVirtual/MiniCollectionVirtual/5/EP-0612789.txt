<INVENTION-TITLE>
Production process of soft, solid, biodegradable films and products obtained.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C08B3700	C08B3700	C08J518	C08J518	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C08B	C08B	C08J	C08J	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C08B37	C08B37	C08J5	C08J5	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Process for the manufacture of biodegradable, solid, flexible films usable in particular for producing packaging or packages or the protection of surfaces or of products. The process consists in employing a vegetable matter containing pith, such as sorghum, kenaf or corn cob, in extracting hemicelluloses, in particular hemicelluloses B therefrom, in dissolving them, in spreading the solution onto a surface with a view to forming a liquid layer and in performing drying of the said layer with a view to forming the solid film. The latter is characterised by a degree of internal hydration of between 10 % and 25 %, a solubility in water higher than 200 g/l and a tensile mechanical strength of between 15 and 40 MPa.
</ABSTRACT>
