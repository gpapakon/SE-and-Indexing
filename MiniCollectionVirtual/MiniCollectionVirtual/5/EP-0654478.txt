<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the preparation of a mixture of alphar and beta chloroethyl glucopyranose.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C07H100	C07H100	C07H1500	C07H1504	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C07H	C07H	C07H	C07H	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C07H1	C07H1	C07H15	C07H15	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Process for the preparation of a mixture, essentially consisting of alpha - and ss-chloroethylglucopyranose, by reaction of chloroethanol with glucose, 1 mol of glucose being predissolved in 2 to 10 mol of chloroethanol at 100 to 130 DEG C in the absence of catalysts, it optionally being possible to remove water by distillation, and 1 to 30% by weight of acidic catalyst, preferably acidic inorganic and/or organic ion exchangers, based on glucose employed, then being added at 60 to 90 DEG C and the mixture being stirred for 10 to 600 minutes.
</ABSTRACT>
