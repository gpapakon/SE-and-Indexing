<INVENTION-TITLE>
HEATING UNIT FOR OIL OR GAS BOILER AND MODULES FOR PRODUCING SAME
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
The invention concerns a heating unit for oil or gas boiler, wherein said heating unit (3) comprises a case (5) lined internally, in a rear zone, with a shell (6), ring-shaped transverse fins (35) are provided between the shell (6) and the case (5) for defining transverse annular channels (36) wherein the fuel gases follow in parallel a transverse path before emerging from the heating unit (3), thereby improving heat exchange between the fuel gases and the water (22) they are meant to heat.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A heating body (3, 103) for an oil or gas boiler (1) comprising:

a jacket (5, 105) having a longitudinal axis (4) suitable for co-operating
with a burner (10) emitting a flame in a determined direction (7)

along said axis (4) and having an open rear transverse end (8, 108) for
receiving the burner (10), a front transverse end (11) closed by a transverse

wall (12), and a periphery (13) closed by a longitudinal wall (14, 128) which is
at least approximately circularly cylindrical about said axis (4) so as to define

a longitudinal hearth (15) which is blind in a front zone (16) of the jacket (5,
105);
means (9, 19, 20, 109, 120, 128, 129) for defining a chamber (21,
121) for circulating water (22) in contact with said transverse and longitudinal

walls (12; 14, 128), on the outside of the jacket (5, 105);
a longitudinal shell (6) that is at least approximately circularly
cylindrical about said axis (4) placed inside a rear zone (17) of the jacket (5,

105) so as to define the hearth (15) therein and so as to co-operate with said
longitudinal wall (14, 128) to define therein an annular transit chamber (18,

118) for combustion gases;
inlet means (40, 140) for admitting the combustion gases into the
transit chamber (18, 118) from the hearth (15);
outlet means (41, 141) for exhausting the combustion gases from the
transit chamber (18, 118) to an exhaust (53); and
fins (35, 135) disposed in the transit chamber (18, 118) so as to co-operate
with said longitudinal wall (14, 128) and with the shell (6, 106) to

define therein channels (36, 136) for guiding the combustion gases from said
inlet means (40, 140) towards said outlet means (41, 141), the fins (35, 135)

being in heat conducting relationship at least with said longitudinal wall (14,
128);

manifolds (40, 41, 140,141) serving the channels (36, 136) in parallel.
