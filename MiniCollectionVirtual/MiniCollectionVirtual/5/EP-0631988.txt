<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for purification of waste waters from aldol condensation reaction of n-butyraldehyde
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C02F900	B01D1104	C02F126	B01D1104	C02F900	C02F126	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C02F	B01D	C02F	B01D	C02F	C02F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C02F9	B01D11	C02F1	B01D11	C02F9	C02F1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Wastewaters from the aldol condensation reaction, to decrease the COD value, are extracted at pH 1 to 6 with monohydric alcohols which contain 8 or more carbon atoms in the molecule and/or with hydrocarbons which contain 6 or more carbon atoms in the molecule.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A process for cleaning waste water from the aldolisation reaction of n-butyraldehyde,
characterised in that the waste water is set to a pH of 0 to 6,

and a precipitating organic phase is optionally separated off and the waste
water is then extracted with monoalcohols containing 8 to 16 carbon atoms

in the molecule.
The process according to claim 1, characterised in that the waste water is
set to a pH of 1 to 3.
The process according to claim 2, characterised in that the pH is set with
sulfuric acid.
</CLAIMS>
