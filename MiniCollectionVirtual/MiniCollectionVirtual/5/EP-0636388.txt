<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the disposal of halons or halons containing fluorohydrocarbons or fluorochlorohydrocarbons.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A62D300	B01D5332	B01D5332	B01D5370	B01D5370	B01D5374	B01D5375	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A62D	B01D	B01D	B01D	B01D	B01D	B01D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A62D3	B01D53	B01D53	B01D53	B01D53	B01D53	B01D53	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a process for disposing of halons or halon-containing fluorocarbons (fluorinated hydrocarbons) or chlorofluorocarbons (CFC), wherein these are thermally or photochemically dissociated in the presence of a hydrogen source, the gas formed, which contains HF, HBr, CO2 and water and possibly HCl, is cooled and the hydrogen halides are absorbed in a scrubbing liquid in a gas scrubbing column and neutralised with a base, and also, if desired, elemental bromine or chlorine present in the gas is reduced using a reducing agent to give the corresponding salts.
