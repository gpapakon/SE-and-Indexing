Pressure probe for quantitative determination of contaminants in groundwater.

<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a pressure probe (1) for quantitative determination of contaminants in groundwater, especially mineral oil hydrocarbons and/or polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, in situ using a measurement device (6). The object of the invention is to obtain a pressure probe, using which a direct assessment, which can be made in situ, regarding possible contamination can be carried out in a corresponding required deep-probing process and evaluated. This object is achieved by the combination of the following technical features:   a) the pressure probe (1) for determining physical parameters of the ground has an additional measurement head having a measurement chamber (9) for holding a groundwater sample to be measured, and a fibre-optic cable (12) connected to the measurement device (6), and b) the measurement device preferably consists of a technical laser (14) having an attached computer (17) for evaluation, time-resolved laser-induced fluorescence being used as the measurement process.
