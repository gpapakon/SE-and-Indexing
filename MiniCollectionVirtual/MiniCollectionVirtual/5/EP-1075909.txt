Cutting knife

<ABSTRACT>
Cutting knife has a fixing part with a receiver for fixing to an oscillating drive; a cutting part (22) with a cutter (28, 30); and a number of flat layers (34, 36, 38, 40) joined together, at least two of these layers being made of a metallic material. An Independent claim is also included for a process for the production of the cutting knife comprising producing a number of thin layers (34, 36, 38, 40); hot-joining the layers, preferably by forging to produce a metallic green body for the knife; heat treating by hardening or tempering; and post-processing the green body by grinding and/or polishing.

<CLAIMS>
Cutting knife for cutting tough, elastic material, in particular
for cutting sealing strips on windows in vehicles

or in brickwork, comprising a securement portion (20) having
a securement receptacle (26; 26b) for securing to an

oscillatory drive (12) and with a cutting portion (22;
22a; 22b; 22e) having at least one cutting edge (28, 30;

28a, 30a; 28b, 30b; 28c, 30c), 
characterized in that
 the
cutting knife (10) comprises a plurality of flat bonded

layers (34, 36, 38; 34a, 36a, 38a, 40a, 42, 44; 34b, 36b,
38b), at least two of the layers (34, 36, 38; 34a, 36a,

38a, 42, 44; 34b, 36b, 38b) comprising a metallic material.
Cutting knife of Claim 1, 
characterized in that
 the metallic
layers (34, 36, 38; 34a, 36a, 38a, 40a, 42, 44; 34b,

36b, 38b) are bonded to one another through heat bonding,
preferably by forging.
Cutting knife of Claims 1 or 2, 
characterized in that
 some
of the layers (34, 36, 38; 34a, 36a, 38a, 40a, 42, 44;

34b, 36b, 38b) are made of materials with differing material
properties.
Cutting knife of Claim 3, 
characterized in that
 at least
one outer layer (38, 40; 38a, 40a; 38b) is provided having 

good cutting properties, in particular high hardness, and
at least one inner layer (34, 36; 34a, 36a; 34b, 36b) is

provided having a good elasticity and a high bending elongation.
Cutting knife of Claim 3, 
characterized in that
 at least
one outer layer (38, 40; 38a, 40a; 38b, 40b) is provided

having a softer material as the remaining layers and/or
greater elasticity than the remaining layers.
Cutting knife of any one of the preceding claims, 
characterized
in that
 at least one (40; 40a) of the layers (34,
36, 38; 34a, 36a, 38a, 40a, 42, 44; 34b, 36b, 38b) comprises

a wear resistant material, preferably wolfram carbide,
silicon carbide, titanium carbide, chromium oxide,

silicon oxide, titanium oxide, aluminium oxide, boron nitride,
titanium nitride, molybdenum or mixtures thereof.
Cutting knife of any one of the preceding claims, 
characterized
in that
 at least one outer layer (40; 40a) of the
cutting portion (22; 22a) contains friction-reducing additives,

preferably segregates of molybdenum sulfide and/or

Cutting knife of any one of the Claims 1 to 6,

Method for producing a cutting knife (10) for cutting
tough, elastic material, in particular for cutting sealing

strips on windows in vehicles, comprising a securement
portion (20; 20b; 20c) having a securement receptacle (26;

26b; 26c) for securing to an oscillatory drive (12) and a 
cutting portion (22; 22a; 22b; 22c) with at least one cutting

edge (28, 30; 28a, 30a; 28b, 30b; 28c, 30c), the
method comprising the steps of:


(a) producing a plurality of thin metal layers (34, 36,
38; 34a, 36a, 38a, 42, 44; 34b, 36b, 38b),
(b) heat bonding the metallic layers (34, 36, 38; 34a,
36a, 38a, 42, 44; 34b, 36b, 38b), preferably by

forging to produce a metallic blank (32) for the
cutting knife (10),
(c) heat treating the blank preferably by hardening or
tempering the blank (32), and
(d) subsequent processing the blank (32) by grinding
and/or polishing.
The method of Claim 15, wherein the securement receptacle
(26) is formed after the heat treatment of step (c), preferably

by electric erosion or laser cutting.
Method of Claim 15, wherein the cutting knife (10b; 10c)
is produced from a blank comprising an elastic and/or soft

material and wherein a plurality of metal layers is at
least applied in the region of its at least one cutting

edge (28b, 30b; 28c, 30c).
Method of Claim 17, wherein the blank made of elastic
and/or soft material is produced together with the securement

receptacle (26b) by stamping. 
Method of Claims 15 or 16, wherein the blank (32) is bent
in a hot condition to produce a preferably U-shaped, angled

or knee-shaped cross section.
Method of any one of the Claims 15 to 19, wherein at least
one additional wear resistant layer (40; 40a) is applied

to the cutting portion.
Method of Claim 20, wherein the wear resistant layer (40;
40a) is produced from wolfram carbide, silicon carbide,

titanium carbide, chromium oxide, silicon oxide, titanium
oxide, aluminium oxide, boron nitride, titanium nitride,

molybdenum or mixtures thereof.
Method of Claim 20 or 21, wherein the at least one wear
resistant layer (40; 40a) is applied by thermal spraying,

preferably by plasma spraying or by CVD or PVD.
Method of Claim 20 or 21, wherein the wear resistant layer
(40; 40a) is produced by thermal spraying of carbides,

which are contained in a metallic matrix, preferably
nickel, cobalt or alloys thereof.
Method of any one of the Claims 20 to 23, wherein the wear
resistant layer (40; 40a) includes friction-reducing additives,

preferably segregates of molybdenum sulfide and/or
graphite.
Method for producing a cutting knife (10) for cutting
tough, elastic materials, in particular for cutting sealing

strips on windows in vehicles, comprising a securement 
portion (20; 20a) having a securement receptacle (26; 26b)

for securing to an oscillatory drive (12) and a cutting
portion (22; 22a) with at least one cutting edge (28, 30;

28a, 30a), the method comprising the steps of:

(a) producing a blank (32) made of steel,
(b) heat treating the blank (32), preferably by tempering
the blank (32),
(c) subsequent processing of the blank (32) preferably
by grinding and/or polishing, and
(d) applying at least one wear resistant layer (40; 40a)
to the cutting portion (22; 22a) by thermal spraying.
Method of Claim 25, wherein the wear resistant layer (40;
40a) is made from molybdenum, a carbide, an oxide, a metal

carbide, a metal oxide or mixtures thereof.
Method of Claim 26, wherein the wear resistant layer (40;
40a) is formed by thermal spray application of carbides,

which are contained in a metal matrix, preferably nickel,
cobalt or alloys thereof.
Method of Claims 25, 26 or 27, wherein the blank (32) is
subjected to roughening blasting before the step of thermal

spraying. 
Method of any one of the Claims 25 to 28, wherein an adhesive
linking layer is applied before spray application of

the wear resistant layer (40; 40a).
Method of any one of the Claims 25 to 29, wherein the
blank (32) along with the securement receptacle (26) is

produced by stamping.
Method of any one of the Claims 25 to 30, wherein the
blank (32) is bent to produce a preferably U-shaped, angled

or knee-shaped cross section before the step (b) of
heat treating.
Method of any one of the Claims 25 to 31, wherein in the
wear resistant layer (40; 40a) includes friction-reducing

additives, preferably segregates of molybdenum sulfide
and/or graphite.
