<INVENTION-TITLE>
COMPUTER INPUT UNIT, IN PARTICULAR FOR CAD.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G06F116	G06F116	G06F302	G06F302	G06F3023	G06F3023	G06F3033	G06F3033	</CLASSIFICATIONS>

<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G06F1	G06F1	G06F3	G06F3	G06F3	G06F3	G06F3	G06F3	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A computer input unit, in particular for CAD, consists of a computer keyboard (1) and of a thumb-actuated mini pointing pad (2) arranged approximately at the middle of the field of alphanumeric keys next to the space bar (3), on the side of the user. The cursor (or cross-hair) on the screen is moved by moving the thumb of one hand on the surface of the mini pointing pad, whereas the other fingers remain on the alphanumeric keys. In order to click an object (or a menu field), the pressure exercised on the sensor surface of the mini digitising tablet (2) is increased for a short time; this (increased) pressure actuates a contact or a contactless sensor and causes clicking.
</ABSTRACT>
