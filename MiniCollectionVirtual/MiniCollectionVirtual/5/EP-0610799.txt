<INVENTION-TITLE>
Sheet-stapling device.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B27F700	B27F719	B42B400	B42B402	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B27F	B27F	B42B	B42B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B27F7	B27F7	B42B4	B42B4	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The fasteners are driven into the stack from one side, while at the other a punch actuates two pivoting tools bending the fastener free ends. A roller (28,29) acts against the rear face (30b,21b) furthest from the fastener (34) of each tool. The rollers turn on the common actuating punch (26), which travels in a straight line. The rollers can be arranged one behind the other in the lengthwise direction of the fastener, or they can rotate freely alongside each other on a common pin. Alternatively, they can have a flange and bearing trunnion at each side.
</ABSTRACT>
