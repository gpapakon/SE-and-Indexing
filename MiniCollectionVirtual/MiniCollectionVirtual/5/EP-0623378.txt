<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a process for reducing the pollutant concentration from exhaust gases of combustion plants or in the exhaust air of recycling or drying plants in which the pollutant-containing gases are passed through a dry filter bed and the filter bed is continuously renewed, characterized in that the hot exhaust gases are passed via a distributor cone (11) into a filter bed (17), the filter bed (17) is composed of pellets which are made up of   (a) special lime T, containing calcium carbonate and hexagonal carbon and   (b) sugar industry residue products, namely     (i) syrupy molasses and/or   (ii) carbonation sludge from sugar production;   the pellets which are pollutant-laden and at least in part disintegrated to form dust are discharged beneath the distributor cone and the bed height of the filter bed is replenished in accordance with the discharge. The invention also relates to an apparatus for carrying out the process.
