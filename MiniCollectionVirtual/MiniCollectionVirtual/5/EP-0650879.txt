<INVENTION-TITLE>
Plunger buffer for railway vehicles.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B61G1100	B61G1102	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B61G	B61G	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B61G11	B61G11	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A plunger buffer for rail vehicles is formed essentially from a buffer plunger (1) with buffer plate (1a), a buffer sleeve (2) with base plate (2a) which can be screwed on to the vehicle and a spring (3). In order to be able to construct such a plunger buffer in the simplest and most cost-effective way possible such that in particular a high degree of resistance to wear can be achieved, the buffer plate (1a) consists of a material whose surface can be hardened. After the surface hardening, the buffer plate (1a) has a hardness of at least 350 HV 30 at Rht 250
