<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the preparation of 2-hydroxynaphthalene-6-carboxylic acid
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C07C5138	C07C5110	C07C6500	C07C6511	C07C5112	C07C5110	C07C51347	</CLASSIFICATIONS>

<ABSTRACT>
A process for the preparation of 2-hydroxynaphthalene-6-carboxylic acid using potassium carbonate and CO at temperatures above 260@C and a pressure of at least 10 bar is characterised in that 2-hydroxynaphthalene-3-carboxylic acid and/or 2-hydroxynaphthalene-3,6-dicarboxylic acid or their potassium salts and optionally potassium  beta -naptholate are used as starting materials. The process according to the invention enables, by reuse of the by-products, the product selectivity to be increased considerably with respect to 2-hydroxynaphthalene-6-carboxylic acid.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A process for the preparation of 2-hydroxynaphthalene-6-carboxylic
acid using potassium

carbonate and CO at temperatures above 150°C and at
a pressure of at least 10 bar, which comprises using

2-hydroxynaphthalene-3-carboxylic acid and/or
2-hydroxynaphthalene-3,6-dicarboxylic acid and/or

their potassium salts and, if desired, potassium
β-naphtholate as starting materials.
The process as claimed in claim 1, wherein the
naphthalenecarboxylic acids employed can be obtained

as secondary products from the Kolbe-Schmitt
reaction.
</CLAIMS>
