<INVENTION-TITLE>
VIDEO TELECONFERENCING SYSTEM.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>H04N714	H04N714	H04N715	H04N715	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>H04N	H04N	H04N	H04N	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>H04N7	H04N7	H04N7	H04N7	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
In videoteleconferencing systems (VS), the video signal corresponding to one frame of each subscriber station (T) is transmitted to a video conference centre (Z). This converts the video signal of some subscriber stations into a video signal corresponding to one small picture. This occupies an unnecessarily large transmission capacity of a telecommunications network (N). According to the invention, only the amount of video data which corresponds to the minimum amount of video data required for meeting the current requirements of the further subscriber stations is transmitted from one of the subscriber stations (T) to the video conference centre (Z).
