<INVENTION-TITLE>
Rear view mirror, particularly for trucks
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B60R106	B60R106	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B60R	B60R	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B60R1	B60R1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A rear-view mirror has a holding arm (1) and a casing (2) which is mounted on the latter in an adjustable fashion. On the casing (2) there is fixedly but releasably mounted a piece of mirror glass (11). The piece of mirror glass (11) is fastened to a backing plate (12) and is exposed over its entire surface (14). The backing plate (12) is releasably mounted in the casing (2) in such a way that it is in particular elastically locked into place.

<CLAIMS>
An exterior rear view mirror, in particular for trucks, comprising a holder
(1), an adjustable, possibly motor-adjustable housing (2) on the holder (1)

and a mirror glass (11) disposed on the housing (2) in a manner fixed relative
to the latter but interchangeable, which mirror glass (11) is fixed on a

support plate (12) and the entire surface (14) of which lies flee, the support
plate (12) being detachably disposed on the housing (2), 
characterized in
that
 the support plate (12) is provided with a rim (13) encircling the edge
(15) of the mirror glass (11) and not projecting over the surface (14) of the

mirror glass (11).
An exterior rear view mirror according to claim 1, 
characterized in that

the rim (13) tapers toward the mirror glass (11) in the direction toward the
surface (14) of the mirror glass (11).
An exterior rear view mirror according to one of claims 1 or 2, 
characterized
in that
 the wall portion (18) adjacent to the support plate (12), of the
housing (2) does not project from the circumference of the support plate

(12).
An exterior rear view mirror according to one of claims 1 to 3, 
characterized
in that
 the support plate (12) supporting the mirror glass (11) is
locked into place in the housing (2).
An exterior rear view mirror according to one of claims 1 to 4, 
characterized
in that
 on its rear (16) turned away from the mirror glass (11), the 
support plate (12) is provided with a locking web (17) which engages with

a suited recess (21) in the housing (2).
An exterior rear view mirror according to claim 5, 
characterized in that

the locking web (17) is provided with at least one projection (20) engaging
with the recess (21).
An exterior rear view mirror according to one of claims 1 to 6, 
characterized
in that
 an encircling, groove-like and externally open recess (23) is
formed between the support plate (12) and the adjacent wall portion (18) of

the housing (2).
</CLAIMS>
