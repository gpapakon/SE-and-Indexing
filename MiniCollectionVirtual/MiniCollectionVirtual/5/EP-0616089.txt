<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the forming of wooden elements and resulting elements used in connections.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>E04B126	E04B126	E04C312	E04C316	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>E04B	E04B	E04C	E04C	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>E04B1	E04B1	E04C3	E04C3	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The process is intended for forming wooden elements such as beams, trunks and tree segments having a longitudinal passage over their entire length and thus having two ends, each possessing a hole. It is characterised in that a piece of wood (1-2) is attached to at least one of the ends of an element (A) by being engaged in the existing hole (B), which piece of wood (1-2) has the same cross section as that of the said hole (B) in order to plug it, since the said piece (1-2) is formed in order to make it suitable for constituting a connection (assembly) member.
