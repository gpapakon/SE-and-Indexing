<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process and apparatus for the introduction of alkali chlorate and acid in a reactor for the production of chlordioxide.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C01B1100	C01B1102	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C01B	C01B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C01B11	C01B11	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The reaction of alkali metal chlorate solution with an acid, e.g. hydrochloric acid, for producing a gaseous mixture of chlorine dioxide and chlorine is carried out in a reactor having one or more reaction stages. First, the alkali metal chlorate solution and the acid are passed together through a mixing chamber. The solution mixture here formed is conveyed from the mixing chamber into the uppermost reaction stage of the reactor.
