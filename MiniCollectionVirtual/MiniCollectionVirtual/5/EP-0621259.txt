<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for preparing aromatic amines and diazonium salts.

<ABSTRACT>
A process for the preparation of halogenated aromatic amines by reducing the corresponding nitro compounds with hydrazine in the presence of a heterogenous catalyst containing a metal from Group VIIIa, characterised in that the reaction is carried out   a) in a temperature range from approximately -20@C to approximately 70@C using b) approximately 1.0 to approximately 1.7 equivalents of hydrazine, based on the nitro group(s) to be reduced,   c) in the presence of a heterogenous palladium catalyst.   The process gives halogenated aromatic amines in good yield without dehalogenation reactions. If appropriate, the product can be reacted directly in situ to give diazonium salts.
 
</ABSTRACT>
