<INVENTION-TITLE>
Bent strip-like integrated optical waveguide.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G02B6122	G02B6122	G02B6125	G02B6125	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>G02B	G02B	G02B	G02B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G02B6	G02B6	G02B6	G02B6	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
2.1 A description is given of a strip-like integrated optical waveguide (1), having a longitudinal axis (10) which runs between the ends (11, 12) of the waveguide in a bent manner, for producing optical connections between any components of an optical circuit, in which conversion losses arising from the change in the local transverse mode distribution can be kept small. 2.2 For this purpose, the longitudinal axis has at the two ends of the waveguide the curvature zero and between the two ends of the waveguide at least two sections (101, 102) in which in each case the curvature of the longitudinal axis increases from one end of the section in the direction of another end of this section in a continuously monotonous manner from the curvature zero at the one end to a curvature (¦Kmax¦), whose absolute value is a maximum, at the other end of the section. 2.3 Use in optical telecommunications systems.
