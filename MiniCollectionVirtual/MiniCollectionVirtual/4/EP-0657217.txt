<INVENTION-TITLE>
Laboratory apparatus with a socket for a support rod.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B01F1300	B01F1308	B01L900	B01L900	F16B202	F16B218	F16M1302	F16M1302	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B01F	B01F	B01L	B01L	F16B	F16B	F16M	F16M	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B01F13	B01F13	B01L9	B01L9	F16B2	F16B2	F16M13	F16M13	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A laboratory apparatus, in particular a magnet stirrer (1) or a shaker, has a socket (2) for a support stand rod (3) which is provided on a separate support stand rod holder (4) which is adjustably attached and fixable to the laboratory apparatus. The support stand rod (3) can by this means be arranged at different points on the housing of the laboratory apparatus so that the accessory apparatuses attached thereto, in particular sensors, dosing units or hoses can be more readily fixed in the respective desired position. Moreover, a plurality of support stand rod holders (4) each having one support stand rod (3) can be simultaneously attached at various positions of the laboratory apparatus so that even in the case of complex trial structures, sufficient potential attachment sites for accessory apparatuses are present.
