<INVENTION-TITLE>
PROCESS FOR GLUEING TWO NON-METALLIC SUBSTRATES BY MEANS OF AN ADHESIVE.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B29C6534	B29C6536	C08J512	C08J512	C09J506	C09J506	C09J1102	C09J1104	H05B602	H05B602	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B29C	B29C	C08J	C08J	C09J	C09J	C09J	C09J	H05B	H05B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B29C65	B29C65	C08J5	C08J5	C09J5	C09J5	C09J11	C09J11	H05B6	H05B6	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A glueing process consists in distributing the ferromagnetic fillers in an adhesive or in the substrate and in inductively heating them within a magnetic field of 10 kHz to 1 MHz. For that purpose, a material having a resistivity of 10 to 108 ohm . m, a coercive field of less than 60 A/m, an induced magnetic field of less than 1000 mT and a particle size of 1500 to 100.000 (my)m3 is selected, or a material having a resistivity of 10 to 108 ohm . m, a coercive field of 60 to 12.000 A/m, an induced magnetic field of 1000 to 10.000 mT and a particle size of 1 to 1500 (my)m3, so that the sum of the losses by eddy currents and by hysteresis allows these fillers to provide a power of 1000 to 10.000 W/cm3. The heating temperature is limited to about the maximum polymerisation temperature of the adhesive by setting its duration to less than 40 s for the temperature at the interface to correspond at least to the minimum polymerisation temperature.
</ABSTRACT>
