<CLAIMS>
A method for supporting a patient in a 
proper position for a treatment, particularly for a dental 

and/or orosurgical and/or orthodontic treatment, wherein a 
patient to be treated is positioned in a specific position on a 

treating chair (1) or a treating couch (1)
 
characterized in that
 
a region in which the patient is to be treated, particularly the 

dental or jaw region, is movable by the treating chair (1) or 
treating couch (1) to a spatial position which is defined by the 

height of the treating physician and which has previously been 
stored for the treating physician or which is stored by the 

treating physician, and in that defined spatial position the 
orientation of that region in which the patient is to be treated 

is adjusted in dependence on predetermined stored parameters for 
each of different treatments, particularly of the tooth and jaw 

regions. 
A method according to claim 1,
 
   characterized in that the elevation of the 

defined spatial position of that region in which the patent 
is to be treated corresponds approximately to the elevation 

of the underarm of the treating physician when that underarm 
extends from the body of the physician at an angle of about 

90° whereas his or her upper arm lies against the trunk of the 
physician. 
A method according to claim 1 or 2,
 
   characterized in that
 
   during a movement of the patient to the position 

for the treatment the head of the patient is vertically 
and laterally inclined in a position defined by a headrest (10),  

 
which is attached to the treating chair (1) or treating couch 

(1) and is movable by a motor and preferably provided with a 
concentric cushion, which is soft-hard from the inside to the 

outside. 
A method, according to any of the 
preceding claims, wherein the spatial position and orientation 

of that region in which the patent is to be treated are adjusted 
or more accurately adjusted in that measured parameters, such as 

particularly the weight or height of the patient, are detected 
by measuring sensors, the detected parameters are associated 

with predetermined positions of the treating chair (1) or 
treating couch (1), and the treating chair (1) or treating couch 

(1) is subsequently moved to said positions. 
A method according to claim 4,
 
   characterized in that
 
   the defined spatial position for a given 

treating physician is determined in that the measuring sensors 
detect the height of the treating physician and compute the 

defined spatial position in dependence on said height. 
A method according to claim 4 or 5,
 
   characterized in that
  
 

   the respective parameters of the patient 
are detected by measuring sensors which are provided on the 

treating chair (1) or treating couch (1) and particularly 
consist of instruments for measuring weight or pressure, 

when the patient occupies the treating chair (1) or treating 
couch (1). 
A method according to claim 6,
 
   characterized in that
 
   the height of the patient is detected in 

that the extent of the longitudinal displacement of the headrest 
(10) or the position of the head of the patient on the 

headrest (10) is measured. 
A method according to any of claims 1 to 7,
 
   characterized in that
 
   the parameters are detected by the measuring 

sensors in that measuring sensors which are spaced from 
the treating chair (1) or treating couch (1) and particularly 

consist of an instrument for measuring weight and length are 
used to detect the weight or height before the patient occupies 

the treating chair (1) or treating couch (1).  
 
A method according to any of the 
preceding claims, wherein the position of 

that region in which the patient is to be treated, particularly 
of the tooth or jaw regions, is adjusted or still more 

accurately adjusted by the treating chair (1) or treating 
couch (1) in that further parameters of the patient are detected 

by a direct measurement of the spatial position and/or 
the spatial dimensions of the region which is to be treated 

and are compared with previously stored values of prescribed 
positions and the patient is moved to a position which 

corresponds to the predetermined stored prescribed position. 
A method according to claim 9,
 
   characterized in that the direct measurement
 
   of the spatial position and/or the spatial dimensions of that 

region in which the patient is to be treated is effected in 
that a distance-measuring instrument measures the position of 

the maxillary arch and/or mandibular arch or of the tooth arch 
(20, 21) or the position of the soft tissues which cover the 

maxillary or mandibular arch. 
A method according to claim 10,
 
   characterized in that
 
   the distance-measuring instrument consists 

of a camera (38), which is to be moved to or into the oral 
cavity of the patient and which has preferably a wide-angle 

lens, the spatial position of that camera is detected and evaluated 
and the camera optically detects the position of the 

jaw arches and/or the position of the tooth arches (20, 21) 
and said optically detected position is evaluated. 
A method according to claim 9 or 10,
 
   characterized in that
 
   the direct measurement of the spatial position 

and/or the spatial dimensions of that region in which the 
patient is to be treated is effected in that the spatial reflections 

of light from a bite-on plate held by the patient  
 

with his or her teeth or his or her jaw or from a reflecting 
and preferably fluorescent layer which has been applied to 

the lips of the patient are detected and are subsequently 
evaluated by a camera (38, 45) and/or a plurality of photovoltaic 

cells (4, 6). 
A method according to any of the preceding 
claims,
 
   characterized in that that region which is 

to be treated, particularly the tooth and/or jaw regions, remains 
or remain substantially stationary in the defined spatial 

position during a change of the position of the patient. 
A method according to claim 13,
 
   characterized in that
 
position control elements (2, 3, 4), in particular actuators of 

said elements (2, 3, 4), of the treating chair (1) or treating 
couch (1) are actuated in such a coordination that only a simple 

position control movement is imparted by a given position 
control element (2, 3, 4) so that the treating chair (1) or 

treating couch (1) performs only a single position control 
movement from a previous position to a new position and those 

position control elements (2, 3, 4) which are actuated at a time 
are actuated substantially during the same time. 
A method according to any of the prece
 
   ding claims,
 
   characterized in that 

light marks which indicate the optimum location 
at least of the treating physician are projected on the 

floor beside the treating chair (1) or the treating couch (1) 
and the light marks particularly indicate a larger surface 

area in which the feet of the treating physician are required 
to stand, or the prescribed location at least of the feet of 

the treating physician is directly indicated by the light  
 

marks, or luminous means (49) attached to the treating chair 
(1) or the treating couch (1) indicate that location, preferably 

in a dial position with reference to the sternum. 
A method according to any of the preceding 
claims,
 
   characterized in that
 
   the instructions for the operation, particularly 

the positioning, the position and the maintenance of 
the position of the treating chair (1) or treating couch (1) 

or a work-illuminating lamp (42) are given by voice and received 
by a microphone (50) and delivered to a voice recognition 

and output unit. 
An apparatus for supporting a patient in 
a proper position for a treatment, particularly for carrying out 

a method according to any of the preceding claims, comprising a 
treating chair (1) or a treating couch (1), position control 

elements (2, 3, 4) attached to said chair or couch, and means 
(5) for controlling the position control elements (2, 3, 4), 

which means comprise at least a memory, which memory is adapted 
to store in association with each other data of the position 

control elements (2, 3, 4) for different positions and 
orientations of the treating chair (1) or of the treating couch 

(1) charachterized in that
 
said control means (5) for said position control elements (2, 3, 

4) respectively comprise at leat an input unit (6, 7) a 
microprocessor and an output unit (8), said memory being 

provided for storing data associated to the individual treating 
physician and a region in which a patient is to be treated, 

which data have previously been stored and/or can be entered by 
the input unit (6, 7) or a microphone (50) and can be output 

and/or read in via the output unit (8) from a data carrier. 
An apparatus according to claim 17,
 
   characterized in that
 
measuring sensors, particularly for the weight or the height of 

the patient or the physician, are connected  
 

to the means (5) for controlling the position control 
elements (2, 3, 4), and that data detected by the measuring 

sensors, particularly the weight and/or height of the patient 
and/or physician, are adapted to be stored in the memory. 
An apparatus according to claim 17 or 18,
 
   characterized in that
 
   the measuring sensors are attached to the 

treating chair (1) or treating couch (1) and the sensor for 
measuring the height of the patient or of the physician is 

attached to the means for the longitudinal adjustment of a 
headrest (10) of the treating chair (1) or treating couch (1). 
An apparatus according to any of claims 
17, 18 or 19,
 
   characterized in that
 
   the measuring sensors are arranged near the 

treating chair (1) or treating couch (1), preferably in the 
region from which the treating chair (1) or the treating couch 

(1) is accessible, and consist of one or more light barriers 
or a distance-measuring instrument and/or of weighing means 

arranged adjacent to the floor. 
An apparatus according to claims 17 to 20,
 
   characterized in that further measuring sensors 

for detecting the position of the region in which the 
patient is to be treated, particularly of the tooth or jaw regions, 

are arranged near the treating chair (1) or treating 
couch (1) and are connected to the means (5) for controlling 

the position control elements (2, 3, 4) and data concerning the 
spatial position of the region which has been detected by the 

measuring sensors are adapted to be stored in the memory. 
An apparatus according to claim 21,
 
   characterized in that
  
 

   the measuring sensors for detecting the region 
to be treated consist of a camera (38) provided with units 

(39) for detecting the spatial position of the camera (38), 
which comprises a wide-angle lens, and evaluating units (40) 

for evaluating the spatial position of the images which have 
been recorded by the camera (38), and the evaluating units (40) 

are connected to the means (5) for controlling the position 
control elements. 
An apparatus according to claim 21,
 
   characterized in that
 
   one or more ultrasonic generators (43) and 

one or more ultrasonic receivers (44) are arranged near the 
treating chair (1) or treating couch (1) and are connected to 

an evaluating unit, which communicates with the means (5) for 
controlling the position control elements (2, 3, 4). 
An apparatus according to claim 21,
 
   charac
terized in that
 
   light sources (42, 48) which illuminate the 

region to be treated are arranged near the treating chair (1) 
or treating couch and a camera (45) and/or other photovolaic 

cells (46), which are connected to an evaluating unit, are 
preferably arranged near the headrest (10). 
An apparatus according to any of claims 
17 to 24,
 
   characterized in that
 
   means (47) for projecting light marks are 

provided near the treating chair (1) or treating couch (1) 
and said means (47) communicate with the means (5) for controlling 

the position control elements (2, 3, 4) and are adapted 
to project light marks on the floor in dependence on the 

data which concern the spatial position and orientation of the 
treating chair (1) or treating couch (1) and have been stored 

in the memory.  
 
An apparatus according to any of claims 
17 to 25,
 
   characterized in that
 
   a microphone (50) which is connected to an 

evaluating unit for voice recognition is arranged near the 
treating chair (1) or treating couch (1), said evaluating unit 

is connected to the means (5) for controlling the position control 
elements (2, 3, 4) and instructions entered by voice input 

are adapted to be decoded and forwarded to the means for 
controlling. 
An apparatus according to any of claims 
17 to 26,
 
   characterized in that
 
   in addition to the treating chair (1) or the 

treating couch (1) the dental instruments and means which are 
adpated to be controlled by the input unit (6, 7) or the microprocessor, 

particularly by voice input via the microphone, 
include at least the work-illuminating lamp (42). 
An apparatus according to any of claims 
17 to 27,
 
   characterized in that
 
   automatic and/or voice-controllable means 

are provided for repositioning the treating chair, preferably 
with the aid of the camera (38) in order to maintain the patient 

in the proper position for the treatment if that position 
is changed by the patient. 
An apparatus according to any of claims 
17 to 28,
 
   characterized in that additional optical control 

means are provided between the treating person and the 
treating chair (1) and are adapted to automatically re-align 

the treating chair when the treating person has changed his or 
her dial position.
