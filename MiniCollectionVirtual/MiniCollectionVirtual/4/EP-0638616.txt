<ABSTRACT>
Pigment salts of   a) an organic dye which contains at least one acid group -SO3H, -COOH or -PO(OH)2 and is selected from the group consisting of azo dyes, quinacridones, diketopyrrolopyrroles, phthalocyanines, indanthrones, flavanthrones, pyranthrones, anthraquinones, perylenes, dioxazines, perinones, thioindigo, isoindolines and isoindolinones, and b) a nitroxyl (N-oxide) compound comprising at least one unsubstituted or substituted 5-, 6- or 7-membered heterocycle which contains a divalent group of the formula 
<

  or

  in which   R1 and R2 independently of one another are methyl, ethyl or phenyl or together form a 4-12-membered aliphatic ring,   L is an aromatic radical, and   Y is O., OH or OR in which R is C5-C6-cycloalkyl or C4-C10-alkyl, with salt formation taking place between the acid groups of the dye and the nitroxyl (N-oxide) groups.   These pigment salts are distinguished by outstanding light fastness and weather resistance.
