<ABSTRACT>
An implant (10), in particular a stopper or an obturator for the medullary canal, consists of at least one hollow body (12) whose wall is at least partially porous, which can be elastically, visco-elastically or plastically deformed and occludingly inserted into a long bone. The implant has the advantage of being adaptable to various medullary canal diameters, so that practically all medullary canals can be hermetically sealed.
</ABSTRACT>
