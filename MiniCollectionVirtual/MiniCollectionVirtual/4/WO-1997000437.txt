<INVENTION-TITLE>
OPTICAL PROBE WITH SENSOR MADE OF AN OPTICAL POLYMER
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G01N2125	G01N2127	G01N2155	G01N2155	G02B600	G02B600	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>G01N	G01N	G01N	G01N	G02B	G02B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G01N21	G01N21	G01N21	G01N21	G02B6	G02B6	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
An ATR spectrometer contains an optical probe with a sensor made of a transparent material, a light feeding line and a light carrying-off line. The spectrometer is characterised in that the sensor contains an optical polymer or a mixture of different optical polymers. The probe of the disclosed spectrometer has the advantage of being economical and extremely flexible, so that it may be adapted to the concentration range of the solution to be tested. The existing gap between concentrations that may be measured by conventional transmission spectroscopy and by state of the art ATR spectroscopy is thus closed.
</ABSTRACT>
