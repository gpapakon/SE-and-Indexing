<INVENTION-TITLE>
Use of a mass spectrometer with secondary ionisation for the inspection of containers.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>G01N2762	G01N2762	G01N2764	G01N2764	G01N3300	G01N3300	G01N3344	G01N3344	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>G01N	G01N	G01N	G01N	G01N	G01N	G01N	G01N	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>G01N27	G01N27	G01N27	G01N27	G01N33	G01N33	G01N33	G01N33	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
In order to test PET bottles for contamination, use is made of a mass spectrometer in which the gas sample is ionised by an ionised primary gas. This causes substantially less, or well-defined, fragmentation of the gas sample. The resulting spectrum therefore has a high information content without additional data being required and can be evaluated very quickly, which now allows use of a mass spectrometer in industrial inspection.
