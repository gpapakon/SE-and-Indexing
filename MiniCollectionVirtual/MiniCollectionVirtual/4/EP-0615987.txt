<INVENTION-TITLE>
Phenolic resins of esterified rosin acids, process for their preparation and their application as binder in printing inks.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C08G800	C08G828	C08G1600	C08G1600	C09D1108	C09D1108	C09D1110	C09D1110	C09F100	C09F104	</CLASSIFICATIONS>

<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C08G8	C08G8	C08G16	C08G16	C09D11	C09D11	C09D11	C09D11	C09F1	C09F1	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Natural resin acid esters which have been modified by means of phenolic resins are obtained by reacting     A) natural resins or natural resin acids, B) monocyclic or polycyclic phenols which can be alkylolated (alkoxylated),   C) aldehydes or aldehyde acetals,   D) aliphatic, cycloaliphatic or araliphatic polyols,   E) calcium and/or zinc compounds, and   F) optionally ethylenically unsaturated hydrocarbon resins, at a temperature of from 100 to 250 @C, and subsequently removing the water by azeotropic distillation by means of an entrainer at from 200 to 300 @C.
</ABSTRACT>
