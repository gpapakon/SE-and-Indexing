<INVENTION-TITLE>
Method of buryng ashes
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>E04H1300	E04H1300	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>E04H	E04H	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>E04H13	E04H13	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A hole (1) is dug and the plant (2) laid into it before it is filled around by earth (4) and ash (5). Ashes are first filled into the hole around the roots, human ashes are used. The plant may be a bush or a tree. The hole may be filled in layers of earth and ash. Rod or tube shaped structures (8) may be buried into the ground near the root area (6) in order to pour in the ashes. Further ashes may be added at a later date through the tubes.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A burial method whereby a large number of
graves gradually creates a forest, characterized in that a

hole is dug wherein a tree is placed whereupon said hole is
filled with ashes remaining after cremation that are brought

as close as possible to or into a root zone of said tree and
in direct contact with surrounding earth, and with earth.
The method of claim 1, characterized in that
said hole is filled with layers of said earth and said ashes.
A burial method whereby a large number of
graves gradually creates a forest, characterized in that

ashes remaining after cremation are brought into a root zone
of a preexisting tree in that rod-like or tube-like bodies

for introducing said ashes into said root zone are provided
at the time when said tree is planted and said ashes are

brought into said root zone through openings remaining after
removing said rod-like bodies or by feeding said ashes

through said tube-like bodies, respectively.
The method of anyone of claims 1 to 3
characterized in that said cremation ashes stem from a human

being.
</CLAIMS>
