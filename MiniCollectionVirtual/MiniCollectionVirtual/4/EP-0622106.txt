<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for removal of oxides of nitrogen and sulphur from the exhaust gases of combustion installations.

<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to an advantageous development of a process for purifying combustion plant flue gases containing oxides of nitrogen and sulphur by selective catalytic reduction of the nitrogen oxides, subsequent catalytic oxidation of sulphur dioxide with oxygen to give sulphur trioxide and scrubbing out the sulphur trioxide from the flue gases as aqueous sulphuric acid. The development according to the invention relates to concentration of the aqueous sulphuric acid to sulphuric acid over 95% strength by weight.
