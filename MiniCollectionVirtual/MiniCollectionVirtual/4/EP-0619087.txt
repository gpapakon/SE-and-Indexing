<INVENTION-TITLE>
Apparatus for treating and/or shaping hair, with a device for removing a curled lock
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
- The invention relates to an apparatus for treating and/or shaping hair, preferably for manual use, including, in an arrangement starting from a principal body:     - a gripping means (1) - a body (2), for rolling up the locks of hair, having a longitudinal axis (x-x') - at least one movable clamp (10) for gripping the lock of hair on the rolling-up body (2), characterised in that the movable clamp (10) is mounted so as to be freely displaceable over the rolling-up body (2) between two limit positions in order to ensure removal of the treated lock (M') in its initial treatment condition.   Apparatus for shaping hair.

<CLAIMS>
An appliance for treating and/or shaping hair, said appliance
preferably being for manual use, and including a main body on which

the following are mounted:

graspable means (1);
a winding body (2) for receiving a lock of hair and having a
longitudinal axis (x-x'); and
at least one pivotally mounted clamp (10) for clamping the lock
of hair against the winding body (2);
said appliance being characterized in that the clamp (10) is
mounted on a slide (11) that is freely displaceable on the winding

body (2) between two abutment positions corresponding to the ends of
the winding body (2).
An appliance according to claim 1, characterized in that the slide
(11) is constituted by a ring surrounding the winding body (2).
An appliance according to claim 1 or 2, characterized in that the
winding body (2) is mounted on the main body to be free to rotate

about its longitudinal axis (x-x').
An appliance according to claim 3, characterized in that it
includes control means (3) for starting and stopping rotation of the

winding body (2).
An appliance according to any one of claims 1 to 4, characterized
in that the winding body (2) is made of a plastics material.
An appliance according to any one of claims 1 to 5, characterized
in that it is constituted by a curling appliance.
</CLAIMS>
