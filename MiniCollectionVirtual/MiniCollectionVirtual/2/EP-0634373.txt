<INVENTION-TITLE>
Tooth filling made of barium-free glass with good x-ray absorption.
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a barium-free dental glass having good X-ray absorption which has a composition, in % by weight, based on oxide, of SiO2 45-65, B2O3 5-20, Al2O3 5-20, CaO 0-10, SrO 15-35 and F2-O 0-2. The glass has a refractive index of 
<
/= 1.56 and an Al equivalent thickness of 
>
/= 3 mm and a coefficient of thermal expansion  alpha  of 
<
/= 5 x 10
<
-6
>
/K in the range from -30 to +70 DEG C. The glass has very good mechanical and chemical properties and, after grinding to the particle sizes usual in dentistry, can be used in dental composites for synthetic resin pastes which are suitable as tooth fillings.
