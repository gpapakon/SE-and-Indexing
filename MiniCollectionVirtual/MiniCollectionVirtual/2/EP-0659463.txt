<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for controlling the sorption performance of a filter.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B01D5304	B01D5304	B01D5350	B01D5350	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B01D	B01D	B01D	B01D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B01D53	B01D53	B01D53	B01D53	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A process is described for controlling the sorption performance of a filtration separator, in which the separator serves for dedusting gases and bears a gas-permeable layer as sorbent which binds the gaseous pollutant contained in a gas. In this process it is provided that the mass of the sorbent situated on the filtration separator increases with increasing pollutant mass stream and is decreased with decreasing pollutant mass stream, a linear relationship existing between the mass stream of the pollutant to be separated off and the mass of the unused sorbent.
</ABSTRACT>
