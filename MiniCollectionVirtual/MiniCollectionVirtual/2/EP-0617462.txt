<ABSTRACT>
Amorphous hydrogen-containing carbon (a-C:H) with a low interfacial state density is obtained when a-C:H layers produced on a semiconductor substrate by plasma deposition are subjected to a treatment with hydrogen at elevated pressure and elevated temperature.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Method for preparing amorphous, hydrogenated
carbon (a-C:H) having a low interfacial state density,

characterized in that a-C:H layers generated on a
semiconductor substrate by means of plasma deposition

are subjected to a treatment with hydrogen at a
pressure of 50 to 1500 bar (5x10
6
-15x10
7
 Pa) and a
temperature of 100 to 500°C.
Method according to claim 1, characterized in
that an inert gas is added to the hydrogen.
Method according to claim 2, characterized in
that a noble gas, in particular argon or helium, is

added.
Method according to claim 2 or 3,
characterized in that the portion of inert gas amounts

to 1 to 50 % by volume.
Method according to one of claims 1 to 4,
characterized in that the treatment with hydrogen takes

place at a pressure of up to about 1000 bar.
Method according to one or more of claims 1 to
5, characterized in that the treatment with hydrogen

takes place at a temperature of up to about 350°C.
Method according to one or more of claims 1 to
6, characterized in that the treatment with hydrogen

takes place within a period of several hours.
Method according to one or more of claims 1 to
7, characterized in that the semiconductor substrate is

silicon, silicon carbide, gallium arsenide or indium
phosphide.
Amorphous, hydrogenated carbon in the form of
a plasma-deposited layer on a semiconductor substrate,

characterized by an interfacial state density
≤ 5.10
11
 cm
-2
.eV
-1
.
Use of amorphous, hydrogenated carbon
according to claim 9 as gate-insulation in MIS field-effect

transistors.
