<INVENTION-TITLE>
Apparatus for the removal of particles adhered to an elongated material.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B08B502	B08B502	C03C2512	C03C2512	C03C2570	C03C2570	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B08B	B08B	C03C	C03C	C03C	C03C	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B08B5	B08B5	C03C25	C03C25	C03C25	C03C25	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A device (1) for removing particles adhering to an elongated product (3) has an inlet nozzle (9) with a first nozzle bore (11), and an outlet nozzle (13) with a second nozzle bore (15). The inlet nozzle (9) is arranged at a distance upstream of the outlet nozzle (7) in the direction of passage of the elongated product (3), so that a purging (flushing) chamber (17) is formed therebetween. The cross-sections of the nozzle bores (11; 15) are only slightly larger at their narrowest points (12; 16) than the cross-section of the elongated product (3). The cross-section of the first nozzle bore (11) of the inlet nozzle (9) expands in the direction of passage of the elongated product (3), while the cross-section of the second nozzle bore (15) of the outlet nozzle (13) tapers (narrows) in the direction of passage. A purging gas can be fed into the purging chamber (17) through at least one purging gas duct (27).
