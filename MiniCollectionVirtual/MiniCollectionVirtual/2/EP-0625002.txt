<INVENTION-TITLE>
FODDER CONDITIONING ROLL, DEVICE AND MOWER CONDITIONER USING SUCH ROLLS
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A01D8200	A01D8200	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A01D	A01D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A01D82	A01D82	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A fodder conditioning roll remarkable in that each projection (17a, 17b, 17c, 17d; 18a, 18b, 18c, 18d; 19a, 19b, 19c, 19d; 20a, 20b, 20c, 20d) in a group of projections is joined at one of its edges to a corresponding edge of an adjacent projection in the same group of projections, by means of a branch (17'a, 17'b, 17'c, 17'd; 18'a, 18'b, 18'c, 18'd; 19'a, 19'b, 19'c, 19'd; 20'a, 20'b, 20'c, 20'd) with a reduced cross-section, so that all the projections (17a, 17b, 17c, 17d; 18a, 18b, 18c, 18d; 19a, 19b, 19c, 19d; 20a, 20b, 20c, 20d) and branches (17'a, 17'b, 17'c, 17'd; 18'a, 18'b, 18'c, 18'd; 19'a, 19'b, 19'c, 19'd; 20'a, 20'b, 20'c, 20'd) in one group of projections form a lug (17; 18; 19; 20) extending continuously along the roll. A fodder conditioning device and a mower conditioner using such fodder conditioning rolls are also disclosed.
</ABSTRACT>
