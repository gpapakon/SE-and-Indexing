<INVENTION-TITLE>
Prosthetic assembly for forming a knee joint
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61F238	A61F238	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A61F	A61F	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A61F2	A61F2	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The prosthetic assembly comprises:   - a first type of femoral element (1) capable of interacting either with a movable plate (2) of a tibial base (4) in order to constitute a movable-plate knee prosthesis, or with a fixed plate (3) of a tibial base (5) in order to constitute a fixed-plate knee prosthesis with preservation of the posterior cruciate ligaments; - a second type of femoral element (6) capable of interacting with a fixed plate (7) of the tibial base (5) in order to constitute a fixed-plate knee prosthesis stabilized at the back.

<CLAIMS>
Prosthetic assembly comprising a mobile
plate (2) of a tibial base plate (4) and a femoral

element (1) able to co-operate either with said
mobile plate (2) of said tibial base plate (4) to

form a mobile-plate knee prosthesis or with a
fixed plate (3) of a tibial base plate (5) to form a

fixed-plate knee prosthesis with conservation of the
posterior cruciate ligaments, characterised in

that:

the lower surface of the mobile plate (2)
has, within its thickness, a non opening cavity (2a)
the cavity (2a) has a shape closely
resembling a figure of eight,
the cavity (2a) co-operates with a shaft
(4a) that protrudes from the tibial base plate (4) to

control the rotatory movement and the antero-posterior
and medio-lateral movement of the plate

(2).
Assembly according to claim 1,
characterised in that the shaft (4a) has a

rectangular cross-section, the sides of the smallest
dimensions being rounded.
Assembly according to claim 1,
characterised in that the femoral element (1) bears

condyle bases (1a-1b) (6a-6b) shaped so as to
generate a constant linear contact throughout flexion

of the knee.
Assembly according to claim 1,
characterised in that the prosthetic assembly

comprises a fixed plate (3) forming a fixed unit with 
the tibial base plate by being screwed down and/or by

clipage.
Assembly according to claim 1,
characterised in that the femoral element and the

tibial base plate (1) bear or do not bear anchoring
shafts (9).
Assembly according to claim 1,
characterised in that the corresponding radii of the

curvature of the condyle bases of the femoral element
are identical.
</CLAIMS>
