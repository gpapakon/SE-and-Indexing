<INVENTION-TITLE>
BOLOMETRIC THERMAL DETECTOR
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
Thermal detector comprising a heat-sensitive material characterized in that the latter comprises a doped ferroelectric material whose resistance increases considerably with temperature, in the region of the ferroelectric/paraelectric transition. In particular, the material can be a doped ferroelectric ceramic comprising barium titanate. Applications as infrared detectors.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Thermal detector comprising a layer of heat-sensitive 
material, characterized in that the sensitive 

material comprises a doped ferroelectric material 
exhibiting a low resistance, a ferroelectric/paraelectric 

transition temperature close to ambient temperature and a 
pronounced rise in resistance with temperature, in the 

vicinity of the ferroelectric/paraelectric transition, 
during thermal detection. 
Thermal detector according to Claim 1, 
characterized in that the sensitive material is a doped 

ferroelectric ceramic. 
Thermal detector according to Claim 2, 
characterized in that the sensitive material comprises 

barium titanate. 
Thermal detector according to Claim 3, 
characterized in that the sensitive material comprises 

strontium titanate. 
Thermal detector according to Claim 1, 
characterized in that the sensitive material comprises a 

layer of doped ferroelectric material and a semiconductor 
layer. 
Thermal detector according to one of Claims 1 
to 5, characterized in that the doping is provided by a 

material of the antimony or yttrium type.  
 
Thermal detector according to one of Claims 1 
to 5, characterized in that the doping is provided by a 

rare earth. 
Thermal detector according to one of Claims 6 
or 7, characterized in that the percentage of dopant is 

between approximately 0.1% and approximately 1%. 
Thermal detector according to one of Claims 1 
to 8, characterized in that it includes a multiplexing 

circuit, a thermally insulating thin-film microstructure 
and a matrix segmentation at the level of the sensitive 

layer, so as to define picture elements. 
Thermal detector according to Claim 9, 
characterized in that the insulating microstructure 

consists of microbridges made of insulating material. 
Thermal detector according to Claim 10, 
characterized in that the insulating material is silicon 

nitride. 
Thermal detector according to one of Claims 
10 or 11, characterized in that each microbridge supports 

an individual element of sensitive material made of 
ferroelectric ceramic. 
Thermal detector according to Claim 12, 
characterized in that planar electrodes are arranged on 

the lower surface and on the upper surface of the 
individual elements. 
Thermal detector according to Claim 12, 
characterized in that electrodes are located on the side 

walls of the sensitive elements and also partially cover 
the upper face of the said elements 
</CLAIMS>
