Rucksack with a device for carrying a snowboard or the like

<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a rucksack with a device, in particular for carrying a snowboard, skis or the like. In the back region of the rucksack, a vertical tunnel (14) running approximately parallel to the rear side of the rucksack, is provided to receive the snowboard (2) and/or the skis. Arranged below the bottom region of the rucksack is a holding device for the lower end of the snowboard (2) and/or the skis.

<CLAIMS>
A rucksack having a device for the transport of a
plate-shaped item,
characterised in that
 beneath the bottom region of
the rucksack a retention device for the lower end of

the plate-shaped item (2) and in the rear region of
the rucksack a vertical tunnel (14) extending

roughly parallel to the rear side of the rucksack
for receiving the plate-shaped item (2) are

provided.
A rucksack according to Claim 1,
characterised in that
 the retention device has the
form of an insertion pocket (4), which is attached

by means of a connecting part (5) to the rucksack.
A rucksack according to Claim 2,
characterised in that
 the insertion pocket (4) is
formed by the connecting part having a flap-type

construction and being turned over towards the
bottom region of the rucksack,


and in that
 the lateral edges of the overlapping
regions of the connecting part (5) are fastened to

one another.
A rucksack according to Claim 2 or 3,
characterised in that
 at least one strap arrangement
(11, 12, 13) is provided to change the spacing

between the insertion pocket (4) and the bottom
region of the rucksack.
A rucksack according to Claim 4,
characterised in that
 the strap arrangement
comprises a first strap part (11), which is attached 

to the insertion pocket (4), and a second strap part
(12), which is attached to the rucksack, and a

closure part (13), in which the first and second
strap parts (11, 12) can be adjustably fastened to

one another in their longitudinal direction.
A rucksack according to Claim 4 to 5,
characterised in that
 at least two strap
arrangements (11, 12, 13) are disposed in the

direction of the width of the insertion pocket (4)
spaced fro
m one another.
A rucksack according to Claim 1,
characterised in that
 the retention device has the
form of a downwardly hanging flap-type part (5)

fixed to the rucksack, which at its free end
possesses at least one strap arrangement (11', 12',

13'), so that towards the bottom region of the
rucksack it can be turned over and fixed to form a

loop region for receiving the lower end of the
plate-shaped item (2).
A rucksack according to Clam 7,
characterised in that
 at least two strap
arrangements (11', 12', 13') are provided in the

direction of the width of the flap-type part (5).
A rucksack according to Claim 7 and 8,
characterised in that
 the strap arrangement
comprises a first strap part (11') disposed at the

end region of the flap-type part (5'), a second
strap part (12') fixed to the bottom region of the

rucksack and a closure part (13'), in which the
first and second strap parts (11', 12') can be

adjustably fixed to one another in their
longitudinal direction. 
A rucksack according to one of Claims 1 to 9,
characterised in that
 the retention device (4)
comprises reinforcements (15) in the region for

receiving the end of the plate-shaped item (2).
A rucksack according to one of Claims 1 to 10,
characterised in that

a stiffening plate (9), whose side faced towards the

A rucksack according to Claim 11 or 12,
characterised in that
 the padding is made from a
foamed material.
A rucksack according to one of Claims 11 to 13,

A rucksack according to Claim 14,

 the lengths of fabric (8, 10)

A rucksack according to one of Claims 1 to 15,
