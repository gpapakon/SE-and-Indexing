<INVENTION-TITLE>
Element forming a part of a working chamber from a motor having a coating.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C23C1416	C23C1416	F02B7704	F02B7704	F16J926	F16J926	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C23C	C23C	F02B	F02B	F16J	F16J	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C23C14	C23C14	F02B77	F02B77	F16J9	F16J9	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A piston-ring coating is composed of a relatively inert, anti-adhesive metal such as, for example, niobium or zirconium. It is applied by the target method in a vacuum and has a small thickness of the order of eight  mu m. Together with a special final honing operation in the axial direction and in interaction with an appropriate cylinder running surface, improved run-in characteristics, lower exhaust emissions, better leaktightness and better oil stripping are obtained.
