<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the preparation of nitride and/or carbide ceramic powder of metals on metalloids by flash-pyrolysis and the powder obtained by this process.
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
The flash pyrolysis process consists in sending rough drops of ceramic precursor into a heated chamber containing a gas at a temperature and for a period of time sufficient to obtain a powder. The diameter of the drops formed is greater than 10  mu m and the chamber is brought to a temperature greater than 500@C. The metal or metalloid carbide and/or nitride powder obtained in the amorphous or crystalline form contains spherical particles with a mean diameter of greater than 0.2  mu m, 90 % of which have a size less than 0.4  mu m. This powder is advantageously used in the automobile and aeronautical fields for the manufacture of ceramic articles having good thermomechanical properties.
