<INVENTION-TITLE>
Method and apparatus for determination of an at least approximately circular contact surface.
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
The task is to find a simple possibility for objectively determining the contact surface of a deformable medium on a plane surface with high accuracy and at low technical cost. This is achieved according to the invention when the plane surface is provided as a film (layer) waveguide (5) into which there is coupled an incoherent, parallel, homogeneous, one-dimensionally widely expanded light bundle (2), and the coupled-out light is fed to a photoreceiver row (7) for detecting one-dimensional intensity distributions. An evaluation device (8) generates a difference curve (83) from a normal curve (81) without a touching medium and a calibration curve (82) with a touching medium, and the exact diameter of the contact surface is determined from the distance of two associated extremes of the difference curve (83). The invention is preferably applied in ophthalmology for the purpose of measuring the applanation circle on the tonometer, and is used for analytical purposes in the measurement of liquid drops.
