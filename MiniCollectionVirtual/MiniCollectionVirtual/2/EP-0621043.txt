<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for manufacturing endoprotheses made of glass ionomer cement.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61F200	A61F200	A61F228	A61F228	A61F230	A61F230	A61L2700	A61L2700	A61L2746	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	A61F	A61L	A61L	A61L	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61F2	A61L27	A61L27	A61L27	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Endoprostheses can be prepared from a glass ionomer cement in an economic, fitting manner adapted to suit the requirements to be met in the field of medicine, such that a free-flowing cement composition is prepared, introduced under pressure into a moulding tool and allowed to cure therein. The moulding tool used is a thin-walled shell which is not dimensionally stable in itself and which is supported during injection of the cement composition by an outer, dimensionally stable moulding tool. The cured prosthesis can be left in the shell until it is implanted. In the latter case, the shell simultaneously serves as packaging.
</ABSTRACT>
