<INVENTION-TITLE>
Closing means for flasks or the like with compensation for looseness
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to an assembly for stoppering (closing) a flask or the like, of the type comprising a cap screwed on to the neck of the flask or the like. The assembly according to the invention comprises a tapped internal ring (1) for screwing on to the neck (16), an outer lower ring (7) rotationally integral with the said inner ring (1) but free in terms of translation, the said inner ring (1) being equipped with elastic tabs (5) bearing on the said outer lower ring (7), and a cap (8) which can move in terms of translation with respect to the said inner ring (1). Application to stoppering.

<CLAIMS>
Stopper assembly of a bottle or the like of
the type comprising a ca
p screwed on the neck of the bottle
or the like comprising a screw-threaded internal ring (1)

for screwing on the neck (16) and a cover (8) movable in
translation relative to said internal ring (1),

characterized in that it further comprises an external
lower ring (7) fixed in rotation with said internal ring

(1) but free in translation, said internal ring (1) being
provided with resilient tongues (5) bearing on said

external lower ring (7) and comprising a recess (13)
coacting with a tongue (12) of the external lower ring (7).
Stopper assembly according to claim 1,

characterized in that the cover (8) is fixed in rotation
with said external lower ring (7), for example by a

snapping (9, 10) and coaction between grooves (11) and
tongues (12).
Stopper assembly according to claim 2,

characterized in that the internal ring (1) is closed at
its upper portion (3), the internal surface of said upper

portion (3) comprising preferably a self-sealing fluid-tight
plug (4).
Stopper assembly according to claim 1,

characterized in that the cover (8) is mounted slidably and
pivotably on the internal ring (1) and coacts by its base,

directly or indirectly, with the upper edge of the external
lower ring (7).
Stopper assembly according to claim 4,

characterized in that a keeper (28) is mounted oscillably
on the base of the cover (8) with an internal lip (27) of

triangular cross section disposed in an external throat
(26) of the cover (8).
