<INVENTION-TITLE>
Surgical forceps for applying suture clips.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61B1712	A61B17128	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A61B	A61B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A61B17	A61B17	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
These forceps have a magazine (7) in which the U-shaped suture clips are stored and guided, these clips being stored and guided in a linear magazine (7) and pushed by a spring towards the open end (72) of the magazine; the forceps are equipped with a slide (2) which can be moved axially in translation above the magazine; this slide is fitted with control means allowing a pair of jaws (30) located at the exit of the magazine to be closed; the slide (2) is also solidly attached to a retention tongue (81) which can be retracted instantaneously upwards when the slide advances, in order to allow the first clip (6b) in the row to be transferred between the jaws (30). Surgical equipment for suturing blood vessels.
