Connecting terminal for at least one electrical installation

<ABSTRACT>
An electrical connecting element for at least one electrical equipment has insulation-piercing terminal contact points (5) and additional plug contact points (31) for the connection of conductive parts.

<CLAIMS>
Connecting terminal for at least one electrical 
installation, 


with a casing (1) made of electrically insulating 
material and having fastening means (74), 
with contact elements (16, 21, 22) having spatially 
separate terminals (16, 31, 32, 38) electrically 

connected to one another for electrically conductive 
parts, of which at least one terminal (16) is 

constructed as an IDC contact terminal, and at least one 
further terminal (31, 32, 38) is constructed as a plug 

contact, both terminals (16, 31, 32, 38) being arranged 
in the operating mode in the casing (1) to be at least 

all-insulated by surrounding casing parts, and 
with at least one receiving or connecting area (52, 71, 
23) arranged on the casing (1) for an installation (53, 

73, 530), in the region of which at least one contact 
(31, 35, 26) lies. 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 1, characterised 
in that at least one plug contact (31) is fitted for 

plugging in a soldering lug or a flat plug. 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 1 or 2, 
characterised in that it has two adjacent plug contacts 

(31), which are fitted for plugging in a soldering lug 
or a flat plug, and each of which is electrically 

connected to its own IDC contact terminal (16). 
Connecting terminal according to one of the preceding 
claims, characterised in that it has at least one plug 

contact (31, 32, 38), its direction of insertion being 
arranged at right angles to the direction of insertion  

 
of the IDC contact terminal (16). 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 1, characterised 
in that the receiving or connecting area has mechanical 

stop or holding means (52, 3) for the electrical 
installation. 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 1, characterised 
in that the receiving or holding area is covered by 

separable closing means (80, 3) for insulation to the 
outside. 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 6, characterised 
in that the closing means (80, 3) are constructed to 

lock onto the casing (1). 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 6 or 7, 
characterised in that electrical connecting elements 

(87) are allocated to the closing means (80) to bridge 

at least two contacts located in the receiving or 
connecting area. 
Connecting terminal according to one of the preceding 
claims, characterised in that lead-in ducts (49, 50; 56) 

for the conductive parts to be connected are allocated 
to the plug contacts in the casing (1), said lead-in 

ducts being capable of receiving these parts possibly 
including an attached insulation (20) over a part of 

their length. 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 9, characterised 
in that at least one lead-in duct (50) is constructed to 

widen outwards in a funnel shape.  
 
Connecting terminal according to one of the preceding 
claims, characterised in that at least one plug contact 

(at 47) has a direction of insertion oriented parallel 
to the direction of insertion of the IDC contact 

terminal (16). 
Connecting terminal according to one of the preceding 
claims, characterised in that it has at least two plug 

contacts (31; 32) accessible from opposite sides of the 
casing (1). 
Connecting terminal according to one of the preceding 
claims, characterised in that the contact elements have 

at least one contact spring (21) made of material with 
good electrical conduction, in which the IDC contact 

terminal (16) and the plug contact or contacts (31, 32, 
47) are disposed. 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 13, characterised 
in that the IDC contact terminal (16) is formed by two 

parallel contact spring parts (27) not connected over 
their length, in which two flush IDC slots (14) are 

arranged; and that the two contact spring parts (27) are 
mounted laterally in the casing (1). 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 13 or 14, 
characterised in that the contact spring (21) has a base 

part (24) and two opposing leg parts (25) projecting 
therefrom, and in at least one part (24, 25) thereof a 

plug contact gap (31, 32, 47) is arranged, which is 
covered at least partially by a locking part (30, 22), 

which may be locked onto the part (54, 19, 59) to be 
plugged in.  

 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 16, characterised 
in that a clamping spring (22) with locking parts (30, 

47) cooperating with the respective contact gap (31, 32, 
37) is inserted into the contact spring (21). 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 16, characterised 
in that in the region of at least one contact gap (37), 

the clamping spring (22) has a plug opening, which is 
defined by notched locking tabs (47) which may be spread 

apart on one side. 
Connecting terminal according to Claims 14 to 16, 
characterised in that contact spring (21) is constructed 

in the region containing the base and leg parts (24; 25) 
so as to be substantially enclosed all around by upper 

wall parts (26); and that the parts (27) bearing the IDC 
slot (14) are notched on the upper wall parts (26). 
Connecting terminal according to one of Claims 13 to 14, 
characterised in that on a substantially flat portion 

(24) of the contact spring (21) a region (38) with a 
substantially L- or U-shaped cross-section is arranged, 

said region forming a plug-type connection and being 
pressed outwards from the plane of this portion, and 

having a locking part (41) projecting into it. 
Connecting terminal according to one of the preceding 
claims, characterised in that several contact springs 

(21) are arranged in the casing (1) in their own 
chambers (67), which are separated from one another by 

insulating intermediate walls (68) and are possibly 
closed by a joint cover (3). 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 20, characterised  
 

in that the cover is arranged on the opposite side of 
the casing to the IDC contact terminals (16) and has 

supporting means (75) for parts (27) of the contact 
elements forming the IDC contacts. 
Connecting terminals according to Claim 20 or 21, 
characterised in that the cover (3) and/or the 

intermediate walls (68) have intermeshing parts (77, 78; 
76) for preventing leakage paths. 
Connecting terminal according to one of Claims 20 to 22, 
characterised in that the casing (1) and/or the cover 

(3) have at least one lead-through duct (96) for a 
looped through conductor. 
Connecting terminal according to one of Claims 5 to 23, 
characterised in that it is fitted for use with a 

capacitor (53, 530) as electrical installation; and that 
the casing (1) has positive holding means (52, 3) for 

the capacitor in the receiving or connecting area. 
Connecting terminal according to one of the preceding 
claims, characterised in that it has contact terminals 

(35) fitted for connection of a bleeder resistor (73). 
Connecting terminal according to one of Claims 13 to 25, 
characterised in that at least one electrical 

installation (530) is arranged in a region at least 
partially enclosed by at least one contact spring (21). 
Connecting terminal according to Claim 26, characterised 
in that the IDC contact terminal (16) of the contact 

spring (21) electrically connected to the electrical 
installation (530) is mechanically supported by the 

electrical installation. 
</CLAIMS>
