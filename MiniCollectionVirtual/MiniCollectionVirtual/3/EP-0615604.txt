<ABSTRACT>
The invention concerns a device for feeding fresh air into and extracting exhaust air from an enclosed space, the device having a frame (1) with at least one housing (3) located behind it. In addition to vents (8) for the exhaust air, the device is fitted with vanes (5) positioned in the fresh-air flow.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Device for feeding fresh air into and 
extracting exhaust air from a room, having a concealed 

frame (1), behind which at least one housing (3) is 
situated, wherein inclinedly orientated vanes (5) for 

the fresh air are disposed in the concealed frame (1) 
next to vents (8) for the exhaust air, characterised 

in that the vanes (5) are formed from punched-out 
sheets, and receiver grooves (7) are bevelled from the 

vanes (5), through which grooves bars (6) are 
inserted, which are mounted in walls of the housing 

(3) in the position of use. 
Device according to claim 1, characterised in 
that a vent opening (10) does not protrude, or only 

protrudes slightly, beyond a plane (11) of the 
concealed frame. 
Device according to claim 1 or 2, 
characterised in that bird-excluding grilles and/or 

filter mats (12) are provided behind the vanes (5) 
and/or the vents (8) in the housings (3, 4). 
</CLAIMS>
