<ABSTRACT>
The subject of the invention is an implantable drainage valve for the treatment of hydrocephalus, comprising a body (1) forming a valve seat (3), a closure element (4) and a spring (5), one end of which applies the closure element onto the seat. According to the invention, it comprises a moving weight (6, 6', 6'') on which the end of the spring lying opposite the closure element bears.

<CLAIMS>
An implantable drainage valve for the treatment of 
Hydrocephalus, comprising a body (1) forming a valve seat (3), a closure 

member (4), and a spring (5) of which one end urges the closure 
member against the seat, characterised by the fact that it includes a 

regulator means (6, 6', 6'') on which bears the end of the spring remote 
from the closure member. 
A valve according to Claim 1, in which the body is cylindrical, 
the regulator means comprising at least one ball housed within the body. 
A valve according to either of Claims 1 and 2, in which the 
spring is a helicoidal spring.
