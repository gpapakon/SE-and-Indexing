<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for obtaining lipid fractions from powdery egg-products.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A23J700	A23J700	A23L132	A23L132	C11B100	C11B110	C11B1100	C11B1100	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>A23J	A23J	A23L	A23L	C11B	C11B	C11B	C11B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>A23J7	A23J7	A23L1	A23L1	C11B1	C11B1	C11B11	C11B11	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
A process for obtaining lipid fractions from powdered egg products with a content of phospholipids entails the egg product being extracted   a) with liquid propane under a pressure 
<
/= 200 bar and at a temperature 
<
/= 70@C and b) with an entrainer consisting of an aliphatic alcohol with 1 to 4 C atoms.   It is possible in this way to prepare lipid fractions with good organoleptic properties and with a phospholipid content of at least 20% by weight.
</ABSTRACT>
