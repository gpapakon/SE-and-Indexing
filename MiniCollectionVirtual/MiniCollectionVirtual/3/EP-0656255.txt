<INVENTION-TITLE>
Plated coins and process for their manufacture.
</INVENTION-TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a process for manufacturing coins which essentially consist of chromium-containing stainless steel, the coining operation being fed coin blanks in which the stainless steel, which has a 0.2% creep limit (tensile yield strength) of less than 200 N/mm
<
2
>
, is core layer and is provided on both sides with a coating of non-ferrous metal, which preferably consists for a proportion of 60 to 100% of copper and the 0.2% creep limit of which lies at least 40 N/mm
<
2
>
 below that of the stainless steel.
</ABSTRACT>
