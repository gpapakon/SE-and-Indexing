<INVENTION-TITLE>
Acupuncture point stimulating device.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>A61H3900	A61H3900	A61N118	A61N118	</CLASSIFICATIONS>

<ABSTRACT>
The comparatively weak currents generated by bimetallic elements in conjunction with sweat as the electrolyte, as well as the magnetic force lines emanating from the magnet have to act for a comparatively long time in order to stimulate or sedate acupuncture points and lines. The invention describes a device which permits long-term action without impairing or disabling the patient. The bimetallic elements (5.6; 8.9) and/or magnets may be fitted on the sidepieces of glasses, a headband, a wristwatch band (1) or an arm sweat band.
