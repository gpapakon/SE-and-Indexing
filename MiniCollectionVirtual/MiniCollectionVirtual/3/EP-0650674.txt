<CLAIMS>
An imprint set for taking an imprint of the contact trace of one
or more limbs of the human body, for example a foot, comprising a giving

sheet (3) having a colour-delivering coating, a receiving sheet (1b)
which is provided with an adhesive layer (6) on its side towards the

colour-delivering coating on the giving sheet (3), a separating sheet (5)
arranged between said two sheets, and a transparent foil (1a) covering

the outside of the giving sheet, characterised in that the giving sheet
(3) is of a two-part nature comprising a cover sheet (3) to whose inner

edge (3b) a disposable carbon paper (4) is fixed in opposite relationship
to the adhesive layer (6) of the receiving sheet (1, 1b), and that the

transparent foil (1a) is supplemented to form a pocket (1) which
accommodates the entire imprint set and whose inner pocket side forms the

receiving sheet (1b) and is correspondingly covered with the adhesive layer
(6) and which has a removal opening (1c) at the side opposite the inner

edge (3b) of the cover sheet (3).
Apparatus according to claim 1 characterised in that the cover
sheet (3) with the carbon paper (4) glued to its inner edge is replaced

by a single folded sheet (3c, 4c) of which a part (4c) is provided with a
carbon coating.
Apparatus according to claim 1 characterised in that the
separating sheet is in the form of a simple double-protection foil (12)

of which one half touches the adhesive layer while the other half has a
projecting tongue portion (12a) to permit it to be pulled out.
Apparatus according to claims 1 to 3 characterised in that it is
in the form of a double pocket in which the two individual pockets are

secured together by a strip (9).
A process for producing an imprint of the contact trace of a
limb of the human body with an apparatus according to claim 1, claim 2 or

claim 3 and claim 4 wherein firstly the foil or double-protection foil 
acting as the separating layer between the adhesive layer of the pocket

and the carbon paper is pulled out, that then the imprint is produced by
the limb pressing against or standing on the cover sheet side of the

pocket and that finally the cover sheet and therewith the disposable
carbon paper joined to the cover sheet is unrolled from the adhesive

layer of the pocket and pulled out of the pocket.
A process according to claim 5 wherein the imprint set is folded
one or more times prior to making the imprint.
