<INVENTION-TITLE>
Hard hydrophobic polyurethane.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C08G1800	C08G1830	C08G1836	C08G1840	C08G1842	C08G1862	C08G1865	C08G1869	H01B342	H01B342	</CLASSIFICATIONS>

<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C08G18	C08G18	C08G18	C08G18	C08G18	C08G18	C08G18	C08G18	H01B3	H01B3	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Hard (rigid) polyurethanes are obtainable by reacting     1. polyisocyanates with   2. a polyol mixture comprising   a) - OH-functional polyesters having a molecular weight of 260 - 2000 based on C9-C22-fatty acids, b) - OH-functional, oligomeric polyolefins having a molecular weight of 700 - 6000, and optionally c) - at least difunctional polyols having a molecular weight of 62 - 8000, preferably 62 - 400, which are different from components 2a) and 2b),  optionally in the presence of   3. water as blowing agent and 4. auxiliaries and additives which are known per se in PUR chemistry.
</ABSTRACT>
