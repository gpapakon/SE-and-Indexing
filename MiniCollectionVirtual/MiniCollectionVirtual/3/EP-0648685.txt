<INVENTION-TITLE>
Tray provided with recesses for holding containers
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>B65D7100	B65D7170	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>B65D	B65D	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>B65D71	B65D71	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a tray, designed with recesses, for receiving a plurality of containers, tubes and the like, whose screw-on lids fit into the recesses. The tray contains an additional part. The additional part is an element which, on the one hand, fits into the recesses in the tray and, on the other hand, is designed on the inside such that the containers with their screw-on lids can be received there. If an additional part comprises two recesses which are joined together via webs, two containers can correspondingly be received in the recesses of the additional part, said additional part in turn being received as a unit in two adjacent recesses in a tray.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
Recess-containing tray for receiving a number of 
containers, tubes and the like, the screw tops of which 

are received in the recesses, characterized by a number 
of additional, in each case single-piece, insert parts 

(24) having two, three or more recesses, the outsides of 
which are coordinated with the recesses (14) of the tray 

(12) and the insides of which are coordinated with the 
screw tops of the tubes. 
Tray according to Claim 1, characterized in that 
the insert parts (24) are designed as thin-walled 

thermoformed plastic parts. 
Tray according to Claim 1 or 2, characterized in 
that the insert parts (24) are of cup-like design and 

mutually adjacent recesses are connected to one another 
via crosspieces (25). 
</CLAIMS>
