PROCESS FOR STABILISING COPOLYMERS OF MALEIC ANHYDRIDE AND VINYL ETHERS

<ABSTRACT>
A process is disclosed for stabilising copolymers of maleic acid or maleic anhydride and vinyl alkyl ethers so that they do not lose their molecular weight in an aqueous solution or in a solution of organic solvent. For that purpose, 0.001 to 10 % by weight, in relation to the copolymers, of a reducing agent, a tertiary butyl group-containing phenol or their mixtures are added to the copolymers.
</ABSTRACT>

<CLAIMS>
A process for stabilizing a copolymer of maleic 
acid or maleic anhydride and vinyl alkyl ethers against 

a reduction in molecular weight, which comprises adding 
from 0.001 to 10 % by weight, based on the copolymer, of 

a reducing agent, selected from the group consisting of 
sulfurous acid, phosphinic acid, dithionic acid, ascorbic 

acid and ammonium or alkali metal salts of said acids, to 
a solution of the copolymer in water or an organic 

solvent. 
A process as calimed in claim 1, wherein one of 
said reducing agents is added to a solution of the 

copolymer in water. 
</CLAIMS>
