<INVENTION-TITLE>
Process for the preparation of substantially salt-free aqueous Bis-aminooxy-alkane solutions.
</INVENTION-TITLE>
<CLASSIFICATIONS>C07C23900	C07C23920	C08K500	C08K532	C25B300	C25B300	</CLASSIFICATIONS>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>C07C	C07C	C08K	C08K	C25B	C25B	</CLASSIFICATIONS-THIRD>
<CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>C07C239	C07C239	C08K5	C08K5	C25B3	C25B3	</CLASSIFICATIONS-FOURTH>
<ABSTRACT>
Process for the preparation of substantially salt-free aqueous bis-aminooxy-alkane (I) solutions, by   a) converting a dihaloalkane (II) with a ketoxime (III) in the aqueous solution of a strong mineral base to give the alkylene bis-oxime ether (IV), b) clearing the alkylene bis-oxime ether (IV) with a strong mineral acid and water to give the ketone and the mineral acid salt of the bis-aminooxy-alkane (I) and separating off the ketone in a manner known per se, c) treating the remaining aqueous solution with an alkali metal hydroxide to liberate the bis-aminooxy-alkane (I), and d) removing the ionic components from the resulting aqueous solution by electrodialysis.
</ABSTRACT>
